The Senate Judiciary Committee confirmation hearing of Judge Amy Coney Barrett began Monday with a sharp emphasis by Democrats on what her appointment could mean for the future of the Affordable Care Act. The day ended without seeing the rancor that has dominated other Supreme Court confirmation hearings under President Trump. That civility could end Tuesday at 9 a.m. Eastern, when senators begin their questioning of Barrett.
