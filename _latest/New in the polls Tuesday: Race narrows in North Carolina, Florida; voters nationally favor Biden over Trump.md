A key base of his &#8212; senior voters &#8212; is showing up in smaller numbers than in 2016 exit polls. Biden, meanwhile, is struggling Hispanic voters, a key Democratic voting bloc in the previous election.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><strong class="gnt_ar_b_al">2020 election:</strong><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/elections/2020/10/20/election-2020-biden-leads-trump-narrow-florida-race-poll-finds/5988558002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Joe Biden has narrow lead over President Donald Trump in new Florida poll</a></p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">Biden up by one in North Carolina: WaPo and ABC News</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">A&#160;poll conducted by&#160;<a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2020/10/20/poll-north-carolina-presidential-race/?arc404=true" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Washington Post/ABC News</a> also effectively puts Trump and Biden in range of one another, with Biden only up by one percent.</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-news-politics-3" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">A small majority of registered voters (51%) in the state disapprove of Trump's presidency, but that number remains slightly lower than national ratings.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><strong class="gnt_ar_b_al">Polls:</strong><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/elections/2020/10/20/election-2020-trump-biden-nearly-tied-north-carolina/5989978002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">North Carolina voters nearly split on Trump and Biden; Cunningham has slight edge on Tillis</a></p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">Biden up in Pennsylvania, Michigan, Wisconsin; Trump up in Texas: CNN</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><a href="https://www.cnn.com/2020/10/20/politics/cnn-poll-of-polls-pennsylvania-michigan-wisconsin/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">CNN polling </a>for 10 swing states has Biden up by a statistically significant amount in Pennsylvania (52&#160;to 43&#160;percent), Michigan and Wisconsin (both 51 to 43 percent) &#8212;
