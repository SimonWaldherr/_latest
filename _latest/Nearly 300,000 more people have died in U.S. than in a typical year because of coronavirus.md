<div><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">Ten residents of a Kansas nursing home have died in a coronavirus outbreak that infected every one of its residents, health officials said.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">The Andbe Home, a privately owned facility located near the Nebraska border, confirmed all 62 residents have contracted the virus, <a href="https://www.facebook.com/NortonCountyHealthDeptandHomeHealth/photos/a.1207179892627142/3751157051562734/" target="_blank">the Norton County Health Department said in a news release</a>. In addition to the 10 who died, one is hospitalized and the other 51 are being cared for at the nursing home. An unspecified number of staff members have also tested positive, the health department said.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">The health department is working with emergency management authorities to help manage the outbreak.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">&#8220;Steps are being taken to prevent any further outbreak including quarantining residents in their rooms and not allowing outside visitors into the facility,&#8221; the news release said.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">The coronavirus has had a particularly devastating impact on nursing homes, <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2020/06/04/nursing-homes-coronavirus-deaths/?itid=lk_inline_manual_101" target="_blank">killing more than 30,000 of their residents nationwide</a>, according to federal data. The data showed that many facilities lacked the staff and protective equipment as the pandemic raged. A Senate report released last month <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2020/09/22/senate-democrats-covid-nursing-home/?itid=lk_inline_manual_101" target="_blank">found equipment shortages grew more acute</a> between July and August.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">Across Kansas, health officials have reported nearly 71,000 coronavirus cases and 870 deaths, according to Washington Post tracking.</p></div>
