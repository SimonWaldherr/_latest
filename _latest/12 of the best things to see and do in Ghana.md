<div><p class="Paragraph__component">(CNN) &#8212; <p>Slotted into the underbelly of Africa's northwestern huddle, Ghana is a country that's steadily reinventing itself.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Known for its colorful culture, wildlife and natural beauty, the West African nation had one of Sub-Saharan Africa's fastest-growing economies in the past few years, thanks to cocoa and a relatively recent discovery of oil.</p></p><p id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="Ad__tag Article__adBody Ad__hasLabel"></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>The economic upturn helped to inject a new lease of life into the arts and fashion scene, leading to a boost in restaurant openings. </p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>While Ghana has no doubt been impacted by the pandemic, the country remains as eclectic and alluring as ever.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>The country has long been a mecca for volunteers, and there are many hip and affordable accommodation options available, as well as more high-end hideaways.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>A handful of international hotel brands have opened up here over the past few years, but traveling here still feels authentic. </p></p><p id="ad_nat_btf_01" class="Ad__tag"></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>While it's likely be a while before many of us are able to do so due to current travel restrictions -- its borders by land and sea remain closed to passenger travel, there's no harm in planning ahead.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>From surfing at deserted beaches, to hanging out with wild elephants, here are some of the top things to do while visiting Ghana .</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p><h3>Visit Cape Coast Castle </h3></p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Now a UNESCO World Heritage site, Cape Coast Castle was one of around 30 "slave forts" built by Dutch, Portuguese and British traders along the country's coastline.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>The huge white stone building offers knowledgeable guides who take visitors down to the bleak dungeons where slaves were once shackled, and the Door of no Return, the point they would have been crammed into ships heading for America. </p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Previous visitors include the Obamas, who came here during their first visit to Africa after the former US President was sworn into office. Michelle Obama's roots have been traced back to slaves taken from this castle.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>There's also a fantastic vegetarian restaurant named Baobab caf&#233; that serves delicious, healthy smoothies and juices. </p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Visitors can also head inland to Kakum National Park and swing across the rainforest on its 350-meter-long canopy walkway.</p></p><span><a></a><span><a href="https://www.facebook.com/baobabhouse/" target="_blank">Baobab Caf&#233;</a> , Cape Coast; + 233 54 043 6130</span></span><p class="Paragraph__component"><p><h3>Try local, naturally grown foods</h3></p></p><img class="Image__image" src="https://dynaimage.cdn.cnn.com/cnn/e_blur:500,q_auto:low,w_50,c_fill,g_auto,h_28,ar_16:9/http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.cnn.com%2Fcnnnext%2Fdam%2Fassets%2F201012103817-09b-ghana-things-to-do-restricted.jpg" alt="Be sure to try traditional Ghanian dishes like Banku, made by mixing fermented corn and cassava dough in hot water."><p class="CaptionedImage__caption">Be sure to try traditional Ghanian dishes like Banku, made by mixing fermented corn and cassava dough in hot water.</p><p class="CaptionedImage__credit">Jordi Perdigo/Anadolu Agency/Getty Images</p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Baobab trees are scattered all over the country, and they happen to produce one of the world's most nutrient-dense and antioxidant-rich fruits.</p></p><p id="ad_rect_btf_01" class="Ad__tag Article__adBody Ad__hasLabel"></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>This can be made into a powder, which is wonderful in smoothies, and is said to boost the immune system as well as fight fatigue. </p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Ghana is also filled with Moringa trees, a powerhouse of proteins and vitamins that aids metabolism. A tea or powder can be made from its leaves. </p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>As for top local dishes, kenkey, made from corn, is sold in most local "chop bars" and is definitely worth a try.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Banku, cooked by mixing fermented corn, cassava dough and hot water into a smooth paste, is also very popular.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>However, red-red, a stew of black eyed peas made with red palm oil and served with fried plantain, is particularly delicious.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p><h3>Check out the coastline</h3></p></p><img class="Image__image" src="https://dynaimage.cdn.cnn.com/cnn/e_blur:500,q_auto:low,w_50,c_fill,g_auto,h_28,ar_16:9/http%3A%2F%2Fcdn.cnn.com%2Fcnnnext%2Fdam%2Fassets%2F201012104012-02b-ghana-things-to-do-restricted.jpg" alt='"Environmentally aware" resort Ko-Sa Beach, located near fishing village Ampenyi, offers beachfront properties.'><p class="CaptionedImage__caption">"Environmentally aware" resort Ko-Sa Beach, located near fishing village Ampenyi, offers beachfront properties.</p><p class="CaptionedImage__credit">Courtesy Ko-Sa Beach Resort</p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Ghana has a chunk of tropical coastline, over 335 miles (560 kilometers) of it, in fact.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>There are plenty of fantastic spots to choose from close to Accra, but the beaches are far more pristine slightly further afield.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Ko-Sa Beach Resort, a two-hour drive east from Accra, is run by a Dutch couple and suits all budgets, with beachfront, traditionally constructed buildings with thatched roofs as well as more affordable options.</p></p><p class="Paragraph__component"><p>Children from the neighboring fishing villages come here to sell peanuts and bananas. But their approach is gentle, only adding to the chilled out vibe.</p></p><span><a></a><span><a href="https://www.ko-sa.com/" target="_blank">Ko-Sa Beach Resort</a> , Cape Coast; + 233 244 375
