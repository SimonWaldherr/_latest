Davis has died at the age of 81."><p class="gnt_em_img_ccw gnt_em_img_ccw__cap gnt_em_img_ccw__crd"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">British rocker Spencer Davis, whose band&#160;The Spencer Davis Group was known for 1960s hits&#160;&#8220;Gimme Some Lovin&#8217;&#8221; and &#8220;I&#8217;m a Man,&#8221;&#160;died Monday at age 81.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Davis&#8217; agent, Bob Birk, said Tuesday that he died in a hospital while being treated for pneumonia. He didn't give a location, but British media reported that Davis lived in California.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">USA TODAY has reached out to representatives for Davis seeking additional information.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"He was a very good friend," Birk told the BBC. "He was a highly ethical, very talented, good-hearted, extremely intelligent, generous man. He will be missed."</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Davis was born in Swansea, Wales in 1939. He began working as a musician while he was a student at the University of Birmingham.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Influenced by the burgeoning British blues and skiffle scenes, he performed in bands with future stars including the Rolling Stones&#8217; Bill Wyman and Christine Perfect &#8212; later Fleetwood Mac&#8217;s Christine McVie. He formed the Spencer Davis Group in 1963, with a teenage <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/life/music/2017/08/24/listen-steve-winwoods-new-live-version-higher-love/599710001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Steve Winwood</a> on keyboards and guitar, his brother Muff Winwood on bass and Pete York on drums.</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-entertainment-music-2" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">With Steve Winwood as lead vocalist, the band had two No. 1 U.K. singles &#8212; &#8220;Keep on Running&#8221; in 1965 and &#8220;Somebody Help Me&#8221; in 1966 &#8212; and seven British top 40 hits before Steve Winwood&#8217;s departure in 1967.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Davis released several solo albums without recapturing his 1960s fame, and later reformed the Spencer Davis Group without the Winwood brothers. In later years he was regarded as an influential elder statesman of British rock.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Davis is survived by his partner June and three adult children.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Follow Gary Dinges on Twitter @gdinges</p><a class="gnt_em gnt_em__lp gnt_em_gl gnt_em__el" href="/picture-gallery/entertainment/2020/01/10/2020-stars-celebrities-we-have-lost-entertainment-industry/2798762001/"><img class="gnt_em_gl_i" alt="" width="660" height="372"><p class="gnt_em_gl_i_nt"></p><p class="gnt_em_gl_vg"></p><p class="gnt_em_t gnt_em_t__hb"></p></a></div>
