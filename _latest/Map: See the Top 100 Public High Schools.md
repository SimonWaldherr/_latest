<div><p>The top 100 public high schools ranked by U.S. News have a few things in common: They were the best in demonstrated academic outcomes, and they reported high graduation rates. </p><p>But these high schools aren't clustered in just a handful of states. Twenty-nine states are home to at least one high school breaking the top 100 in the U.S. News <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/national-rankings">Best High Schools</a> national rankings, with schools sprinkled across all regions of the country. The rankings evaluate schools on six factors, including college readiness of students, based on data from the 2017-2018 school year.</p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p>The top-ranked public high school of 2020, <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/virginia/districts/fairfax-county-public-schools/thomas-jefferson-high-school-for-science-and-technology-20461">Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology</a>, is located in <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/virginia">Virginia</a>. TJHSST is a magnet school that enrolled more than 1,700 students in 2017-2018. </p><p>Arizona, California, New York and Texas tied for the highest number of high schools in the top 100. Two of the 11 best high schools in <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/arizona">Arizona</a> that make the top 100 are located in Phoenix. In <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/california">California</a>, the 11 best high schools are spread across the state in cities from San Francisco to La Jolla. The 11 best high schools on this list in <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/new-york">New York</a> are all located in various boroughs of New York City. And 11 of the top 100 are located in <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/texas">Texas</a>, with three in Dallas, three in Houston, one in Austin and the rest in various cities across the state. </p><p>The majority of the public high schools in the top 100 are either charter or magnet schools. Schools the U.S. Department of Education did not categorize as charter or magnet make up 46 of the 100, while 21 are <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/national-rankings/charter-school-rankings">charter</a> schools and 33 are <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/national-rankings/magnet-school-rankings">magnet</a> schools. </p><p>The sizes of the top 100 public high schools range from just 130 students in grades 9-12 at the <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/florida/districts/miami-dade-county-public-schools/young-women-s-preparatory-academy-4913">Young Women's Preparatory Academy</a> in <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/florida/rankings">Florida</a> for the 2017-2018 school year to 5,839 students at the <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/new-york/districts/new-york-city-public-schools/brooklyn-technical-high-school-13269">Brooklyn Technical High School</a> in New York in the same year.</p><p id="ad" class="ad"></p><p>The map below shows the locations of the top 100 Best High Schools nationwide and can be filtered by school type &#8211; charter, magnet or traditional. Click on the dots to learn more about each school.</p><p><i>See the <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/rankings-overview">complete rankings</a> of the Best High Schools.</i></p></div>
