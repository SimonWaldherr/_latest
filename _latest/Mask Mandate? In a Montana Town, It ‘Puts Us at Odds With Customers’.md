<div><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">HAMILTON, Mont. &#8212; Outside River Rising Bakery sits an older gentleman, his face uncovered. He&#8217;s here every morning, greeting customers as he drinks his coffee and reads. Inside, people mill about, waiting to order. A group of moms chat at a corner table.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">The employees wear masks, but patrons are not required to. Most don&#8217;t. It feels almost normal. As if the pandemic had never happened.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">Half a block away in Hamilton, at Big Creek Coffee Roasters, most customers don&#8217;t go inside; instead they wait to order at a makeshift to-go window. There are a lot of strollers and Lululemon tights, and most people in the line are wearing a mask. If anyone did go inside, wearing one would be mandatory.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">One Montana block, two small businesses &#8212; and two different decisions about asking customers to wear masks.</p></div>
