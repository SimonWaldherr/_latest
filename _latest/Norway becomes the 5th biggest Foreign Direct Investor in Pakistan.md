<div><p>Ambassador of Norway to Pakistan, Kjell-Gunnar Eriksen via his Twitter said that Norway has become the 5th biggest Foreign Direct Investor (FDI) in Pakistan in the July-September 2020 and also becomes third biggest European investor.</p>

<p>As per the statistics, investment made by multinational companies reached a six month high at US$189 million in September 2020.</p>

<p>Norway was the largest investor with net FDI of US$248.2 million in the same quarter of last year. It was followed by Malta with net investment of US$55.6 million and China with US$55.4 million.</p>

<p>The Chinese firms are the top investors in Pakistan and invested a net US$97 million in September 2020, followed by Hong Kong based investors who poured a net US$22.8 million, according to the State Bank of Pakistan.</p>

<p>According to Norway Embassy in Pakistan, the bilateral, diplomatic relations between Norway and Pakistan are good. Norway recognized Pakistan as a sovereign state in 1947, shortly after its foundation.</p>

<p>Norway and Pakistan have good and close relations. According to the World Bank, Norway&#8217;s nominal gross domestic product in 2015 was US$388 billion, compared to US$ 270 billion for Pakistan. The Norwegian GDP per capita in 2015 was US$ 74,482 and Pakistan&#8217;s US$1,435.</p>
