<p><strong>The big story: DOJ files antitrust suit against Google</strong></p>
<p>The suit <a href="https://techcrunch.com/2020/10/20/justice-department-will-reportedly-file-its-antitrust-lawsuit-against-google-today/">accuses Google</a> of &#8220;unlawfully maintaining monopolies in the markets for general search services, search advertising, and general search text advertising in the United States.&#8221; It&#8217;s co-signed by 11 states, all with Republican attorneys general &#8212; Texas, Arkansas, Florida, Georgia, Indiana, Kentucky, Louisiana, Michigan, Missouri, Montana and South Carolina.</p><p class="piano-inline-promo"></p>
<p>Google called the U.S. Department of Justice&#8217;s case <a href="https://techcrunch.com/2020/10/20/google-calls-dojs-antitrust-lawsuit-deeply-flawed-in-gif-laden-blog-response/">&#8220;deeply flawed&#8221;</a> and offered a platform-by-platform argument that it doesn&#8217;t actually have unfair market dominance. For example, it attributed its popularity in search to a superior product, rather than anti-competitive practices.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Wall Street investors <a href="https://techcrunch.com/2020/10/20/investors-appear-to-shrug-at-antitrust-lawsuit-aimed-at-google/">don&#8217;t seem to be particularly alarmed by the suit</a>.</p>
