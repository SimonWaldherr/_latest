 <p>     </p> <p>Mr Hancock said there is &#8220;growing evidence of the debilitating consequences of long Covid&#8221; and warned of increasing rates of the virus among older people in the UK.</p> <p>The Health Secretary told the Commons: &#8220;Cases continue to rise among the over-60s, who are the most likely to end up in hospital or worse.</p> <p>&#8220;I&#8217;m very worried that the cases per 100,000 among the over-60s is 401 in the Liverpool City Region, 241 in Lancashire and in Greater Manchester has risen over the past week from 171 to 283.&#8221;</p> <p>Mr Hancock said the Government is &#8220;working hard&#8221; to secure a Tier 3 deal with Greater Manchester.</p> 



<p class="spacer"></p>
<img alt="">

 
<p class="credit"> (Image: Getty Images)</p>

   <p>It came as Downing Street escalated its war of words with Greater Manchester, warning that the region's intensive care capacity could be overwhelmed by Covid-19 within week.</p> <p>Projections produced by the Government suggested Manchester&#8217;s hospitals risked being overwhelmed by November 12.</p> <p>The Prime Minister&#8217;s official spokesman told a Westminster briefing: &#8220;Cases in Greater Manchester continue to rise.</p> <p>&#8220;In the over-60 age group, cases have tripled in the most recent 15 days of full data &#8211; there were 89 cases per 100,000 on September 27 compared to 282 per 100,000 on October 12.</p> <p>&#8220;Hospital admissions in Greater Manchester are doubling every nine days.&#8221;</p> 



<p class="spacer"></p>
<img alt="">

 
<p class="credit"> (Image: PA)</p>

   <p>Currently the number of Covid-19 patients in intensive care is around 40% of that seen at the peak of the first wave.</p> <p>Asked if that meant hospitals being overwhelmed, the spokesman said: &#8220;Yes, that&#8217;s the entire surge ICU capacity.&#8221;</p> <p>The Prime Minister&#8217;s official spokesman said talks with Greater Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham and local leaders were continuing.</p> <p>&#8220;If at all possible, we do want to engage constructively with Greater Manchester and agree on a way forward,&#8221; the spokesman said.</p> <p>But if agreement cannot be reached, the Government will need to intervene &#8220;in order to protect hospitals and save the lives of residents&#8221;.</p> <p> The comments came after ministers insisted Greater Manchester&#8217;s <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/coronavirus-1">coronavirus</a> status will end within days &#8211; either with a deal which could see tens of millions of pounds in extra funding for the region or with the imposition of harsh restrictions from Westminster. </p> 



<p class="spacer"></p>
<img alt="">

 
<p class="credit"> (Image: Andy Commins / Daily Mirror)</p>

   <p>Greater Manchester&#8217;s leaders have resisted moving into Tier 3, which would see pubs and bars ordered to close as part of a package of strict measures &#8211; but Communities Secretary Robert Jenrick said it was time for &#8220;decisive action&#8221;.</p> <p>He said a range of support had been offered to the region, including &#8220;more resources for local councils&#8221;, greater involvement in contact tracing and the potential use of the military to assist local authorities.</p> <p>Greater Manchester&#8217;s mayor, Andy Burnham, has pressed for a Commons vote to break the &#8220;impasse&#8221;.</p> <p>Senior Conservatives, including the influential chairman of the 1922 committee, Sir Graham Brady, have backed the Greater Manchester mayor in resisting Tier 3 for the region.</p> <p>Mr Burnham called for MPs to help workers and businesses hit with the harshest restrictions by having an urgent debate this week, warning &#8220;this is not just a Greater Manchester issue&#8221;.</p> <p>Mr Jenrick said there had been &#8220;productive&#8221; negotiations with Greater Manchester, but Monday&#8217;s talks would be the &#8220;final discussions&#8221;.</p> <p class="loading-circle primary-theme-spinner"></p>

<p>&#8220;We do now need to draw these conversations to a conclusion and to take decisive action,&#8221; Mr Jenrick told BBC Radio 4&#8217;s Today programme.</p> <p>&#8220;We have set out the action that we think is appropriate but we are also willing to continue, and have final discussions, with council leaders and the mayor in Greater Manchester today to see if we can reach an amicable agreement.&#8221;</p> <p>Liverpool City Region received a &#163;44 million package as part of its Tier 3 measures, but Greater Manchester wants more extensive support &#8211; including a furlough scheme paying 80% of wages for affected workers.</p></div>
