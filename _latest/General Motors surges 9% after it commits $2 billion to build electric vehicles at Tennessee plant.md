<ul><li><strong>General Motors surged 9% on Tuesday after it said it would invest $2 billion in its Spring Hill, Tennessee, manufacturing plant to build electric vehicles.</strong></li><li><strong>The new Cadillac Lyriq will be the first electric vehicle model to roll off the production line in Tennessee, according to the announcement.&#160;</strong></li><li><strong>General Motors also said it would invest an additional $152.5 million in five other manufacturing plants spread across Michigan.</strong></li><li><strong>The announcement came ahead of tonight's 8 p.m. reveal of its new electric Hummer.</strong></li></ul><p><a href="https://markets.businessinsider.com/stocks/gm-stock?utm_source=markets&amp;utm_medium=ingest" target="_blank">General Motors</a> surged 9% on Tuesday after <a href="https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/gm-investing-2-billion-to-transition-spring-hill-tennessee-plant-to-build-electric-vehicles-including-cadillac-lyriq-301155945.html" target="_blank">it said it would</a> invest $2 billion into its Spring Hill, Tennessee, manufacturing plant to transition it to electric vehicle production.</p><p>The first electric vehicle scheduled to roll off the line will be the Cadillac Lyriq, according to GM. The company didn't provide an official launch date of the vehicle.</p><p>In addition to electric vehicles, the Tennessee plant will also continue to build internal combustion engine-powered vehicles.</p><p>General Motors also said it would invest $152.5 million in five manufacturing plants across Michigan.
