<div><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">&#8220;I don&#8217;t know anyone who voted for Trump in 2016 who would not do it again,&#8221; said Bob Turner, a former Republican congressman who has lived in Breezy Point for 40 years.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">Breezy Point is a private community, with roughly 2,800 homes on land managed by a cooperative that also furnishes services typically provided by the city. In the 2016 presidential election, Mr. Trump garnered 77.5 percent of the vote in Breezy Point. Hillary Clinton swept the city overall with 78.4 percent.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">A short drive away in Queens &#8212; perhaps the most ethnically diverse place in the nation, if not the world &#8212; are predominantly Black Democratic strongholds areas where Mr. Trump received less than 5 percent of the vote in 2016.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">In a city where a majority of residents are people of color, the two-square-mile Breezy Point remains 92 percent white. Locals refer to it as the &#8220;Irish Riviera&#8221; because 62 percent of residents claim all or partly Irish ancestry.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">The enclave has its own security force and prides itself on the kind of resilience and self-sufficiency on full display after the terrorist attacks of Sept. 11, 2001, which killed more than 30 Breezy Pointers, many of them firefighters, and after <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2012/12/25/nyregion/breezy-point-battered-seaside-haven-recalls-its-trial-by-fire.html" title="">Hurricane Sandy</a>, which in 2012 <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2012/12/25/nyregion/breezy-point-battered-seaside-haven-recalls-its-trial-by-fire.html" title="">destroyed</a> about 350 houses and damaged hundreds more.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">Other areas of the city voted overwhelmingly for Mr. Trump in 2016. In Staten Island, he won a majority of the vote; in some Orthodox Jewish sections of Brooklyn, he topped 80 percent.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">The Breezy Point cooperative has rules about home sales that can make it difficult for outsiders to become homeowners. Prospective buyers have to put at least 50 percent down in cash, and get recommendations from three residents and approval by its board, residents said.</p></div>
