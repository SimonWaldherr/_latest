It happens after the DNA in skin cells is damaged (typically due to harmful UV rays) and then not repaired so it triggers mutations that can form malignant tumors.&#160;</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Around 15,900 new cases occur every year in the UK, with 2,285 Britons dying from the disease in 2016, according to Cancer Research UK statistics.&#160;</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Causes</p><ul class="mol-bullets-with-font"><li class="">Sun exposure: UV and UVB rays from the sun and tanning beds are harmful to the skin</li><li class="">Moles: The more moles you have, the greater the risk for getting melanoma&#160;</li><li class="">Skin type: Fairer skin has a higher risk for getting melanoma</li><li class="">Hair color: Red heads are more at risk than others</li><li class="">Personal history: If you've had melanoma once, then you are more likely to get it again</li><li class="">Family history: If previous relatives have been diagnosed, then that increases your risk</li></ul><p class="mol-para-with-font">Treatment&#160;</p><ul class="mol-bullets-with-font"><li class="">Removal of the melanoma:</li></ul><p class="mol-para-with-font">This can be done by removing the entire section of the tumor or by the surgeon removing the skin layer by layer. When a surgeon removes it layer by layer, this helps them figure out exactly where the cancer stops so they don't have to remove more skin than is necessary.&#160;</p><ul class="mol-bullets-with-font"><li class="">Skin grafting:&#160;</li></ul><p class="mol-para-with-font">The patient can decide to use a skin graft if the surgery has left behind discoloration or an indent.&#160;</p><ul class="mol-bullets-with-font"><li class="">Immunotherapy, radiation treatment or chemotherapy:&#160;</li></ul><p class="mol-para-with-font">This is needed if the cancer reaches stage III or IV. That means that the cancerous cells have spread to the lymph nodes or other organs in the body.&#160;</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Prevention</p><ul class="mol-bullets-with-font"><li>Use sunscreen and do not burn</li><li>Avoid tanning outside and in beds&#160;</li><li>Apply sunscreen 30 minutes before going outside</li><li class="">Keep newborns out of the sun</li><li>Examine your skin every month</li><li class="">See your physician every year for a skin exam&#160;</li></ul><p class="mol-para-with-font mol-style-italic">&#160;Source: Skin Cancer Foundation and American Cancer Society</p><p class="adHolder mpu" id="mpu_factbox_1"><p class="mol-ads-label-container"><p class="mol-ads-label">Advertisement</p></p>
