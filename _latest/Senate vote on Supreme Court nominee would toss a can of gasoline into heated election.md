<div><p class="gnt_ar_by">The Editorial Board</p><p class="gnt_ar_pb">USA TODAY</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">If Judge Amy Coney Barrett's nomination to the Supreme Court is approved Thursday by the Judiciary Committee&#160;and <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2020/10/21/any-coney-barrett-senate-vote-monday-supreme-court-nominee/6005198002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Monday&#160;by the full Senate</a>,&#160;it will&#160;mark a milestone.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">For the first time in more than a century, the majority of the court&#8217;s nine members will have been selected by presidents who reached the Oval Office by <a href="https://www.factcheck.org/2008/03/presidents-winning-without-popular-vote/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">losing the popular vote</a>.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Also novel is that this nomination is being rammed through <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/opinion/todaysdebate/2020/09/26/trump-nominates-amy-coney-barrett-questions-senate-editorials-debates/3532487001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">in the midst of a&#160;presidential election</a>, against <a href="https://www.senate.gov/legislative/nominations/SupremeCourtNominations1789present.htm" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">230 years of precedent</a>.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">And this particular election, it should be noted, is not your typical one. <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/elections/2020/10/21/voter-turnout-election-could-reach-highest-rate-more-than-century/3712184001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Tens of millions of Americans have already</a> rushed in mail ballots or stood in line for <a href="https://www.abc.net.au/news/2020-10-14/why-some-americans-had-to-wait-11-hours-to-vote/12765128" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">as many as 11&#160;hours</a> to vote.</p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">Judging Judge&#160;Amy Coney Barrett</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Under normal circumstances, the Editorial Board&#160;would be assessing Barrett according to our <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/opinion/2017/04/02/gorsuch-trump-supreme-court-confirmation-conservatives-senate-democrats/99953744/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">usual criteria for nominees to the nation's&#160;highest court</a>. We'd&#160;be&#160;weighing her impressive legal career, her sterling&#160;academic credentials and her appealing life story against some curious and troubling aspects of her work.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The latter include a ruling in which she said the use of the <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/2020/10/05/amy-coney-barrett-scotus-could-affect-workplace-protections/5879057002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">N-word did not constitute workplace discrimination</a> and her refusal during her confirmation hearings to <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2020/10/14/amy-coney-barrett-senators-grill-judge-supreme-court-hearing/3642788001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">accept climate science</a> as settled.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">We'd also be debating whether her conservative views place her within the broad judicial mainstream, and whether she has sufficient respect for legal&#160;precedent.&#160;</p><img class="gnt_em_img_i" alt="Supreme Court nominee Amy Coney Barrett and Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell on Sept. 29, 2020."><p class="gnt_em_img_ccw gnt_em_img_ccw__cap gnt_em_img_ccw__crd"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">But we can&#8217;t even get to these questions&#160;without stating the obvious:&#160;This rushed nomination and confirmation process is&#160;a slap in the face&#160;to the American people. It threatens to undermine&#160;the credibility of the Supreme Court and&#160;invite retaliation from the Democrats if they win control of the White House and Senate.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">While not unconstitutional,&#160;it is wildly <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/opinion/todaysdebate/2020/09/26/trump-nominates-amy-coney-barrett-questions-senate-editorials-debates/3532487001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">hypocritical of Republican leaders</a> to hold up a court nominee for most of 2016&#160;&#8212;&#160;arguing that the people should have their say in the upcoming election&#160;&#8212;&#160;and then deny the people their say this time&#160;when their party&#8217;s nominee is under consideration less than two&#160;weeks&#160;before Election Day.</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-opinion-debate-2" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">Voter laws, Affordable Care Act</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">All this is occurring as the Supreme Court has&#160;claimed sweeping powers that reach deeply into the lives of Americans.&#160;In recent years this court, and ones of slightly different composition, have sided with Republicans on stringent voter ID laws, aggressive voter registration purges and&#160;political gerrymandering.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">More ominously, just after the election, the court will return to the Affordable Care Act, a measure that allows millions to buy health insurance. Republicans have tried to kill the law&#160;in Congress, and failed, and tried to kill it in the courts, and failed.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Into this already heated and volatile situation, Barrett is being thrown like a jerrycan of gasoline. Already, her hurried White House nomination announcement will go down in history as a <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/in-depth/news/investigations/2020/10/07/likely-rose-garden-covid-superspreader-white-house-drew-hundreds/3636925001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">COVID-19 superspreader event</a>.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Everything about this hasty confirmation process for a lifetime appointment&#160;is misguided.&#160;It smacks of a desperate, last-minute power grab by Republicans fearful of the voters' impending verdict.</p><a class="gnt_em gnt_em__lp gnt_em_gl gnt_em__el" href="/picture-gallery/opinion/columnists/mike-thompson/2020/05/11/presidential-election-2020-trump-biden-cartoons-humor-satire-coronavirus/3110265001/"><img class="gnt_em_gl_i" alt="" width="660" height="372"><p class="gnt_em_gl_i_nt"></p><p class="gnt_em_gl_vg"></p><p class="gnt_em_t gnt_em_t__hb"></p></a></div>
