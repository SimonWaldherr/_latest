<div><p class="teal-video-wrap"></p><p class="gnt_em_vt"></p><p class="gnt_em_vb gnt_em_vb__te"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Tuesday may finally bring answers to the question California theme park operators have been asking for months: When can we reopen?&#160;There's just one problem: big parks like Disneyland and Universal Studios Hollywood may not like it.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Gov. Gavin Newsom indicated during his <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MXaEstnNhnE" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">weekly press conference </a>Monday that the state will categorize theme parks by size and that larger ones may be among the last to reopen to continue to slow the <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/in-depth/news/2020/03/03/coronavirus-map-tracking-spread-covid-19-outbreak-us-and-worldwide/4906772002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">spread of the coronavirus</a>, which causes COVID-19. When they do, attendance would likely be capped at 25%. He said that California&#160;Health and Human Services Secretary&#160;Dr. Mark Ghaly would provide details as part of his weekly COVID-19 update on Tuesday afternoon.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">&#8220;We&#8217;re going to break up the theme parks," Newsom said. "It&#8217;s not just one or two brands. It&#8217;s many different parts that are part of the theme park industry."&#160;</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-travel-experience_america-theme_parks-2" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The&#160;California Attractions and Parks Association, which represents Disneyland and other theme parks across the state,&#160;was <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/experience/america/theme-parks/2020/10/02/disneyland-theme-parks-odds-california-covid-19-guidelines/5894889002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">unhappy with the state's initial draft guidelines</a>.&#160;<a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/09/business/disney-world-coronavirus.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">The New York Times</a>&#160;reported that the early draft indicated that theme parks couldn't reopen until the infection rate in their county moved into Tier 4 ("minimum" risk)&#160;of the <a href="https://covid19.ca.gov/safer-economy/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">state's reopening blueprint</a>. To qualify, the county could only have&#160;one case per 100,000 residents with a positivity rate below 2%.&#160;</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-travel-experience_america-theme_parks-3" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Currently, Orange County, where Disneyland is located, is in Tier 2, which poses a "substantial"&#160;risk of infection.&#160;It reported coronavirus cases at a rate of 37 cases per week per 100,000 people for the week of&#160;Oct. 13-19,&#160;according to Johns Hopkins University data.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Orange County, Florida, home to Disney World, reported coronavirus cases at a rate of 101 cases per week per 100,000 people for the same period. That's nearly three times California's infection rate, and yet Florida has allowed theme parks there to remain open while California's remain shuttered.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"It seems to me that the guidelines that are set up by the state of California are more stringent than any state across the country," Disney CEO Bob Chapek told CNBC in an&#160;<a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/experience/america/theme-parks/2020/10/13/disney-world-attendance-remain-capped-new-cdc-covid-19-guidance/5977875002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">interview&#160;last week</a>.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Newsom has repeatedly cited the arrival of cooler temperatures&#160;and flu season as grounds for additional caution.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">&#8220;This is an area of obvious and real concern,&#8221; Newsom said during his news conference. &#8220;That&#8217;s why we&#8217;re being very sober, and &#8211; forgive me&#160;&#8211; stubborn about some industries in the state that I know are eager to get guidelines.&#8221;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The governor&#160;promised said in mid-September that the guidelines&#160;were <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/2020/09/16/disneyland-calif-governor-closer-allowing-theme-park-reopening/3475196001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">coming&#160;"very, very soon"</a>&#160;but changed course a few weeks later and <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/2020/10/07/disneyland-california-gov-newsom-slows-push-reopen-theme-parks/5917472002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">said&#160;the state was in no hurry</a> to reopen theme parks.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The parks have been been closed for seven months now and in limbo since the summer&#160;as they waited for the state to announce the benchmarks for reopening.&#160; Disneyland, which <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/news/2020/06/24/disneyland-california-delays-reopening-past-july-17/3255300001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">scrapped its planned July 17 reopening</a> due to this summer's COVID-19 spike, and Universal Studios&#160;could only watch and take notes&#160;as their&#160;Florida counterparts reopened.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">But even though Disney and Universal's&#160;Orlando parks opened without spurring new COVID-19 surges, it wasn't enough to prevent financial upheaval.&#160;In late September, <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/money/business/2020/09/29/coronavirus-effect-disney-lay-off-28-000-california-florida/3579963001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Disney announced&#160;layoffs of 28,000 employees</a> in California and Florida, and <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/news/2020/08/09/disney-world-reduce-hours-september-visits-drop-amid-covid-19/3330470001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Disney World shortened its hours</a> in response to&#160;smaller-than-anticipated crowds. The city of Anaheim, where Disneyland&#160;is located, now <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/2020/09/16/disneyland-calif-governor-closer-allowing-theme-park-reopening/3475196001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">expects a&#160;fiscal-year deficit of at least $75 million</a>, instead of the surplus that had been expected before the pandemic struck.</p><p href="https://deadline.com/2020/10/still-shuttered-universal-studios-hollywood-laid-off-over-2200-workers-since-july-1234597054/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Deadline</a> and <a href="https://www.yahoo.com/entertainment/still-shuttered-universal-studios-hollywood-015408227.html?guccounter=1&amp;guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8&amp;guce_referrer_sig=AQAAALynDOKkgBwkYurbxTBcfmlzAHsWMfEFsbYUZbuKrxkTM3YwlIvaIYTbvV9_mZgy0MBweV001wA0kOIOXDBNGe87cHuHzVCo4sW4p2gmFPxq5VMagmIKqTqA3_GCYTk0c9GPPSvqAWwytRylZZD6yACwZXOFSf11aDlb-uMW9pns" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Yahoo News</a> have both reported that Universal Studios Hollywood&#160;has&#160;laid off&#160;more than 2,200 park workers, cutting 1,374 positions permanently and furloughing another 849, though NBCUniversal wouldn't comment on the exact number to either outlet.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">On Saturday, the Coalition of Resort Labor Unions, which had <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/news/2020/06/27/disney-reopening-california-unions-demand-testing-safety-protocols/3270091001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">opposed Disneyland's July reopening,</a>&#160;<a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/travel/experience/america/theme-parks/2020/10/19/reopen-disneyland-unions-tell-california-governor-gavin-newsom/5980172002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">sent a letter to Newsom</a> saying that Disney had listened to its employees and demonstrated it can operate its parks safely.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The unions, including the&#160;International Alliance of Theatrical Stage Employees (IATSE), the American Federation of Musicians (AFM) and&#160;Independent Employee Service Association (IESA),&#160;said they&#160;believe Disneyland can reopen safely once there are fewer than four new cases per 100,000 residents and the&#160;rate of positive tests falls below 5% in Orange County, California, where the park is located. That improvement&#160;would move the county into Tier 3 or "moderate" risk territory.&#160;&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Newsom stressed Monday that the guidelines are being crafted "with our eyes wide open on what's happening now around the world.
