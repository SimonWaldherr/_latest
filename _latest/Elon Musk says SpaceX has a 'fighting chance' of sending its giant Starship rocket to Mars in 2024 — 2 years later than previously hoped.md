<p>SpaceX founder Elon Musk said Friday the company has a "fighting chance" of sending its Starship rocket to Mars in 2024 &#8212; two years later than hoped.</p><p>During <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y5Aw6WG4Dww&amp;feature=emb_title">Mars Society's 2020 virtual conference</a>, Musk said SpaceX could send an unmanned Starship rocket, which is currently in its prototype stage, to Mars in 2024 provided it sees "exponential improvement in our rate of innovation."</p><p>He later said this date was only a "guess."</p><p>Musk <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/why-elon-musk-launched-tesla-mars-falcon-heavy-2018-3">said in 2017</a> that his "aspirational" timeline was for <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/elon-musk-spacex-mars-plan-timeline-2018-10#2022-launch-two-missions-to-mars-full-of-cargo-and-supplies-but-no-people-5">SpaceX to send cargo ships to Mars in 2022</a>, followed by a crewed mission two years later.</p>
