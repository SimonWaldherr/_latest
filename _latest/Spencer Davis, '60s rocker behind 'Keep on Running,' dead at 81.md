him with embracing folk blues and rhythm and blues music. </p><a></a><p>"He was definitely a man with a vision, and one of the pioneers of the British invasion of America in the sixties," Winwood said in a <a href="https://www.stevewinwood.com/news/299410" target="_blank">statement</a> released Tuesday.</p><img class="media__image media__image--responsive" alt="The Spencer Davis Group in 1966 -- class="zn-body__paragraph">The band found success with several hits, including, "Somebody Help Me," "I'm a Man," "Keep On Running" and "Gimme Some Lovin'" -- the latter won Davis a BMI award after the song was broadcast more than 3 million times.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">Davis' songs appeared on the soundtracks of many feature films, including "The Big Chill," "Top Gun," "Notting Hill" and "The Blues Brothers." </p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">In their early days on the club circuit, The Spencer Davis Group toured with several future rock legends, including the Rolling Stones and The He also worked as an executive at Island Records in the mid-70s, working with artists including Bob Marley and Robert Palmer, before heading up an independent Hollywood record label in the early 1980s. </p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">He continued to perform, and created the album "Crossfire", featuring artists including Dusty Springfield and Booker T.
