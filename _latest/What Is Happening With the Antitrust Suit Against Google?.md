<div><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">The <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/20/technology/google-antitrust.html?action=click&amp;module=Top%20Stories&amp;pgtype=Homepage" title="">Justice Department sued Google</a> on Tuesday, accusing the company of illegally abusing its dominance in internet search in ways that harm competitors and consumers.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">The suit is the first antitrust action against the company, owned by Alphabet, to result from investigations by the Justice Department, Congress and 50 states and territories. <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/09/22/technology/justice-dept-case-google-search-dominance.html" title="">State attorneys general and federal officials</a> have also been investigating Google&#8217;s behavior in the market for online advertising. And a group of states is exploring a broader search case against Google.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">Here is what you need to know about the suit.</p><h2 class="css-1aoo5yy eoo0vm40" id="link-64560580">What is really happening here?</h2><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">This is one step against a single company. But it is also a response to the policy question of what measures, if any, should be taken to curb today&#8217;s tech giants, which hold the power to shape markets, communication and even public opinion.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">Politics steered the timing and shape of this suit. <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/09/03/us/politics/google-antitrust-justice-department.html" title="">Attorney General William P. Barr wanted to move quickly</a> to take action before the election, making good on President Trump&#8217;s pledge to take on Big Tech. Eleven states joined the suit.</p></div>
