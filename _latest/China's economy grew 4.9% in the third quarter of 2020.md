<div><p class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">Hong Kong (CNN Business)<a href="https://edition.cnn.com/2020/10/10/economy/china-global-economy-intl-hnk/index.html" target="_blank">China's economy</a> is picking up as the country continues to dig its way out of the turmoil caused by the <a href="https://edition.cnn.com/2020/07/15/economy/china-gdp-intl-hnk/index.html" target="_blank">coronavirus pandemic</a>.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">The world's second largest economy expanded 4.9% in the July-to-September quarter compared to a year ago, according to government statistics released Monday.</p><a></a><p>The pace was quicker than the <a href="https://edition.cnn.com/2020/07/15/economy/china-gdp-intl-hnk/index.html" target="_blank">3.2% increase</a> that China recorded in the second quarter, when it managed to avoid the pandemic-fueled recession that has gripped much of the globe. But the growth was also a bit weaker than expected: Analysts polled by Refinitiv predicted that China's economy would expand 5.2%.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">"China's economy continued its rapid rebound last quarter, with the recovery broadening out and becoming less reliant on investment-led stimulus," wrote Julian Evans-Pritchard, senior China economist for Capital Economics, in a research note. </p><p id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default"></p><p></p><p></p><ul class="cn cn-list-hierarchical-xs cn--idx-5 cn-zoneAdContainer"></ul><p class="zn-body__paragraph">As much of the world continues to struggle with the virus, China's recovery has been relatively speedy. The country enforced stringent lockdown and population tracking policies intended to contain the virus, and set aside hundreds of billions of dollars for major infrastructure projects to fuel economic growth. The economy grew a cumulative 0.7% through the first nine months of this year. </p><p class="read-more-gradient"></p><p class="read-more-link" id="js-body-read-more">Read More</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">"What's more, the monthly data show that growth was still accelerating heading into Q4," Evans-Pritchard added.</p><a href="/2020/10/10/economy/china-global-economy-intl-hnk/index.html"><img class="media__image media__image--responsive" alt="China is winning the global economic recovery" src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhEAAJAJEAAAAAAP///////wAAACH5BAEAAAIALAAAAAAQAAkAAAIKlI+py+0Po5yUFQA7"><p class="img__preloader"></p><img alt="China is winning the global economic recovery" class="media__image" src="//cdn.cnn.com/cnnnext/dam/assets/201008053350-02-china-great-wall-1004-large-169.jpg"></a><span class="el__storyelement__header"><a href="/2020/10/10/economy/china-global-economy-intl-hnk/index.html">China is winning the global economic recovery</a></span><p class="zn-body__paragraph">Industrial production grew 5.8% in the third quarter from a year ago, according to Liu Aihua, a spokeswoman for China's National Bureau of Statistics.
