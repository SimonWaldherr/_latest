<div><p>A zoo employee was mauled to death by a group of bears over the weekend in China as visitors watched in horror.</p>
<p>The gruesome incident occurred in the &#8220;wild beast&#8221; exhibit Saturday at Shanghai Wildlife Park, <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-china-54596717" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">the BBC reported</a>.</p>
<p>Footage posted to Chinese social media platform Weibo shows parkgoers scream from inside a tour bus as a group of bears run toward and surround the employee, <a href="https://www.abc.net.au/news/2020-10-19/zookeeper-mauled-by-bears-in-china-zoo-watched-by-visitors/12783194" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Australian outlet ABC News reported</a>.</p>
<p>&#8220;There&#8217;s someone!&#8221; one of the parkgoers shouts, while another could be heard asking, &#8220;What&#8217;s going on?&#8221;</p>
<p>Shanghai Wildlife Park said it was &#8220;saddened&#8221; by the employee&#8217;s death in a statement posted to its website.</p>
<p>&#8220;Our park is extremely saddened by such a tragedy, expressing deep condolences to the deceased, expressing deep sympathy to the families of the deceased and apologizing for the inconvenience it caused to tourists,&#8221; the statement said.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s unclear what type of bears were involved or whether they will remain at the wildlife park.</p>
<p>The exhibit has been temporarily closed to visitors as the park works with local authorities to investigate the incident, the zoo said.</p>
