<p>Ireland has warned that an estimated 150,000 people &#8212; around 3% of the entire population &#8212; will lose their job within days as the country moves to its highest level of coronavirus restrictions.</p><p>Starting Thursday, non-essential retailers will shut and people are asked to remain at home whenever possible, with a 5-kilometer (3.1-mile) travel limit for exercise. Social or family gatherings in homes or gardens will be banned.</p><p>Restaurants and bars can only provide takeaway service.</p><p>The lockdown, similar to the one imposed in March, will last six weeks.</p>
<p id="gpt-post-politics-mobile_in_post_ad-fluid-2" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-mobile"></p>

<p id="gpt-post-politics-desktop_in_post_ad-fluid-2" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-desktop"></p>
<p>Leo Varadkar, deputy premier, said in a press conference on Monday that the government estimates "about 150,000" will be laid off in the coming days.</p><p>He said it would cost around &#8364;200 million ($236 million) per week to support the economy through employment benefits and grants.&#160;</p><p>"So about &#8364;1.5 billion ($1.77 billion) for six weeks and that's not taking account, though, of the potential fall in [tax] revenue that may arise from business closures," Varadkar said.</p><p>Prime Minister Miche&#225;l Martin said in a televised speech that the country already has "what is probably Europe's strictest regime," but that current measures were not enough to suppress the virus.</p>
<p id="gpt-post-politics-mobile_in_post_ad-fluid-3" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-mobile"></p>

<p id="gpt-post-politics-desktop_in_post_ad-fluid-3" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-desktop"></p>
<p>"The government has decided that the evidence of a potentially grave situation arising in the weeks ahead is now too strong," he said.</p><p>"If we pull together over the next six weeks we will have the opportunity to celebrate Christmas in a meaningful way."</p><p>The "Level 5" restrictions will last until December 1.</p><p>Schools, childcare facilities and manufacturers can stay open.</p>

<p id="gpt-post-politics-desktop_in_post_ad-fluid-4" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-desktop"></p>
<p>The government said the restrictions would come into effect at midnight on Wednesday, <a href="https://twitter.com/gavreilly/status/1318300691975327750">later clarifying this meant late Wednesday night, early Thursday morning</a>.</p><p>Infections have been on the rise in Ireland, with nearly 12,500 new coronavirus cases reported in the past two weeks. The death toll in the country stands at 1,852.</p>
