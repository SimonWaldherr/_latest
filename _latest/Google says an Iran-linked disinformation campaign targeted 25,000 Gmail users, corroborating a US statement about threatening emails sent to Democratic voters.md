<p>Google appears to have corroborated a US national intelligence statement that an Iranian campaign sent registered Democratic voters <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/john-ratcliffe-russia-iran-taking-specific-actions-meddle-in-election-2020-10?r=US&amp;IR=T">a spate of intimidating emails</a>&#160;claiming to be from far-right group The Proud Boys.</p><p>Google first <a href="https://twitter.com/donie/status/1319140203077001216">told CNN reporter</a> Donie O'Sullivan that it had detected 25,000 emails sent to Gmail users as part of a suspected Iranian disinformation campaign, and later confirmed this in a statement to Business Insider.</p><p>It followed Director of National Intelligence John Ratcliffe saying Wednesday that both Russia and Iran had sought to disrupt voting, and specifically that Iran had been sending "spoofed emails." Registered Democratic voters in Florida and Alaska <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/us-voters-alaska-florida-foreign-email-vote-trump-proud-boys-2020-10?r=US&amp;IR=T">started receiving emails Tuesday</a> purporting to be from far-right group The Proud Boys, threatening violence unless they voted for Donald Trump in the upcoming presidential election.&#160;</p><p>Google said that&#160;"we and others have seen evidence that an operation linked to Iran sent inauthentic emails to people in the US over the past 24 hours."</p>
