<div><p>Unemployment rates fell in 30 states in September, while they rose in eight states and were unchanged in 12 states and the District of Columbia, the <a href="https://www.bls.gov/news.release/pdf/laus.pdf" target="_blank">U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics</a> reported Tuesday.</p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p>The trend shows continued recovery from the effects of the COVID-19 pandemic but rates are higher in all states than they were before the coronavirus struck in late February and compared to year-ago levels.</p><p>Nonfarm employment increased in 30 states month to month but decreased year over year in 48 states and the District of Columbia.The largest job gains came in <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-states/new-york">New York</a>, <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-states/california">California</a> and <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-states/new-jersey">New Jersey</a>. On a percentage basis, the biggest gains occurred in New Jersey, New York and <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-states/wyoming">Wyoming</a>. </p><p><a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-states/hawaii">Hawaii</a> had the highest unemployment rate in September at 15.1%, followed by <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-states/nevada">Nevada</a> at 12.6%. <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-states/nebraska">Nebraska</a> had the lowest at 3.5%. </p><p size="5" class="Raw-slyvem-0 ftSHuE"></p><p>Nationally, the jobs recovery has stalled with weekly jobless claims hovering in the high 800,000 range. They spiked in March, then fell through the summer as the economy began to recover and federal stimulus payments such as enhanced unemployment benefits kicked in. <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/business/articles/2020-07-24/extra-unemployment-aid-expires-as-virus-threatens-new-states">Those expired in late July</a> and a second wave of coronavirus infections began to show up, forcing more business shutdowns and restrictions.</p><p>Ian Shepherdson, chief economist at Pantheon Macroeconomics, <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2020/10/08/weekly-jobless-claims.html" target="_blank">told CNBC earlier this month</a> that "the downward trend has stalled, more or less."</p></div>
