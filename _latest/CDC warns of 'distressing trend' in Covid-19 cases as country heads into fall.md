<div><p class="endmarkEnabled">The government's top public health officials warned that the number of Covid-19 cases is rising across a majority of the country in a rare briefing Wednesday afternoon.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Dr. Jay Butler, deputy director for infectious diseases at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, said the agency has noted a "distressing trend" in which coronavirus case numbers are "increasing in nearly 75 percent of the country."</p><p></p><p id="taboolaReadMoreBelow"></p><p class="endmarkEnabled"><strong><a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/coronavirus">Full coverage of the coronavirus outbreak</a></strong></p><p class="endmarkEnabled"><strong></strong>"This past week, we've seen nearly 60,000 cases a day on average, as well as 700 deaths," he said.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Butler, a respected career scientist at the agency, spoke Wednesday at a rare on-camera media briefing at the CDC's headquarters in Atlanta, alongside Dr. Robert Redfield, the CDC's director, and Health and Human Services Secretary Alex Azar.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Much of the increase in cases is centered in the <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/new-covid-cases-surge-midwest-weather-cools-resistance-heats-n1243381" target="_blank">Midwest</a>. States like Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, Minnesota, Nebraska and Wisconsin have recorded rises in Covid-19 case numbers in the last two weeks. Public health officials attribute the spikes, in part, to cooler weather that is forcing people indoors.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"Smaller, more intimate gatherings with family, friends and neighbors may be driving infections," Butler said while acknowledging public pandemic fatigue.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"We get tired of wearing masks, but it continues to be as important as it's ever been," he said.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">As of Wednesday afternoon, the U.S. had logged 8.3 million cases of Covid-19 and more than a quarter-million deaths, according to an NBC News database. The <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/health-news/covid-19-pandemic-has-claimed-far-more-lives-reported-study-n1242970" target="_blank">death toll</a> may, in fact, be far higher, as recent <a href="https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2771761" target="_blank">studies </a>have suggested that many deaths related to Covid-19 have been underreported or misreported.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Redfield said Wednesday that the CDC is considering whether it would be reasonable to cut the time a person exposed to Covid-19 may need to be in quarantine. The current guidance is to quarantine for 14 days.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Redfield said a growing body of data is being looked at using testing during quarantine to shorten the 14-day window to 10 or even seven days.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"We don't want to have people quarantined for 14 days unnecessarily," he said.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled"><strong>Download the <a href="https://smart.link/5d5ad16083f88">NBC News app</a> for full coverage of the coronavirus outbreak</strong></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The health officials offered no updated timeline for trials of Covid-19 vaccine candidates, opting instead to continue the message of optimism that a safe and effective vaccine may be available "in a matter of weeks or months," Azar said.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"In the meantime, to bridge to that next phase, we have to take steps that can keep ourselves, our families and our communities safe," Azar said. That includes washing hands, <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/health-news/cdc-changes-guidance-close-contacts-emphasizes-importance-wearing-mask-n1244147" target="_blank">wearing masks</a> and staying physically distant.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled"><strong><a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/health-news/coronavirus-spreads-first-time-u-s-cdc-says-n1126566">Follow </a><a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health">NBC HEALTH</a><a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/health-news/coronavirus-spreads-first-time-u-s-cdc-says-n1126566"> on </a><a href="https://twitter.com/NBCNewsHealth">Twitter</a><a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/health-news/coronavirus-spreads-first-time-u-s-cdc-says-n1126566"> &amp; </a><a href="https://www.facebook.com/NBCNewsHealth/">Facebook</a>.</strong></p></div>
