Photo: 20 October 2020" src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/4B01/production/_115010291_ec758fb7-f457-40af-aa09-3c11a23c7310.jpg" width="976" height="549" class="css-evoj7m-Image ee0ct7c0"></span></span><p class="css-1ecljvk-StyledFigureCopyright e34k3c23"><p class="css-178wc68-VisuallyHidden e1y6uwnp0">image copyright</p>Reuters</p><p class="css-178wc68-VisuallyHidden e1y6uwnp0">image caption</p>Protesters in Lagos built barricades, blocking roads in Nigeria's commercial hub<p><b class="css-14iz86j-BoldText e5tfeyi0">A number of people taking part in a protest against police brutality have reportedly been shot dead or injured in Nigeria's biggest city, Lagos.</b></p><p>A witness told the BBC he had counted about 20 bodies and at least 50 injured after soldiers opened fire. Amnesty International said it had obtained credible reports of deaths. </p><p>The authorities have promised an investigation into the shooting.</p><p>An indefinite 24-hour curfew has been imposed on Lagos and other regions. </p><p>Protests over a now-disbanded police unit, the Special Anti-Robbery Squad (Sars), have been continuing for two weeks.</p><p>Reacting to the shootings in Lagos, <a href="https://twitter.com/HillaryClinton/status/1318676338354606081" class="css-yidnqd-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">former US Secretary of State Hillary Clinton called on Nigerian President Muhammadu Buhari and the army "to stop killing young #EndSARS protesters".</a></p><p>Nigerian footballer Odion Jude Ighalo, who plays for Manchester United, accused the Nigerian government of killing its own citizens. <a href="https://twitter.com/ighalojude/status/1318666220447715329?s=19" class="css-yidnqd-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">"I'm ashamed of this government," he said in a video posted on Twitter.</a></p><p class="css-72p885-ComponentWrapper-CrossheadComponentWrapper e1xue1i81"><h2 class="css-1jlqpzd-StyledHeading e1fj1fc10">What do we know about the shooting?</h2></p><p>Eyewitnesses spoke of uniformed men opening fire in the wealthy Lekki suburb of Lagos on Tuesday evening.</p><p>Armed soldiers were seen barricading the protest site moments before the shooting, BBC Nigeria correspondent Nayeni Jones reports. </p><p>Social media footage streamed live from the scene shows protesters tending to the wounded. </p><p>An unnamed eyewitness told BBC News: "At exactly 6.45pm [local time] the soldiers pulled up... and they started firing directly at we, the peaceful protesters. </p><p>"They were firing and they were advancing straight at us. It was chaos. Somebody got hit straight beside me and he died on the spot. </p><p>"It was pandemonium and they kept on shooting and shooting at us. It lasted for about an hour and a half and the soldiers were actually taking up the dead bodies."</p><p>He said the soldiers had built a barricade and ambulances could not reach the protest area.</p><p>Four witnesses told Reuters news agency soldiers had opened fire on demonstrators. One of them,  Alfred Ononugbo, 55, said: "They started firing ammunition toward the crowd. They were firing into the crowd. I saw the bullet hit one or two persons."</p><p>Amnesty International spokesman Isa Sanusi later said: "People were killed at the tollgate by security forces... we are working on verifying how many."</p><p class="css-72p885-ComponentWrapper-CrossheadComponentWrapper e1xue1i81"><h2 class="css-1jlqpzd-StyledHeading e1fj1fc10">How have the authorities reacted?</h2></p><p>Gboyega Akosile, a spokesman for the Lagos state governor, tweeted: <a href="https://twitter.com/gboyegaakosile/status/1318654172913405954" class="css-yidnqd-InlineLink e1no5rhv0">"There have been reports of shooting at the Lekki Toll Plaza, following the 24-hour curfew imposed on Lagos State to stop criminals who hid under the #EndSARS protests to unleash mayhem on innocent citizens."</a></p><p>He said the state authorities had ordered an "investigation into the incident". </p><p>Approached by Reuters for comment, a Nigerian army spokesman did not immediately respond.</p><p class="css-72p885-ComponentWrapper-CrossheadComponentWrapper e1xue1i81"><h2 class="css-1jlqpzd-StyledHeading e1fj1fc10">How did the unrest begin?</h2></p><p>Protests began nearly two weeks ago with calls for the Sars, which had been accused of illegal detentions, assaults and shootings, to be disbanded.</p><p>President Buhari dissolved the unit on 11 October.</p><p class="css-bhez6r-Container e1h75hkw0"></p><p class="css-178wc68-VisuallyHidden e1y6uwnp0">media caption</p>Who is policing Nigeria's police?<p>But the demonstrators called for more changes in the security forces as well as reforms to the way the country is run.</p><p>Lagos state Governor Babajide Sanwo-Olu has said that criminals have hijacked the protests.</p></div>
