</p><p>In <strong>Victoria</strong>, the <strong>Australian</strong> state hardest-hit by Covid-19, more than 200 people who went through hotel quarantine must be screened for HIV amid fears of cross-contamination from the incorrect use of blood glucose test devices.</p>
<p>Several such devices were used on multiple people in quarantine between 29 March and 20 August, necessitating screenings for blood-borne diseases such as hepatitis B and C and HIV.</p>
<p>It should be stressed the mistaken use of the devices carries a &#8220;very low clinical risk&#8221; according to the state&#8217;s premier, <strong>Dan Andrews</strong>.</p>
