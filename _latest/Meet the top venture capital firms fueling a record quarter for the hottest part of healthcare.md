<p>Digital health startups are benefiting from a private investment windfall as the coronavirus pandemic continues to upend the healthcare industry.</p><p>According to a <a href="https://www.cbinsights.com/research/report/healthcare-trends-q3-2020/">new report from CB Insights</a>, venture firms poured a record-breaking $21.8 billion into healthcare startups in the third quarter of 2020. The previous record, $18.1 billion, was <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/vcs-digital-health-startups-insitro-oscar-kernel-patient-ping-dispatch-2020-7">set in just the previous quarter</a>.</p><p>Digital health startups remained the highest point of interest to venture capital investors, consistent with previous quarters in 2020. Many of those startups, which rely on easy-to-use technology to deliver care and enable research, have capitalized on shuttered doctors' offices and other pandemic-induced changes in the industry to prove the value of their proposed solutions.</p><p>So far, investors have eaten it up: in the third quarter, VCs invested an all-time-high $8.4 billion in digital health startups alone.</p><p><strong>Read more: </strong><a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/telemedicine-startup-98point6-series-e-funding-venture-capital-l-catterton-2020-10">Why a startup that wants to upend how you go to the doctor raised a fresh $118 million instead of going public&#160;</a></p><p>Venture capital firms have been quick to jump on the opportunity, with many generalist firms joining healthcare specialty groups in competitive funding rounds.</p><p>All told, the five most active firms have backed 24 startups that range from early stage to pre-initial public offering. Some are tackling fertility and direct-to-consumer treatments, while others are looking to rethink hospital operations and IT.&#160;</p><h2><strong>Here are the 5 most active firms</strong></h2><ul><li><strong>General Catalyst</strong> invested in Olive, Ro, Loom, Commure, and Sprout. The firm has raised roughly $7 billion to date, <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/vcs-digital-health-startups-insitro-oscar-kernel-patient-ping-dispatch-2020-7">it told Business Insider in July</a>.</li><li><strong>GV</strong>, formerly known as Google Ventures, invested in Kindbody, Ready Responders, Freenome, Science 37, and Encoded Therapeutics. The Google-backed fund manages more than $4.5 billion.</li><li><strong>Khosla Ventures, </strong>a $5 billion firm, invested in Caption Health, Vicarious Surgical, Siren, Opentrons, and Ontera.</li><li><strong>Flare Capital Partners</strong> has roughly $455 million under management, and invested in LuminDX, Aetion, Eden Health, HealthVerity, Cohere Health.</li><li><strong>Section 32</strong> invested in Freenome, Evidation Health, Thrive Earlier Detection, Sema4, and Vineti. In April, <a href="https://medcitynews.com/2020/04/section-32-targets-350-million-for-third-fund/">MedCity News reported that it's looking to raise $350 million</a> for its latest fund.</li></ul>
