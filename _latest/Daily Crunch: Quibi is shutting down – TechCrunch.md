<p><strong>The big story: Quibi is shutting down</strong></p>
<p>The much-hyped streaming video app led by Jeffrey Katzenberg and Meg Whitman, which raised nearly $2 billion in funding, is <a href="https://techcrunch.com/2020/10/21/quibi-is-dead/">shutting down</a>, according to reports in <a href="https://www.theinformation.com/articles/quibi-to-shutter">The Information</a> and <a href="https://www.wsj.com/articles/quibi-weighs-shutting-down-as-problems-mount-11603301946?mod=breakingnews">The Wall Street Journal</a>.</p><p class="piano-inline-promo"></p>
<p>Katzenberg, a longtime Hollywood executive, had <a href="https://techcrunch.com/2020/05/12/quibi-founder-jeffrey-katzenberg-blames-coronavirus-for-the-streaming-apps-challenges/">blamed the coronavirus pandemic</a> for a lackluster launch in May &#8212; an app designed for on-the-go viewing didn&#8217;t have much appeal when people were largely stuck at home. And whatever the reason, none of Quibi&#8217;s shows ever became a breakout hit.</p>
<p>Quibi executives confirmed the news <a href="https://quibi-hq.medium.com/an-open-letter-from-quibi-8af6b415377f">in a post on Medium</a>.</p>
