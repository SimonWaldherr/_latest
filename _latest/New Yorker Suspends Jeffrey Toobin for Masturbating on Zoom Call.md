<div><span class="abc__textblock size--article"><p>The New Yorker has suspended reporter Jeffrey Toobin. Sources tell VICE it&#8217;s because he exposed himself during a Zoom call last week between members of the New Yorker and WNYC radio.</p>
</span><span class="abc__textblock size--article"><p>Toobin said in a statement to Motherboard: &#8220;I made an embarrassingly stupid mistake, believing I was off-camera. I apologize to my wife, family, friends and co-workers.&#8221;</p>
</span><span class="abc__textblock size--article"><p>&#8220;I believed I was not visible on Zoom. I thought no one on the Zoom call could see me. I thought I had muted the Zoom video,&#8221; he added.</p>
</span><span class="abc__textblock size--article"><p>New Yorker spokesperson Natalie Raabe said: &#8220;Jeffrey Toobin has been suspended while we investigate the matter.&#8221;</p>
</span><span class="abc__textblock size--article"><p>Toobin&#8217;s Conde Nast email has been disabled and he has not tweeted since October 13. He did, however, appear on CNN, where he is the network&#8217;s chief legal analyst, on Saturday. CNN did not respond to a request for comment.</p>
</span><span class="abc__textblock size--article"><p>The New Yorker editor David Remnick wrote in an email to all New Yorker staffers: &#8220;Dear All, As you may have read in various news reports today, one of our writers, Jeff Toobin, was suspended after an incident on a Zoom call last week. Please be assured that we take such matters seriously and that we are looking into it. Best, David.&#8221;</p>
</span><span class="abc__textblock size--article"><p>Doubleday, an imprint of Random House, which published Toobin&#8217;s latest book did not respond to emails. Neither did Toobin&#8217;s literary agent Kristine Dahl nor Greater Talent Network, which represents Toobin. WNYC did not respond to a request for comment.</p>
</span></div>
