<div><p>Tom Petty&#8217;s peers and admirers will celebrate what would&#8217;ve been the <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/local/la-me-tom-petty-20171002-story.html">late rock icon&#8217;s</a> 70th birthday with a star-studded virtual festival on Friday.</p><p>Set to stream on <a class="link" href="https://www.tompetty.com/" target="_blank">Petty&#8217;s website</a> and on Amazon Music&#8217;s Twitch channel, &#8220;Tom Petty&#8217;s 70th Birthday Bash&#8221; will feature performances and appearances by <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2020-09-30/stevie-nicks-fleetwood-mac-solo-concert-film">Stevie Nicks</a>, Beck, Adam Sandler, Brandi Carlile, Chris Stapleton, Eddie Vedder, the Foo Fighters, Jackson Browne, Margo Price and Post Malone, among many others. Mike Campbell and Benmont Tench, both of Petty&#8217;s long-running band the Heartbreakers, are set to perform as well.</p><p>The show, which organizers said will also include previously unseen live footage from Petty and the Heartbreakers, is scheduled to begin at 4 p.m. Pacific on Friday. Earlier that day, SiriusXM will air a Petty tribute with performances by acts including <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2020-08-19/killers-brandon-flowers-imploding-the-mirage">the Killers</a>, Jason Isbell, the Raconteurs and Kurt Vile.</p><p class="google-dfp-ad-caption">Advertisement</p>

<p></p><p>&#8220;Tom Petty&#8217;s 70th Birthday Bash&#8221; comes three years after <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/la-et-ms-tom-petty-appreciation-20171002-story.html">Petty&#8217;s death</a> from an accidental overdose in Los Angeles in 2017. That year, fans held a birthday celebration in the musician&#8217;s hometown of Gainesville, Fla., that became an annual tradition; this year&#8217;s installment will take place online due to the COVID-19 pandemic, though the Petty estate said in a statement that it intends to use the show to raise awareness and proceeds for Save Our Stages, which seeks to aid independent concert venues imperiled by the COVID shutdown.</p><p>Last week, the estate released a <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/music/story/2020-09-21/tom-petty-wildflowers-solo-album-reissue-rick-rubin">lavish reissue</a> of Petty&#8217;s 1994 solo album &#8220;Wildflowers,&#8221; which the singer&#8217;s producer Rick Rubin recently told The Times was Petty&#8217;s favorite of his many projects.</p></div>
