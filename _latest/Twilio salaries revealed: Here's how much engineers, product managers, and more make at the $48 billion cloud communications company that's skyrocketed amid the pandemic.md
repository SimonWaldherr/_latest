That's helped customers like grocery delivery service Instacart and medical record software giant Epic keep pace with the increased demands of the present moment.</p><p>Against that backdrop, Twilio is still growing: Not only does it plan to <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/twilio-acquires-segment-salesforce-analysts-2020-10">acquire</a> the customer data analytics company Segment for $3.2 billion, it's also&#160;<a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/amazon-web-services-twilio-remote-developer-interviews-coronavirus-2020-5">still hiring</a>.&#160;</p><p><strong>Read more:&#160;</strong><a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/twilio-acquires-segment-salesforce-analysts-2020-10">Twilio buying Segment for $3.2 billion is a 'transformational' move, according to analysts, who say it could help the ~$50 billion cloud communications firm take on giants like Salesforce and SAP</a></p><p>While Twilio doesn't give a lot of data into what it pays its workers,&#160;Business Insider dug through the firm's disclosure data with the <a href="https://www.foreignlaborcert.doleta.gov/performancedata.cfm">US Office of Foreign Labor Certification</a> to get a hint.&#160;</p><p>Companies are required to disclose information, including salary (or in some cases, salary ranges), when they hire foreign workers under the H1-B visa program, giving insight into what these major companies are willing to shell out for tech talent.
