French-made, with subtitles for an Anglophone audience, CMA had rave reviews from unimpeachably highbrow figures. The satirical novelist Jonathan Coe called it “the sharpest, lightest, funniest, warmest thing on screen at the moment”.

Like many American accounts of Parisian life, from Henry James onwards, Emily in Paris is too enchanted by the city’s allure to be sharp. But it is light, funny and beguiling. Perhaps, in these trying times, these qualities have become toxic. If so, tant pis for us.
