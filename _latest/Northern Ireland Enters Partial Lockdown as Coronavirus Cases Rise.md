Northern Ireland will enter a partial lockdown, closing schools, pubs and restaurants, in order to curb the spread of the coronavirus.

Arlene Foster, the first minister, announced the partial lockdown on Wednesday in the regional legislature known as Stormont, with the new rules going into effect Friday and lasting for four weeks. School closures, however, will begin Monday and only last for two weeks. Universities are encouraged to switch to distance learning.

The hospitality sector will close for everything except delivery and takeaway. Supermarkets will not be allowed to sell alcohol after 8 p.m. and there will be no indoor or contact sports involving members of different households except at the professional level.

Close-contact personal services, except essential health services, must stop and hairdressers are banned from working in homes. Gyms are allowed to remain open for individual workouts, but group fitness classes are prohibited.

Religious institutions are allowed to remain open, but people must wear face coverings. Retail shops are allowed to remain open.

Northern Ireland currently has more than 21,890 confirmed cases of COVID-19, with 6,286 of those cases testing positive in the last seven days, according to the Department of Health . Tuesday saw 863 new cases.

Foster called the surge in cases a "very worrying increase," The New York Times reported .
