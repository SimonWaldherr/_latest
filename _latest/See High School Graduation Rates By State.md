<div><p>The nation's graduation rate is up to approximately 88%, with the average state graduation rates in the 2017-2018 academic year ranging from 74% to 94%, according to data reported by 17,404 ranked schools in the 2020 U.S. News <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools">Best High Schools</a> rankings. </p><p>A high school graduation rate is an indicator of how well a school is serving all of its students, with the rate calculated by analyzing the proportion of students who started high school in ninth grade that graduate four years later. The high schools most successfully getting students to graduation day within that time frame are spread across the country and are not limited to a specific region or state.</p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p>But some states stand out. The states with the highest average high school graduation rates are <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/iowa">Iowa</a>, <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/kentucky">Kentucky</a> and <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/north-dakota">North Dakota</a>, each graduating an average of 94% of its classes in 2018. </p><p>Other states fall short. <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/arizona">Arizona</a> and <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/new-mexico">New Mexico</a> continue to report the nation's lowest average graduation rates, each at just 74%.</p><p>The map below shows the average graduation rate for each state based on the 2018 graduation cohort, which is made up of students who entered ninth grade in the 2014-2015 school year. <br></p><p>The number of schools from each state in which graduation rate data was available ranges from 35 in the <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/district-of-columbia">District of Columbia</a> to 1,610 in <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/california">California</a>. While no state had a 100% average graduation rate &#8211; and each state has its own requirements for high school completion and determining graduation rates &#8211; some individual schools reached 100% for the class of 2018.</p><p>More than 260 high schools in <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/texas">Texas</a> reported a graduation rate of 100%, the most of any state. <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/kansas">Kansas</a> follows, with 91 high schools reporting a 100% graduation rate, and then <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/missouri">Missouri</a>, with 75. </p><p id="ad" class="ad"></p><p>Alabama, Hawaii, Vermont and Washington reported zero high schools that successfully graduated 100% of their classes. Arkansas, Delaware, Louisiana and Maryland did not provide precise graduation rates for schools whose graduation rates were 95% or higher and therefore are not included in the graph below.<br></p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p>Find more information on the top high schools in <a href="/education/best-high-schools/articles/map-see-the-top-100-high-schools-in-the-nation">each state</a>, and explore data on graduation rates, test scores for state-mandated exams and college placement exams, student-teacher ratios and more on school profile pages on usnews.com.</p><p><i>See the complete <a href="https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/rankings-overview">Best High Schools</a> rankings.</i></p></div>
