<div><p>Click to email this to a friend (Opens in new window)</p><p>Jeff Bridges has revealed that he&#8217;s battling lymphoma.</p>
<p>The iconic actor, 70, made the announcement on Twitter Monday night, <a href="https://twitter.com/TheJeffBridges/status/1318341223384518663" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">writing</a>, &#8220;As the Dude would say &#8230; New s&#8211;t has come to light.&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8220;I have been diagnosed with Lymphoma. Although it is a serious disease, I feel fortunate that I have a great team of doctors and the prognosis is good. I&#8217;m starting treatment and will keep you posted on my recovery.&#8221;</p>
<p>&#8220;I&#8217;m profoundly grateful for the love and support from my family and friends,&#8221; <a href="https://twitter.com/TheJeffBridges/status/1318341224772796417" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Bridges continued.</a> &#8220;Thank you for your prayers and well wishes. And, while I have you, please remember to go vote. Because we are all in this together.&#8221;</p>
<p>Several celebrities chimed in to offer Bridges messages of support, like George Takei, <a href="https://twitter.com/GeorgeTakei/status/1318350055581966336" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">who wrote</a>, &#8220;You&#8217;re a fighter. You can beat this.&#8221; &#8220;Sending you and your family love and healing,&#8221; <a href="https://twitter.com/PattyArquette/status/1318350724011597824" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Patricia Arquette added.</a></p>
<p>Non-Hodgkin lymphoma made up just over four percent of all new cancer cases in 2020, <a href="https://seer.cancer.gov/statfacts/html/nhl.html" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">according to the National Cancer Institute</a>, which adds that the disease has a 5-year relative survival rate of nearly 73 percent.</p>
