<div><p>Face masks are now compulsory in shops in England, but for those who aren&#8217;t keen on the traditional medical mask, this new rule presents a conundrum. Be it down to the logistics and comfort factor (ear ties are tricky with <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/beauty/hair/how-wear-hair-face-mask-best-style-up-down/">long hair</a>, a mask is far from ideal if you're wearing make-up, and the&#160;inevitable steamy lenses are a pain&#160;for <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/fashion/style/how-wear-face-mask-glasses-same-time-stop-mist-fogging-up/">glasses-wearers</a>), or due to the restrictiveness of a structured mask, many people aren&#8217;t keen on wearing them at all, let alone while doing their whole weekly shop.&#160;</p>
<p>However any face covering is better than none at all, and from Friday 24 July, a face covering is a must for your local supermarket shop or trip to a shopping centre. 'Together with other social distancing measures, face coverings can make shoppers feel even more confident about returning to the High Street,' noted Health Secretary Matt Hancock.</p>
<p>Rather than relying on holding a&#160;tissue&#160;over your mouth, which offers questionable protection, or indeed forgoing any covering at all and risking a &#163;100 fine, look to these stylish, safer options instead, all of which constitute as face coverings.&#160;</p>
