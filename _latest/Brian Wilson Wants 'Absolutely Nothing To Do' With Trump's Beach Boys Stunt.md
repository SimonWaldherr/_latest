  Brian Wilson Wants 'Absolutely Nothing To Do' With Trump's Beach Boys Stunt

<div><p><a href="https://www.huffpost.com/topic/the-beach-boys" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Beach Boys</a> co-founders <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/entry/brian-wilson-love-and-mercy_n_5ed9bcecc5b64f697a47370d" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Brian Wilson</a> and Al Jardine want fans to know they&#8217;re not part of a fundraising effort for President <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Donald Trump</a> featuring the current incarnation of the band.&#160;&#160;</p><p>&#8220;We have absolutely nothing to do with the Trump benefit today in Newport Beach. Zero,&#8221; <a href="https://variety.com/2020/music/news/brian-wilson-al-jardine-trump-benefit-beach-boys-disavow-california-mike-love-1234808907/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">a spokesperson for the duo told Variety</a>. &#8220;We didn&#8217;t even know about it and were very surprised to read about it in the Los Angeles Times.&#8221;</p><p>The Beach Boys performed Sunday at a Trump fundraiser in Newport Beach, California, with tickets costing up to $150,000 per couple, <a href="https://www.latimes.com/politics/story/2020-10-18/trump-fundraiser-california" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">the Times reported</a>.</p><p>However, that&#8217;s the current touring version of the group led by <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/topic/mike-love" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Mike Love</a> &#8213; the band&#8217;s other surviving co-founder, who is often at loggerheads with his old bandmates and is a Trump supporter.</p><p>Earlier this year, Wilson and Jardine slammed Love&#8217;s version of the band for <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/entry/brian-wilson-beach-boys-trophy-hunting-convention_n_5e38e731c5b6ed0033ab8269" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">performing at a trophy-hunting convention</a>, and said they supported a petition opposed to the activity.&#160;</p><p>The Beach Boys were founded in 1961 by Wilson, Jardine, Love and Wilson&#8217;s two brothers, Dennis and Carl (who died in 1983 and 1998, respectively).&#160;</p><p>The five co-founders were <a href="https://www.rockhall.com/inductees/beach-boys" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame</a> in 1988.</p><p>The touring band has had a range of lineup changes, but for much of the modern era, Love has fronted a licensed touring version of The Beach Boys. Wilson &#8213; the mastermind behind the group&#8217;s iconic sound and its biggest hits &#8213; has toured under his own name, with Jardine in his band in recent years.</p><p>However, a reunited version of the band featuring Wilson, Love and Jardine toured in 2012.&#160;</p><p class="video-flip youtube" id="youtube-flip-gx5PVjsRamk"></p><p id="inline-newsletter_placeholder"></p>
