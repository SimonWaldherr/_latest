<p>Jeff Bezos, the world's richest person, is worth $190 billion, according to <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/billionaires/">Bloomberg's Billionaires Index.</a></p><p>In August, the Amazon CEO became the <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/amazon-ceo-jeff-bezos-net-worth-passes-200-billion-2020-8">first person in history to be worth $200 billion</a> as Amazon sales surged during the coronavirus pandemic, but his net worth has since dipped back down from that peak.</p><p>Though Bezos' <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2019/04/11/tech/jeff-bezos-pay/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">annual salary is only $81,840</a>, most of his wealth <a href="https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/stocks/how-amazon-stock-jeff-bezos-owns-after-divorce-2019-4-1028087921">comes from his Amazon shares</a>.&#160;His fortune has become so vast that it can be compared to the British monarchy and the GDP of entire countries.&#160;</p><p>Read on for a look at how Bezos' wealth stacks up against a typical American worker, the Queen of England, and the oil-rich country of Qatar.&#160;</p><h2>Bezos makes more money per second than the typical US worker makes in a week</h2><p>Since mid-October 2019, Bezos' fortune has grown by $80 billion. Based on the year-over-year change in his net worth, Bezos has made $152,207 per minute &#8212; and $2,537 per second. That latter figure is more than three times what the median US worker makes in a week.</p><p>That means that in just one minute, the Amazon chief makes well over three times what the median US worker makes in a year: about $41,000, according to <a href="https://www.census.gov/data/tables/2020/demo/income-poverty/p60-270.html">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a> data.</p><p>

Bezos makes about $2.2 million in 15 minutes.</p><p>Bezos, who has <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/jeff-bezos-moment-in-college-he-realized-would-not-be-theoretical-physicist-2018-9" target="_blank" rel="noopener">a bachelor's degree from Princeton University</a>, makes $152,207 every minute, according to Business Insider's calculations.</p><h2>Bezos remained the world's richest person even after a 2019 divorce in which he gave up 25% of the Amazon stock the couple owned</h2><p>After the couple's divorce, Bezos' ex-wife, MacKenzie Scott, is the third-richest woman in the world, after L'Oreal heiress <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/all-about-franoise-bettencourt-meyer-heir-to-the-79-billion-loreal-fortune-2017-9" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Francoise Bettencourt Meyers,</a> and Walmart heiress <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/walmart-alice-walton-wealthiest-woman-spends-fortune-2017-9" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Alice Walton.</a></p><p><a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/mackenzie-bezos-amazon-stake-sales-2019-4" target="_blank" rel="noopener">She kept a 4% stake in Amazon</a>, which now makes her <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/mackenzie-bezos-amazon-career-wealthiest-richest-people-women-world-princeton-2019-4" target="_blank" rel="noopener">the third-richest woman in the world</a>.</p><p>
