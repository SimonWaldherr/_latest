<div><p>But they did it without any help from <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/democratic-party">Democrats</a>, <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/entry/senate-democrats-boycott-amy-coney-barrett-vote_n_5f9090cdc5b66d4a0dbba165?7up" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">all of whom boycotted the hearing</a> in protest of what they say is an illegitimate process being used to jam through President <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Donald Trump</a>&#8217;s nominee before the Nov. 3 election.</p><p>&#8220;This has been a sham process from the beginning,&#8221; Democrats on the committee said in a Wednesday night statement with Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer (D-N.Y.). &#8220;Amidst a global pandemic and ongoing election, Republicans are rushing to confirm a Supreme Court Justice to take away health care from millions and execute the extreme and deeply unpopular agenda that they&#8217;ve been unable to get through Congress.&#8221;</p><p>While Republicans were voting on Barrett&#8217;s nomination, Democrats were gathering outside on the steps of the Senate for a press conference highlighting the threat Barrett poses to health care, reproductive rights, voting rights and other issues. They didn&#8217;t leave their seats empty back in the hearing room: They filled them with poster-sized photos of people who would be hurt by Barrett potentially casting a deciding vote against the Affordable Care Act.</p><p>Democrats have never had much of a chance of stopping Barrett&#8217;s nomination. They simply don&#8217;t have the votes. But progressives have been pressuring them to put up a big fight, and on Thursday they chalked up a small victory with the Democrats&#8217; boycott.</p><p>&#8220;We will not grant this process any further legitimacy by participating in a committee markup of this nomination just twelve days before the culmination of an election that is already underway,&#8221; reads the Democrats&#8217; statement.</p><p>Barrett is on track to get her confirmation vote on Monday.</p><p>This is a developing story.
