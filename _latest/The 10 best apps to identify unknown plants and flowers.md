<div><p>There are hundreds of thousands of different plant&#160;and flower varieties&#160;in the world, meaning it takes a special kind of knowledge to tell your Dracula simia (monkey face orchid) from your Rafflesia keithii (corpse flower).</p>
<p>Luckily, modern&#160;technology has the ability to retain and process databases more comprehensive than any individual could manage alone. With every&#160;plant&#160;and flower you've ever heard of searchable within seconds, plant identifier apps turn your smartphone from handy to horticulturalist in just a few clicks.</p>
<p>So, if you often find yourself wondering about the wild wonders decorating the world around you, here is our list of the best plant identifying apps currently available:</p>
<h3>1. PlantSnap</h3>
<p>The PlantSnap&#160;app makes identifying and buying a doddle.&#160;First take a photograph of the plant, then the app will do its best to recognise it.&#160; Once recognised,&#160;it will give details of name, care information and even where/when it must be planted. Here's the clever bit... once you've found out what your mystery plant is, you can then buy it through the app from one of their HTA certified nurseries.&#160; The&#160;<a href="https://www.plantsnap.com/" rel="nofollow">PlantSnap</a>&#160;app <a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.fws.plantsnap2" rel="nofollow">is available from Android</a> and <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/app/plantsnap-plant-identification/id1451054346?ls=1&amp;mt=8" rel="nofollow">Apple</a>.</p>
<h3>2. FlowerChecker</h3>
<p>The FlowerChecker app uses real botanists to identify unknown plants, moss, fungus and even lichen, perhaps therefore making it the most accurate of the apps. Available from the&#160;<a href="https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/flowerchecker-plant-identify/id916709270?mt=8" rel="nofollow">App Store&#160;</a>and for&#160;<a href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=cz.thran.flowerchecker&amp;hl=en" rel="nofollow">Android</a>.</p>
