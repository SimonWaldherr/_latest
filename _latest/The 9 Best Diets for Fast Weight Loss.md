<div><p size="3" class="Heading__HeadingStyled-sc-1w5xk2o-0-p gPPEYz Heading-sc-1w5xk2o-1 jFucEe">No. 3 (tie) Keto Diet</p><p>Butter, bacon, burgers, oh my! Meals consisting of high-fat foods like these are hallmarks of the <a href="https://health.usnews.com/best-diet/keto-diet/reviews">keto diet</a>, an extremely low-carb, high-fat plan that aims to send you into ketosis, a state in which your body is forced to burn fat, not sugar, for energy. Though quick weight loss is indeed likely on the keto diet, <a href="https://health.usnews.com/health-news/blogs/eat-run/articles/2018-01-25/no-one-diet-is-right-for-everyone-but-the-ketogenic-diet-is-wrong">experts don't recommend it</a> for most people since it lacks nutritional variety and is unsustainable long term. As one expert puts it: "It is a rigid diet, it is not environmentally friendly and we don't have epidemiological evidence of populations eating this way." In other words: Follow at your own risk. </p></div>
