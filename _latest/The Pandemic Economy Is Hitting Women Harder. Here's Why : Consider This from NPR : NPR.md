<p>Women are dropping out of the workforce in much higher numbers than men. <a href="https://www.epi.org/people/valerie-wilson/">Valerie Wilson</a> of the Economic Policy Institute explains that women are overrepresented in jobs that have been hit hardest by the pandemic and child care has gotten harder to come by. <br><br>The situation is especially dire for Latina women, as NPR's Brianna Scott reports. Last month, out of 865,000 women who left the workforce, more than 300,000 were Latina. <br><br><a href="https://lbj.utexas.edu/soto-victoria-m-defrancesco">Victoria de Francesco Soto</a> of the University of Texas at Austin explains why it's not just the pandemic economy hurting women.
