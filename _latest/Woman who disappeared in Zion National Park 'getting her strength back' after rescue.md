<div><p class="endmarkEnabled">For nearly two weeks, Holly Suzanne Courtier was stranded somewhere in Utah's 229-square-mile Zion National Park. She was weak, dehydrated, hungry and alone, according to her daughter &#8212; unable to cry for help or even get on her feet.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">But on Sunday, in a stunning turn of events, <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/woman-who-vanished-zion-national-park-nearly-two-weeks-ago-n1243853" target="_blank">Courtier was discovered </a>and reunited with her family, <a href="https://www.nps.gov/zion/learn/news/missing-person-found.htm" target="_blank">according to the National Park Service</a>.</p><p></p><p id="taboolaReadMoreBelow"></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The circumstances of Courtier's disappearance and ordeal were still largely unclear as of Monday afternoon. But in a statement, Courtier's daughter filled in some of the gaps, describing an agonizing and brutal experience.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"Early in her trip into Zion, she injured her head on a tree," Kailey Chambers said of her mother. "She was very disoriented as a result and thankfully ended up near a water source &#8212; a river bed. She thought her best chance of survival was to stay next to a water source."</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Chambers said that her mother was "too weak and disoriented to actively seek out help," and that she was "without food" for the 12 days she was lost in Zion.</p><p class="lazyload-placeholder"></p><p class="caption__container">Holly Suzanne Courtier, who went missing in Zion National Park.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"She was unable to take more than a step or two without collapsing," Chambers said. "She told me she was so dehydrated she couldn't open her mouth."</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Chambers said thsat her mother's recovery was the family's "top priority right now," and that her mother was "getting her strength back and hydrating."</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"She has been getting fluids because she was extremely dehydrated, and we are slowly introducing foods," Chambers said.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Chambers thanked the public for their support and expressed thanks to "everyone who helped find her."</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Additional information about how Courtier vanished and survived, or where she was found, was not immediately available.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Courtier, 38, was found in the park after a visitor saw her and alerted rangers, the National Park Service said in a statement Sunday.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Courtier, who lives in Los Angeles, disappeared after a private shuttle dropped her off on Oct. 6 at the Grotto park area inside the national park. She was scheduled to be picked up later that afternoon but didn't show up, park officials said.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The search included K-9 units and federal, state and local rescue teams, as well as volunteers.</p></div>
