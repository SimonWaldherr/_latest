<div><p>Ghislaine Maxwell has lost her legal battle to keep sworn depositions giving intimate details about her personal life under wraps.</p>
<p>A federal appeals court <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2020/10/13/judge-make-final-ruling-whether-unseal-embarrassing-trance-documents/">upheld a decision from a lower court </a>that the testimony given by the 58-year-old socialite and associate of disgraced financier Jeffrey Epstein should be made public.</p>
<p>Maxwell is currently being held at a Brooklyn prison awaiting trial on charges that she helped Epstein recruit underage girls as young as 14 to engage in sexual acts in the mid-1990s.</p>
<p>She has pleaded not guilty.</p>
<p>The documents relate to a deposition she gave in 2016 as part of a civil defamation case brought by one of Epstein's victims, Virginia Roberts Giuffre.</p>
<p><a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/royal-family/2020/05/27/prince-andrew-told-17-year-old-accuser-had-daughter-little-bit/">Ms Giuffre alleges Epstein forced her to have sex with Prince Andrew</a> and others when she was only 17. The Duke of York has denied the allegations.</p>
