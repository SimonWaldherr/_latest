  Wedding for rabbi's grandson where 10,000 people were expected is scaled down to 'virtual event' 
description: > 
  AdvertisementThe vastly scaled-down Hasidic wedding for a New York grand rabbi's grandson has taken place - after Governor Andrew Cuomo banned plans for a lavish event which was expected to attract 10,000 people.But the the family agreed to hold a scaled-down service on Monday and do the rest virtually, according to ABC7.'We don't want to disrespect anybody, people are allowed to gather but within reason,' NYPD Assistant Chief Judith Harrison said.Cuomo announced Saturday that the wedding had been banned following a tip-off, as the governor celebrated progress being made to reduce virus hotspots.You just can't have 10,000 people at your wedding,' said Cuomo.
