</p><p><strong>Ireland will close most shops and amenities and impose a 5km (3 mile) travel limit</strong>, some of the severest Covid-19 restrictions in Europe, to curb surging infection rates.</p>
<p>The government <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/live/2020/oct/19/coronavirus-live-news-global-infections-near-40m-as-us-covid-cases-rise-in-all-but-two-states?page=with:block-5f8ddea48f08176e64a8d597#block-5f8ddea48f08176e64a8d597" class="u-underline">has announced</a> that from midnight on Wednesday the country will move to its highest lockdown tier for six weeks. </p>
<p>Visits to private homes will not be permitted and there are to be no gatherings except for tightly controlled weddings and funerals.</p>
<p>A graduated fine system for those who breach the 5km travel limit will be announced later this week, as will new guidelines on social bubbles. Two households can meet outdoors within the travel limit.</p>
<p>Non-essential retail will close along with barbers&#8217; shops, beauty salons, gyms, leisure centres and cultural amenities. Pubs, cafes and restaurants will be allowed to serve takeout meals only.</p>
<p>Schools and creches are to remain open, but teachers&#8217; unions signalled they may challenge this, citing their members&#8217; safety. Martin Marjoram, the president of the Teachers&#8217; Union of Ireland, said his members were anxious and more rapid coronavirus testing was needed for schools to remain open.</p>
