<div><p>Flybe&#160;could start flying once more early next year after administrators sold the collapsed airline's brand to a former shareholder.</p>
<p>Thyme Opco, a&#160;company controlled by hedge fund executive Lucien Farrell, will buy the regional airline&#160;out of bankruptcy protection, including its intellectual property, stock and equipment.</p>
<p>The new owner said it will work with the Civil Aviation Authority to revive the airline.</p>
<p>A spokesman for the firm said: "While we plan to start off smaller than before, we expect to create valuable airline industry jobs, restore essential regional connectivity in the UK and contribute to the recovery of a vital part of the country&#8217;s economy."</p>
<p>Flybe collapsed at the beginning of the pandemic in February after a government rescue bid fell apart, with ministers rejecting&#160;a request for a &#163;100m state loan following seven weeks of talks.&#160;</p>
