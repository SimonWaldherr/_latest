<div><p>A 7.5 magnitude earthquake struck off the Alaska Peninsula on Monday afternoon, triggering a <a href="https://twitter.com/NWS/status/1318314056198115330" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">tsunami warning</a> in the region, according to officials. There were no immediate reports of damage.</p>

    
<p id="mpu-plus-top-right-rail" class="ad-mpu-plus-top-right-rail ad-wrapper ">
    </p>
<p>The U.S. Geological Survey said the quake <a href="https://earthquake.usgs.gov/earthquakes/eventpage/us6000c9hg/executive" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">occurred</a> at 1:54 p.m. PT some 56 miles southeast of Sand Hill and nearly 600 miles southwest of Anchorage. The earthquake had a depth of about 25 miles. Several larger aftershocks were also recorded at magnitudes 5.8, 5.7, 5.2 and 5.5.</p><p>Emergency sirens were activated in Alaska and the Kodiak Emergency Operations Center is monitoring, CBS affiliate KTVA-TV <a href="https://www.alaskasnewssource.com/2020/10/19/tsunami-warning-issued-after-magnitude-74-earthquake-strikes-near-sand-point/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">reported</a>.
