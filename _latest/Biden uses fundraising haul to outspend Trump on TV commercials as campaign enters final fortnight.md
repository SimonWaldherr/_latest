<div><p><a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/joe-biden/">Joe Biden</a> has out-raised and is outspending <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/donald-trump/">Donald Trump</a> by a significant margin, the latest election funding totals show, as Trump campaign insiders raise questions about their strategy with two weeks until the vote.</p>
<p>Mr Biden raked in $383 million dollars in September, breaking the record for the largest ever monthly haul <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2020/09/21/biden-takes-huge-cash-lead-trump-outspending-republicans-us/">set by his own campaign</a> the month before. Mr Trump drew in $135 million less.&#160;</p>
<p>The disparities in spending have exacerbated anxiety inside the Trump campaign with Mr Biden leading nationally by more than 10 points according to the latest average of presidential&#160;polls - on 52.4 per cent, to 41.9 per cent for Mr Trump.&#160;</p>
<p>The huge amounts of cash flowing into the Biden campaign have enabled the 77-year-old to blitz key swing states.</p>
<p>In Michigan, Pennsylvania and Wisconsin Mr Biden spent about $53 million to Mr Trump&#8217;s $17 million over the past month,&#160;with adverts largely attacking the president&#8217;s handling of the coronavirus pandemic.&#160;</p>
