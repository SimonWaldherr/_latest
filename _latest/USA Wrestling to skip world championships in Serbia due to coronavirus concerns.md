<div><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">USA Wrestling <a href="https://www.teamusa.org/USA-Wrestling/Features/2020/October/20/USA-Wrestling-decision-regarding-World-Championships" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">announced</a> Tuesday night that it will not send a team to this year's senior world championships in Serbia in December, bowing out of one of the sport's signature events due to concerns stemming from the coronavirus pandemic.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">USA Wrestling president Bruce Baumgartner said in a statement that the national governing body's executive committee reviewed data regarding COVID-19 and solicited athlete input before deciding that "it would not be in the best interest of all involved" to compete.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The decision also prevents American wrestlers from competing independently, a possibility in which some athletes had expressed interest.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The world championships are slated to begin Dec. 12 in Belgrade, Serbia.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"My heart breaks for our athletes, as nobody is more affected by this decision than they are," said Veronica Carlson, the chairperson of USA Wrestling's athlete advisory committee. "In the same breath, abstaining from the 2020 World Championships is the right decision. ... In choosing to make this decision now, versus delaying it, the athletes have time to recover and refocus on what is most important &#8212;&#160;the 2021 Olympic Games."</p><img class="gnt_em_img_i" alt="Jordan Burroughs celebrates at the 2017 Wrestling World Cup in Paris."><p class="gnt_em_img_ccw gnt_em_img_ccw__cap gnt_em_img_ccw__crd"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">While USA Wrestling hosted a national competition in Iowa earlier this month, the organization said an international competition poses different challenges. The organization said in a news release that travel, potential quarantine situations and uncertainty surrounding health care were among its chief concerns.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">This is not the first time USA Wrestling has skipped the world championships due to safety concerns. In 2002, it did not send a team to the Senior World Freestyle Championships in Iran after being informed by the U.S. government of a possible threat to the team's safety during the trip.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Contact Tom Schad at tschad@usatoday.com or on Twitter <a href="https://twitter.com/Tom_Schad" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">@Tom_Schad.</a></p><a class="gnt_em gnt_em__lp gnt_em_gl gnt_em__el" href="/picture-gallery/sports/gallery/2020/03/12/sports-world-hit-cancellations-coronavirus-continues-spread/5038256002/"><img class="gnt_em_gl_i" alt="" width="660" height="372"><p class="gnt_em_gl_i_nt"></p><p class="gnt_em_gl_vg"></p><p class="gnt_em_t gnt_em_t__hb"></p></a></div>
