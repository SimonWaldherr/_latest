<div><p>During the debate, there were testy exchanges about the future of fracking - the controversial technique used for extracting shale gas from underground.</p><p>Vice-President Pence said the Democrats "want to ban fracking, which would cost hundreds of thousands of American jobs".</p><p>His opponent replied: "Joe Biden will not end fracking."</p><p>Pence then claimed that Harris had "said on multiple occasions, when you were running for president, that you would ban fracking."</p><p>So what are the facts?</p><p>In August 2020, Biden said "I am not banning fracking. Let me say that again: I am not banning fracking."</p><p>However, in March 2020, during a Democratic debate, he said &#8220;No more &#8212; no new fracking.&#8221;</p><p>At the time the campaign team clarified Biden&#8217;s position: a pledge to ban &#8220;new oil and gas permitting on public lands and waters.&#8221; They said this would allow existing fracking to continue. </p><p>Meanwhile, at a CNN town hall event in September 2019, Harris said: "There&#8217;s no question, I&#8217;m in favour of banning fracking."</p></div>
