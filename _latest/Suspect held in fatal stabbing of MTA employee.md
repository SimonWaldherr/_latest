<div><p>A woman was arrested Monday on suspicion of fatally stabbing a Los Angeles County Metropolitan Authority worker late last week.</p><p>Irma Monroy, 25, of Los Angeles, is being held on $2.06 million bail. She is scheduled to be arraigned Oct. 28 on the murder charge along with a previous felony warrant for assault with a deadly weapon.</p><p>Monroy is alleged to have <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2020-10-18/la-metro-employee-fatal-stabbing-7th-street-downtown" target="_blank">stabbed to death an MTA worker</a> at the 7th Street/Metro Center subway station, which serves as a busy hub for the Red, Purple and Expo lines. The incident took place about 10:45 p.m. Friday after a &#8220;verbal dispute&#8221; on the Red Line, according to the Los Angeles Police Department.</p><p>The male victim was taken to L.A. County-USC Medical Center, where he died less than an hour later, Metro spokesman Rick Jager said Sunday.</p><p class="google-dfp-ad-caption">Advertisement</p>
