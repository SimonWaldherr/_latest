<div><p>Writer Jeffrey Toobin has been suspended by The New Yorker &#8212; because he exposed himself on a Zoom call between magazine colleagues and WNYC workers, a report said Monday.</p>
<p>Toobin told Vice in a statement: &#8220;I made an embarrassingly stupid mistake, believing I was off-camera. I apologize to my wife, family, friends and co-workers.&#8221;</p>
<p>A spokeswoman for the magazine said, &#8220;Jeffrey Toobin has been suspended while we investigate the matter.&#8221;</p>
<p>Sources told the Web site that the suspension stemmed from Toobin &#8212; a Harvard Law School grad who also serves as CNN&#8217;s chief legal analyst &#8212; showing his privates during the online meeting.</p>
<p>Toobin is a staff writer at The New Yorker and famously covered the OJ Simpson trial for the mag in the 1990s.</p>
<p>He turned his Simpson overage into a book, which fueled FX&#8217;s &#8220;The People vs. O.J. Simpson: American Crime Story&#8217;&#8217; in 2016.</p>
<p>He is a prolific author who has written about everything from the US Supreme Court to presidents to <a href="https://pagesix.com/2015/03/27/patty-hearst-lashes-out-at-biographer/">onetime kidnapped heiress Patty Hearst.&#160;</a></p>
