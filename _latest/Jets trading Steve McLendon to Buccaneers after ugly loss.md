<div><p>The Jets&#8217; fire sale appears to be underway.</p>
<p>Following <a href="https://nypost.com/2020/10/18/jets-look-completely-inept-in-shutout-loss-to-dolphins/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">a 24-0 loss to Miami</a> on Sunday, the 0-6 Jets traded nose tackle Steve McLendon and a 2023 seventh-round draft pick to the Tampa Bay Buccaneers for a 2022 sixth-round pick, The Post&#8217;s Brian Costello confirmed.</p>
<p>The Tampa Bay Times was the first to report the deal.</p>
<p>McLendon, 34, was in his fifth season with the Jets and had been a staple on the defensive line.</p>
<p>He was also the longest-tenured Jet and considered a leader in the locker room. He is slated to become a free agent at season&#8217;s end, having signed a one-year deal with the Jets worth up to $3 million ($1.65 million guaranteed) last offseason.</p>
