<div><p>Former ABC network executive Channing Dungey will take over as chairwoman of Warner Bros. Television Group, the AT&amp;T unit said Monday, replacing veteran television executive Peter Roth, who will step down next year.</p>
<p>Dungey, who is currently vice president of Original Series at Netflix, has previously worked at Walt Disney&#8217;s ABC network for over 14 years, according to her LinkedIn profile.</p>
<p>In 2016, Dungey was promoted to president of entertainment at ABC, becoming the first black person to run entertainment programming at a major US broadcast network.</p>
<p>Dungey will start her tenure early next year and will report to Ann Sarnoff, chief executive of Warner Media Studios and Networks Group.</p>
<p>Warner Bros. TV said last week that Roth would step down from his role in 2021, after having worked at the entertainment company for 22 years and overseen the development of several hit shows including &#8220;The West Wing,&#8221; &#8220;The Big Bang Theory&#8221; and &#8220;Two and a Half Men.&#8221;</p>
