                <p class="news-vj-component-p--bold">The US president has a huge influence on people's lives both at home and abroad, so when the next election is held on 3 November, the outcome will matter to everyone.</p>
                <p class="">The US political system is dominated by just two parties, so the president always belongs to one of them. </p>
                <p>The <a href="#expander-2e5feb90-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb90-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Republicans</a> are the conservative political party in the US and their candidate in this year&#8217;s election is President Donald Trump, who is hoping to secure another four years in power.</p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb90-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip Republicans</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb90-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">The Republican Party is also known as the GOP, or the Grand Old Party. In recent years, it has stood for lower taxes, gun rights and tighter restrictions on immigration. Support for the party tends to be stronger in more rural parts of America. Former Republican presidents include George W Bush, Ronald Reagan and Richard Nixon.</p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb90-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of Republicans</p>
                
                <p>The <a href="#expander-2e5feb91-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb91-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Democrats</a> are the liberal political party in the US and their candidate is Joe Biden, an experienced politician best-known for serving as Barack Obama&#8217;s vice president for eight years. </p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb91-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip Democrats</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb91-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">The Democratic Party is best defined by its liberal stances on issues like civil rights, immigration, and climate change. It believes government should play a bigger role in people&#8217;s lives, like providing health insurance. Support for the party tends to be stronger in urban parts of America. Former Democratic presidents include John F Kennedy and Barack Obama. </p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb91-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of Democrats</p>
                
                <p class="">Both men are in their 70s - Mr Trump would be 74 years old at the start of his second term, while at 78, Mr Biden would be the oldest first-term president in history. </p>
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/155CD/production/_113810578_us2020_separator-nc.png" alt="Section divider">  
                <h2>How is the winner decided?</h2>
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/BB38/production/_114682974_index-graphic_name_640-2_2x-nc.png" alt="Section image showing map and pin badges">  
                <p>Both candidates compete to win <a href="#expander-2e5feb92-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb92-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">electoral college</a> votes. </p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb92-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip electoral college</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb92-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">Like most voting systems, the electoral college has a number of pros and cons but it is widely respected because of its historical roots in the founding of the US. It usually reflects the popular vote, but it has failed to in two of the last five elections, including when Donald Trump won in 2016. </p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb92-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of electoral college</p>
                
                <p class="">Each state gets a certain number of electoral college votes partly based on its population and there are a total of 538 up for grabs, so the winner is the candidate that wins 270 or more. </p>
                <p>This means voters decide state-level contests rather than the national one, which is why it&#8217;s possible for a candidate to win the most votes nationally - like <a href="#expander-2e5feb93-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb93-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Hillary Clinton</a> did in 2016 - but still be defeated by the electoral college.</p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb93-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip Hillary Clinton</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb93-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">Hillary Clinton was Donald Trump&#8217;s opponent in 2016. She ended up winning nearly three million more votes than Mr Trump, mainly because she picked up a high number of votes in strong Democrat states like New York and California. However, her rival comfortably beat her in the electoral college race 304 to 227 because he won several tight contests in key states. </p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb93-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of Hillary Clinton</p>
                
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/E0B1/production/_113912575_us_electoral_colleges_map_v3_640-3x-nc.png" alt="Graphic: A map of the US showing how many electoral college votes are allocated to each state">  
                <p class="">All but two states have a winner-takes-all rule, so whichever candidate wins the highest number of votes is awarded all of the state&#8217;s electoral college votes. </p>
                <p>Most states lean heavily towards one party or the other, so the focus is usually on a dozen or so states where either of them could win. These are known as the <a href="#expander-2e5feb94-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb94-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">battleground states</a>. </p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb94-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip battleground states</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb94-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">These states, which are often referred to as swing states as well, are where voters are split relatively evenly between Democrats and Republicans. Traditional battleground states include Florida and Ohio. Other states that have been strongly Republican in the past, like Arizona and Texas, are considered battlegrounds in 2020 due to increasing support for the Democratic Party.</p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb94-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of battleground states</p>
                
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/155CD/production/_113810578_us2020_separator-nc.png" alt="Section divider">  
                <h2>Who can vote and how do they do it?</h2>
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/E248/production/_114682975_index-graphic_name_640-3_2x-nc.png" alt="Section image showing ballots and ballot boxes">  
                <p>If you&#8217;re a US citizen and you&#8217;re 18 or over, you should be <a href="#expander-2e5feb95-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb95-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">eligible to vote</a> in the presidential election, which takes place every four years. </p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb95-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip eligible to vote</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb95-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">In 2016, about 245 million people were eligible to vote, but fewer than 140 million people actually did. According to the US Census Bureau, the majority of people who didn&#8217;t register to vote said they just weren&#8217;t interested in politics. Those who registered but didn&#8217;t actually vote said they didn&#8217;t like the candidates.</p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb95-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of eligible to vote</p>
                
                <p class="">However, lots of states have passed laws requiring voters to show identification documents to prove who they are before they can vote. </p>
                <p class="">These laws are often put into place by Republicans who say they&#8217;re needed to guard against voter fraud. But Democrats accuse them of using this as a form of voter suppression as it is often poorer, minority voters who are unable to provide ID like a driving licence.</p>
                <p>How people vote is a contentious issue this year because of the coronavirus pandemic. Some politicians are calling for wider use of postal ballots, but President Trump has said - with very little evidence - that this could result in more <a href="#expander-2e5feb96-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb96-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">voter fraud</a>.</p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb96-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip voter fraud</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb96-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">Critics say postal ballots are susceptible to fraud - and there have been isolated incidents involving Republicans and Democrats - but numerous nationwide and state-level studies have found no evidence of any widespread fraud.</p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb96-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of voter fraud</p>
                
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/155CD/production/_113810578_us2020_separator-nc.png" alt="Section divider">  
                <h2>Is the election just about who is president?</h2>
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/13068/production/_114682977_index-graphic_name_640-5_2x-nc.png" alt="Section image showing the Capitol Hill building">  
                <p>No. All of the attention will be on Trump v Biden, but voters will also be choosing new members of <a href="#expander-2e5feb97-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb97-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Congress</a> when they fill in their ballots. </p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb97-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip Congress</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb97-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">Congress is the part of the US government that writes and passes laws. It's made up of two chambers - the House of Representatives and the Senate. Members of the House serve two-year terms while senators serve six-year terms and are split into three groups, meaning a third of them are up for election every two years.</p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb97-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of Congress</p>
                
                <p class="">Democrats already have control of the House so they will be looking to keep hold of that while also gaining control of the Senate. </p>
                <p class="">If they had a majority in both chambers they would be able to block or delay President Trump&#8217;s plans if he were to be re-elected. </p>
                <p class="">All 435 seats in the House are up for election this year, while 33 Senate seats are also up for grabs. </p>
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/155CD/production/_113810578_us2020_separator-nc.png" alt="Section divider">  
                <h2>When will we find out the result?</h2>
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/9428/production/_114682973_index-graphic_name_640-1_2x-nc.png" alt="Section image showing a clock and a calendar">  
                <p class="">It can take several days for every vote to be counted, but it&#8217;s usually pretty clear who the winner is by the early hours of the following morning. </p>
                <p class="">In 2016, Donald Trump took to the stage in New York at about 3am to give his victory speech in front of a crowd of jubilant supporters. </p>
                <p>But don&#8217;t set your alarm clocks just yet. Officials are already warning that we may have to wait longer - possibly days, even weeks - for the result this year because of the expected surge in <a href="#expander-2e5feb98-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb98-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">postal ballots</a>.</p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb98-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip postal ballots</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb98-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">There are two ways to vote in the US - by going to a polling station on election day or by using a postal ballot - but the rules vary depending on which state you are in. All states offer some form of voting by post, but many require you to provide a reason for not being able to vote in person on election day. Some states are accepting the coronavirus as a valid reason to use a postal ballot, but not all of them are at the moment. </p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb98-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of postal ballots</p>
                
                <p>The last time the result wasn&#8217;t clear within a few hours was in 2000, when the winner wasn&#8217;t confirmed until <a href="#expander-2e5feb99-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content" class="expander-button" id="expander-button--2e5feb99-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">a Supreme Court</a> ruling was made a month later. </p>
                
                    <a class="offscreen offscreen--appear-on-focus skip-link" href="#end-of-2e5feb99-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">Skip a Supreme Court</a>
                    <p tabindex="-1" id="expander-2e5feb99-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb-content">In 2000, the race between Republican candidate George W Bush and Democratic candidate Al Gore was so close that it came down to the result in Florida, where just a few hundred votes separated them. There were weeks of legal battles, which triggered a recount, but eventually the Supreme Court stepped in. Mr Bush was declared the winner, giving him all 25 of Florida&#8217;s electoral college votes and leaving him with a winning total of 271 overall. Mr Gore, who won more votes nationally than Mr Bush, conceded defeat.</p>
                <p tabindex="-1" class="offscreen skip-link" id="end-of-2e5feb99-0266-11eb-869e-071bd90979bb">End of a Supreme Court</p>
                
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/155CD/production/_113810578_us2020_separator-nc.png" alt="Section divider">  
                <h2>When does the winner take office?</h2>
                <img src="https://c.files.bbci.co.uk/10958/production/_114682976_index-graphic_name_640-4_2x-nc.png" alt="Section image showing the White House">  
                <p class="">If Joe Biden wins the election, he wouldn&#8217;t immediately replace President Trump as there is a set transition period to give the new leader time to appoint cabinet ministers and make plans.</p>
                <p class="">The new president is officially sworn into office on 20 January in a ceremony known as the inauguration, which is held on the steps of the Capitol building in Washington DC. </p>
                <p class="">After the ceremony, the new president makes their way to the White House to begin their four-year term in office. </p>
