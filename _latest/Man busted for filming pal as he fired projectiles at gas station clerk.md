<div><p>Police arrested a man who filmed as his pal fired a Roman candle at a Canadian gas station employee in a terrifying scene caught on surveillance video.</p>
<p>The two men entered Petro Canada gas station in Mississauga &#8212; a large city neighboring Toronto &#8212; just after midnight July 4, <a href="https://o.canada.com/news/local-news/watch-one-sought-one-charged-in-fireworks-attack-on-mississauga-clerk" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">O.Canada.com reported</a>.</p>
<p>Video <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hf0e0v5g9dA&amp;feature=emb_logo" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">released by Peel Regional Police</a> shows the men going to the back of the store to check their hair before they walk up to the clerk &#8212; one of them lighting a Roman candle and firing multiple flaming projectiles at the cowering employee.</p>
<p>In total, 10 projectiles were fired, according to the local outlet &#8212; some at close range.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, Mohammed Mumin, 21, of Toronto, allegedly filmed the entire ordeal, according to the report.</p>
<p>Mumin was arrested Sunday, more than three months after the incident, and charged with assault with a weapon, mischief endangering life and forcible confinement, the outlet reported.</p>
 
<p>The clerk suffered minor injuries.</p>
<p>Police are still searching for the man who aimed the fireworks at the clerk.</p>
