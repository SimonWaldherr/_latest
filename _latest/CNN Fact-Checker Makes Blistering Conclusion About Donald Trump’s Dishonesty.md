<div><p><a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/cnn">CNN</a>&#8217;s fact-checking reporter <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/topic/daniel-dale">Daniel Dale</a>&#160;showed how President <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Donald Trump</a>&#8217;s &#8220;dishonesty is getting worse&#8221; in the final stretch of the 2020 election.</p><p>Dale noted in <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2020/10/19/politics/fact-check-trump-dishonest-weekend-florida-michigan-georgia-wisconsin/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">an article for the CNN website Monday</a> how Trump had told &#8220;at least 66 separate false and misleading claims&#8221; over the weekend at public events and rallies in Florida, Georgia, Wisconsin, Michigan and Nevada.</p><p>The president also repeated some of those 66 lies on multiple occasions, said Dale.</p><p>&#8220;For fact checkers, the period from Friday through Sunday was one of the most challenging of Trump&#8217;s entire presidency,&#8221; he lamented.</p><p>Trump&#8217;s dozens of untruths centered on the coronavirus pandemic, his Democratic rival Joe Biden, the economy and his own record, said Dale.</p><p>And while the president&#8217;s penchant for lying is well-known (The Washington Post in July reported that Trump <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/entry/donald-trump-lie-wont-die-cnn-fact-checker_n_5f85584dc5b62f97bac7618d" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">had told a whopping 20,000 lies</a> during his time in office), Dale said Trump&#8217;s weekend of falsehoods was &#8220;an egregious stretch for the President, no matter how much he was talking.&#8221;</p><p class="content-list-component embed-asset yr-content-list-embed"></p>
