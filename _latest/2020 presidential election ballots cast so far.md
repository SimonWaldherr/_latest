<div><p class="speakable">Many Americans are voting early in the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/2020-presidential-election" target="_blank">2020 presidential election</a> amid concerns regarding the <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/health/infectious-disease/coronavirus" target="_blank">coronavirus</a> pandemic&#160;and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/topic/usps-controversy" target="_blank">U.S. Postal Service</a> timing.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="speakable">More than 26.8&#160;million total ballots cast as of Saturday suggests a record turnout for this year's race compared to the 1.4 million&#160;ballots that were cast at a similar time in 2016, <a href="https://electproject.github.io/Early-Vote-2020G/index.html" target="_blank">according to</a> data from the&#160;United States Elections Project.</p><p>"That&#8217;s unprecedented in a modern election in the United States,"&#160;Elections Project founder and University of Florida political science professor&#160;<a href="https://sites.google.com/site/uselectionsproject/dr-mcdonald-bio">Michael McDonald</a>&#160;wrote on the project website.</p><p>He expected&#160;"around 150 million people" to&#160;vote in this year's election, the&#160;"highest turnout since 1908 of those eligible to vote."</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>That number of early ballots cast so far represents 19.4%<strong>&#160;</strong>of the total national&#160;voter turnout in 2016.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Ten states, however, have already reached more than 30% of their 2016 voter tunrout as of Saturday, including Texas (38.3%), South Dakota (32.8%), Minnesota (30.7%), Vermont (42.7%), Virginia (32.5%), Georgia (34.5%) and&#160;New Jersey (32.7%).</p><p>Texas on Saturday emerged with the largest voter turnout so far; more than 3.4 million Texans have cast ballots so far. California and Florida trail just behind with 3 million and 2.4&#160;million ballots cast, respectively.&#160;</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Swing state Michigan has&#160;seen&#160;voter turnout reached nearly 1.4&#160;million&#160;on. Virginia, North Carolina, New Jersey, and Georgia have also counted more than a million ballots each.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p><strong><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/new-jersey-federal-judge-rejects-trump-campaign-challenge-mail-in-voting-plans" target="_blank">NJ JUDGE REJECTS TRUMP CAMPAIGN'S CHALLENGE TO STATE'S MAIL-IN VOTING PLANS</a></strong></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>McDonald said he "expected some things to be different since states changed their laws" to accommodate voters amid the pandemic. McDonald added&#160;that "70 million mail-in ballots [are] expected to go out to voters" ahead of Nov. 3.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>"People did not have to take advantage of this," he said of mail-in ballots and early voting. But many people already have.</p><p>Ballot data is still unavailable in some states, including&#160;Idaho,&#160;New York, Arkansas, Missouri and Connectciut. (AP Photo/Laurence Kesterson)"><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics" target="_blank">Democrats</a> have requested 23.5&#160;million ballots while Republicans have requested&#160;13.1&#160;million &#8212; a 10.3 million ballot request lead based on data from states reporting party registration&#160;including&#160;California, Colorado, the District of Columbia, Flordia, Iowa, Maryland, Maine, North Carolina, New Jersey, Nevada, Oregon, Pennsylvania and&#160;Utah.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p><strong><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/pennsylvania-da-charges-elections-judge-altered-primary-ballots" target="_blank">PENNSYLVANIA DA FILES CHARGES AGAINST ELECTION JUDGE WHO ALLEGEDLY ALTERED PRIMARY BALLOTS</a></strong></p><p>Those ballot requests by party, however, may not be an accurate indicator of final election results, according to McDonald's frequently asked questions page on the Elections Project website.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>"Just because registered Democrats are leading Republicans in early voting, that does not mean the Republicans will not make up ground on Election Day," McDonald wrote, adding that "registered Democrats typically lead Republicans during early voting, and Republicans vote on Election Day, a pattern that persists across many states and elections."</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>McDonald shared two possible scenarios for this year's voter outcome.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>"The first is that many voters...have successfully flattened the curve on mail-in ballots, meaning election officials will be able to more accurately process ballots," he said.
