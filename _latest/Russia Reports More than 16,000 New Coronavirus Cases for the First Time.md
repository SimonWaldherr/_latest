<div><p>Russia reported another daily record of new coronavirus cases on Tuesday, surpassing 16,000 cases for the first time during the pandemic. </p><p><a href="https://xn--80aesfpebagmfblc0a.xn--p1ai/information/" target="_blank">The country's coronavirus headquarters reported</a> 16,319 new cases on Tuesday, breaking Monday's record of 15,982. Officials now report more than 1.4 million COVID-19 cases in Russia. </p><p id="ac-lre-player"></p><p>More than 321,390 of the cases are currently active. The new virus cases were reported in 84 regions across the country and 28.6% of them are asymptomatic. </p><p class="Box-w0dun1-0 SlideshowEmbed__HeadingBox-fkpjfn-1 htMIQB"><h3 size="3" class="Heading__HeadingStyled-sc-1w5xk2o-0-h3 eSzSqG Heading-sc-1w5xk2o-1 jFucEe">Cartoons on the Coronavirus</h3></p><img src="https://www.usnews.com/dims4/USNEWS/3fb7da4/2147483647/thumbnail/640x420/quality/85/?url=http%3A%2F%2Fmedia.beam.usnews.com%2Ffe%2F0f%2F85d8b892433d81019199582dbd46%2Fsbr101720dapc.jpg" class="Image__PictureImage-sc-412cjc-0 cjIqES Image-sc-412cjc-2 SlideshowEmbed__Picture-fkpjfn-0 eSVwNn"><p class="SlideshowEmbed__ArrowWrapper-fkpjfn-6 jGHfsH"></p><p>Additionally, the headquarters reported 269 deaths on Tuesday, bringing the death toll to 24,635. </p><p>According to the headquarters, more than 374,000 COVID-19 tests were conducted over the past day, bringing the total number of tests conducted to 54.6 million. </p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p>Despite the surge in cases, "a second lockdown is not being considered," the head of the Ministry of Economic Development Maxim Reshetnikov <a href="https://xn--80aesfpebagmfblc0a.xn--p1ai/news/20201020-1505.html" target="_blank">said</a>. </p><p>Mikhail Murashko, head of the Russian Ministry of Health, urged residents to minimize social activity and comply with prevention measures. </p><p>"Today there is great hope. It's so easy to prevent [a new infection], it's so simple," the minister <a href="https://xn--80aesfpebagmfblc0a.xn--p1ai/news/20201020-1324.html" target="_blank">said</a>. "You can avoid certain health problems by distancing yourself, limiting visits now during this period. We must come to an option for accessing the vaccine for everyone, but at the same time we need to gain time."</p><p>Russia may see mass vaccination of its citizens with the vaccine Sputnik V as early as next month, <a href="https://xn--80aesfpebagmfblc0a.xn--p1ai/news/20201020-1332.html" target="_blank">according to Vasily Ignatiev</a>, head of one of the country's largest pharmaceutical companies, R-Pharm.</p><p>He said that five production sites are manufacturing the vaccine and the issue of mass vaccination is on the daily agenda of discussion by Russia officials. </p></div>
