 <p>More properties could yet be evacuated, Ealing Council said.</p> <p>Shop owner Jatinder Singh, 36, said he received a phone call from a neighbour to tell him his mobile phone shop had been totally destroyed.</p> <p>He told the&#160;<a href="https://www.standard.co.uk/news/uk/southall-gas-explosion-shop-ealing-a4572398.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Evening Standard</a>: &#8220;It&#8217;s an absolute mess."</p> <p>Station Commander Alan Brand, who is at the scene, said this morning: &#8220;Four adults and one child were rescued at the rear of the property by firefighters using a ladder.</p> <p>"A further fourteen adults and two children self evacuated from surrounding properties. Specialist crews are searching the building for anyone else that may be involved."</p>

 <p><a href="http://" name="signup" rel="nofollow"></a>Londone Fire Brigade was called at 6.38am. Six fire engines and around 40 firefighters from Southall, Hayes, Heston, Ealing and Feltham fire stations were at the scene.<a href="http://" name="signup" rel="nofollow"></a></p> <p>Gliveera-Singh Purewall, 29, was asleep some 50 metres away when the blast went off.</p> <p>Speaking to <a href="https://www.mylondon.news/news/west-london-news/ealing-explosion-live-updates-firefighters-19140113" target="_blank">MyLondon</a>, the builder said: &#8220;There was a huge blast that shook the room and the whole building. I work behind the shops and the whole buildings were shaking.</p> <p>&#8220;Everyone woke up and came out of their homes. Mostly people did not know what happened and just listened to the blast.</p>

 <p>&#8220;About 15 to 20 minutes later people went home and I stayed out on the street.&#8221;</p> <p>Mr Purewall has been stood at the end of King Street for eight hours with other residents who cannot return home.</p> <p>Last night he was sleeping at the end of King Street, on site on a building he is working on.</p> <p>His home is at the other end of King Street, on Featherstone Road. He has not yet returned home since the blast.</p> <p>He added: &#8220;The whole room shook properly, stuff fell everywhere. Glasses, bottles of coke. Everything.</p> <p>&#8220;Mostly this area is full of Sikh people so we were all up already.
