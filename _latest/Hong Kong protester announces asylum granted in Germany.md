<div><p class="css-38z03z">Germany has granted refugee status to a <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/hong-kong">Hong Kong</a> pro-democracy activist facing a rioting charge in connection with the 2019 protests, the protester told Reuters on Monday.</p><p class="css-38z03z">The 22-year-old university student showed Reuters a letter from the German Federal Office for Migration and Refugees (BAMF) dated 14 October that confirmed the granting of refugee status.</p><p id="sign-in-gate"></p><p class="css-38z03z">&#8220;I chose to flee from Hong Kong because I knew I wouldn&#8217;t be given a fair trial,&#8221; the protester, who did not want to be identified, told Reuters by phone from an undisclosed location in <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/germany">Germany</a>. &#8220;I&#8217;m grateful to the German government.&#8221;</p><p class="css-38z03z">There was no immediate response from BAMF or the German consulate in Hong Kong to Reuters&#8217; requests for comment.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Hong Kong authorities have arrested more than 10,000 people in relation to the pro-democracy protest movement, which prompted Beijing to impose a sweeping national security law on 30 June.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Critics say the new law has further eroded the wide-ranging freedoms promised to the former British colony when it returned to Chinese rule in 1997.</p><p class="css-38z03z">The activist said she was arrested at a protest in November 2019. She says she fled to Germany via Taiwan several days later without telling her family.</p><p class="css-38z03z">&#8220;It felt surreal and I was very upset that I needed to leave Hong Kong like that as I knew I might never return,&#8221; she said.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Two prominent Hong Kong activists, Ray Wong and Alan Li, were also granted political asylum by Berlin in 2018. The group &#8220;Haven Assistance&#8221; run by Wong and Li issued a statement saying the student had been granted refugee status in Germany for three years. It urged Berlin to improve and broaden its asylum policies towards Hong Kong.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Some governments including Britain, Canada and Australia have offered a path to residency or citizenship for people seeking to flee Hong Kong.</p></div>
