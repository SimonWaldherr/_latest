<div><p>Veteran journalist Martin Bashir is "seriously unwell" with coronavirus-related complications, the&#160;BBC&#160;has said.</p>
<p>The 57-year-old, best <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2020/10/19/princess-diana-insisted-not-coerced-bombshell-panorama-interview/">known for his 1995 interview with Diana, Princess of Wales</a>, currently works as the&#160;BBC&#160;News religion editor.</p>
<p>A spokeswoman for the&#160;BBC&#160;said: "We are sorry to say that Martin is seriously unwell with Covid-19 related complications.</p>
<p>"Everyone at the&#160;BBC&#160;is wishing him a full recovery.</p>
<p>"We'd ask that his privacy, and that of his family, is respected at this time."</p>
<p>Bashir began working as a journalist in 1986 but made headlines around the world in 1995 for his&#160;<a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2020/10/19/princess-diana-insisted-not-coerced-bombshell-panorama-interview/">BBC&#160;interview with Diana, Princess of Wales for Panorama</a>.</p>
<p>That i<a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/women/life/diana-didnt-regret-bashir-interview-changed/">nterview has seen renewed interest</a> in the lead up to a new Channel 4 film, which examines the circumstances behind their meeting, airing on Wednesday night.</p>
<p>Bashir's other high-profile interviews have included the suspects in the Stephen Lawrence murder case, entertainer Michael Barrymore, Jeffrey Archer and Major Charles Ingram, dubbed "the coughing major".</p>
<p>In 2003, he conducted a series of interviews with pop singer Michael Jackson for the controversial ITV documentary Living With Michael Jackson.</p>
<p>He later moved to the US where he co-anchored the current affairs show Nightline on ABC before moving to MSNBC, where he served as a political commentator until 2013.</p>
<p>He subsequently returned to the&#160;BBC&#160;as the broadcaster's religion editor.</p>
<p>October 2019 saw him compete in the celebrity spin-off of X Factor.</p>
