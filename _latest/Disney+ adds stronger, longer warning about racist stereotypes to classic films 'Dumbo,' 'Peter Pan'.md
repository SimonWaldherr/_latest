The new disclaimer&#160;offers a more in-depth warning about &#8220;negative depictions&#8221; and &#8220;mistreatment of people or cultures" in titles&#160;such as "Dumbo" (1941), "Peter Pan" (1953),&#160;"The Aristocats" (1970), "The Jungle Book" (1967) and the original &#8220;Lady and the Tramp."&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"These&#160;stereotypes were wrong&#160;then and are wrong now," <a href="https://storiesmatter.thewaltdisneycompany.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">the updated disclaimer reads</a>. "Rather than remove this content, we want to acknowledge its harmful impact, learn from it and spark conversation to create a more inclusive future together. Disney is committed to creating stories with inspirational and aspirational themes that reflect the rich diversity of the human experience around the globe. To learn more about how stories have impacted society, please visit <a href="https://storiesmatter.thewaltdisneycompany.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">www.disney.com/StoriesMatter</a>."</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><strong class="gnt_ar_b_al">Disney+ disclaimer:</strong><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/tv/2019/11/13/disney-outdated-cultural-depictions-warning-classic-films/4183705002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">The&#160;streaming services adds an 'outdated cultural depictions' warning to classics 'Dumbo,' 'Peter Pan'</a></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><strong class="gnt_ar_b_al">'The Help' isn't a helpful resource on racism:</strong><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/movies/2020/06/08/the-help-isnt-helpful-resource-racism-heres-why/5322569002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">&#160;Here's why Twitter is mad the film is trending</a></p><img class="gnt_em_img_i" alt="Tinker Bell, Peter Pan and Wendy in a scene from Disney's 1953 animated &quot;Peter Pan.&quot;"><p class="gnt_em_img_ccw gnt_em_img_ccw__cap gnt_em_img_ccw__crd"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Viewers are then directed to a website that further explains the racially insensitive scenes.&#160;In "Dumbo," for example, a crow named Jim Crow speaks with a stereotypical African-American accent, and in <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/opinion/2020/07/18/indigenous-peoples-peter-pan-nfl-washington-dc-racist-column/5453676002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">"Peter Pan,"</a> Native people are referred to as "redskins" and portrayed speaking in an unintelligible language.&#160;"The Aristocats" and "Lady and The Tramp" feature Siamese cats with stereotypical&#160;Asian accents. All of these characters are voiced by white actors.&#160;&#160;</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-entertainment-movies-2" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The site also notes that Disney is working toward&#160;inclusivity&#160;through its third-party advisory council, which includes organizations such as the African American Film Critics Association and&#160;GLAAD:&#160;"They are supporting our efforts to increase our cultural competency by providing ongoing guidance and thought leadership on critical issues and shifting perceptions."&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The revised language of the new disclaimer comes less than one year after its original advisory, which included a much shorter message.&#160;</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-entertainment-movies-3" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"This program is presented as originally created.
