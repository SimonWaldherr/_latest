<div><p>Welcome to the first week of the 25th Los Angeles Times Festival of Books, Stories &amp; Ideas! </p><p>Because of the ongoing pandemic, this year&#8217;s free celebration of the written word is going virtual. For 25 days, writers, poets, artists, storytellers and bookstore exhibitors will be featured in 25 panels and readings &#8212; all viewable from the comfort of your home. Here&#8217;s what&#8217;s happening this week and how to tune in: <br></p><h2 id="this-weeks-lineup" class="subhead">This week&#8217;s lineup</h2>
<p><b>Monday, Oct. 19, at 6 p.m</b>.: Pulitzer Prize-winning playwright and novelist <b>Ayad Akhtar</b> will discuss his latest novel, &#8220;Homeland Elegies,&#8221; with acclaimed writer and religion scholar <b>Reza Aslan</b>. Akhtar&#8217;s deeply personal novel tells an epic story of longing and dispossession in the world that 9/11 made. Part family drama, part social essay, at its heart this is the story of a father, a son and the country they call home.</p><p><b>Tuesday, Oct. 20, at 5:30 p.m</b>.: <b>Bob Shrum</b>, former political strategist and director of the USC Center for the Political Future, will moderate a conversation on America&#8217;s political system and the role and representation of its citizens with panelists <b>Paul Adler</b>, <b>Jane Junn</b> and <b>John Matsusaka</b>.</p><p class="google-dfp-ad-caption">Advertisement</p>

<p></p><p><b>Wednesday, Oct. 21, at 6 p.m</b>.: <b>Jerry Brown</b>, the two-time former California governor, presidential candidate and mayor of Oakland, will join <b>Jim Newton</b>, a former Times editor-at-large and author of &#8220;Man of Tomorrow: The Relentless Life of Jerry Brown,&#8221; to discuss his storied life in politics. Times California columnist <b>Gustavo Arellano</b> will guide their conversation.</p><p><b>Thursday, Oct. 22, at 4:30 p.m.</b>: In his memoir <a class="link" href="https://www.latimes.com/entertainment-arts/books/story/2020-09-29/a-modern-window-into-the-soul-of-el-salvador">&#8220;Unforgetting: A Memoir of Family, Migration, Gangs, and Revolution in the Americas,&#8221;</a> <b>Roberto Lovato</b> offers an urgent, no-holds-barred tale of intergenerational trauma and interconnected violence between the U.S. and El Salvador. He&#8217;ll discuss his life and work with Times staff writer <b>Esmeralda Bermudez</b>, who was born in El Salvador and raised in Los Angeles.</p><p><b>Friday, Oct. 23, at 6 p.m</b>.: Crime-fiction writers <b>Rachel Howzell Hall</b>, <b>Attica Locke</b> and <b>Ivy Pochoda</b> discuss their latest releases and the real-world issues that drive them: social pressures, injustice, racial tension and female empowerment, all against the backdrop of riveting storytelling. <b>James Queally</b>, a Times crime and policing reporter and crime-fiction author himself, moderates their conversation.</p><p class="google-dfp-ad-caption">Advertisement</p>
