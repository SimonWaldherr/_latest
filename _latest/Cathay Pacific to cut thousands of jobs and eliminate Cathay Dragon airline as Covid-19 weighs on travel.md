<div><p class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">Hong Kong (CNN Business)Cathay Pacific (<a href="https://money.cnn.com/quote/quote.html?symb=CPCAY&amp;source=story_quote_link" class="inlink">CPCAY</a>) is eliminating thousands of jobs and shuttering its regional airline Cathay Dragon as the Covid-19 pandemic roils the global travel industry. </p><p class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">The cuts will affect 5,300 employees in Hong Kong, where the company is based. Some 600 workers outside of the Asian financial hub will also be affected, Cathay said in a statement. </p><p class="zn-body__paragraph speakable">The company also plans to eliminate additional jobs that remained unfilled, either through a recruitment freeze or natural attrition. All told, Cathay is reducing about 8,500 jobs across the company, accounting for about 24% of its headcount.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">"We have taken every possible action to avoid job losses up to this point," said Cathay Pacific Group CEO Augustus Tang in a statement. He said the airline has scaled back capacity, deferred new aircraft deliveries, frozen recruitment and cut executive pay, among other measures. </p><p id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default"></p><p></p><p></p><ul class="cn cn-list-hierarchical-xs cn--idx-5 cn-zoneAdContainer"></ul><p class="zn-body__paragraph">Even so, Tang said the company continues to burn as much as 2 billion Hong Kong dollars ($258 million) per month. Wednesday's changes will reduce the company's cash burn by about 500 million Hong Kong dollars ($65 million) per month, he added. </p><p class="read-more-gradient"></p><p class="read-more-link" id="js-body-read-more">Read More</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">Cathay Pacific stock jumped 4% Wednesday in Hong Kong after the news. </p><a></a><p>Like other major airlines, Cathay Pacific has taken drastic steps to shore up its business this year. Earlier this summer, the Hong Kong government <a href="https://edition.cnn.com/2020/06/09/business/cathay-pacific-hong-kong-bailout-intl-hnk/index.html" target="_blank">agreed to lead a $5 billion bailout of the airline</a> , taking a minority stake.</p><a></a><p>Cathay <a href="https://news.cathaypacific.com/cathay-pacific-group-releases-combined-traffic-figures-for-september-2020" target="_blank">said this week</a> it expects to operate approximately 10% of pre-pandemic passenger flight capacity for the rest of 2020, and under 50% for 2021.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">A lack of consumer confidence, the drop in business travel, and fresh coronavirus spikes in the United States, Europe and elsewhere continue to weigh heavily on global air travel. </p><p id="cnn-end-of-content" class="myFinance-widget"></p><p></p><ul class="cn cn-list-hierarchical-xs cn--idx-13 cn-zoneAdContainer"></ul><a></a><p>The International Air Transport Association, which represents some 290 airlines, said last week that it estimates airline revenues this year will be down at least 50% ($419 billion compared to $838 billion in 2019). The trade group urged governments to use Covid-19 testing to safely re-open borders and called for more financial support "to prevent the systemic collapse of the aviation industry," IATA <a href="https://www.iata.org/en/pressroom/pr/2020-10-13-02/" target="_blank">said in a statement last week</a> </p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">-- Sherisse Pham contributed to this report.</p></div>
