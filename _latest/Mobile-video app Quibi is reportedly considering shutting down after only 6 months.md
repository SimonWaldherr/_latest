<p>Mobile-video service Quibi is considering shutting down just six months after launch, <a href="https://www.wsj.com/articles/quibi-weighs-shutting-down-as-problems-mount-11603301946">according to The Wall Street Journal's Benjamin Mullen</a>.&#160;</p><p>The streaming service, which was founded by former DreamWorks cofounder Jeffrey Katzenberg, raised $1.75 billion from investors like Walmart, PepsiCo, and Anheuser-Busch ahead of its launch in April. But Quibi has struggled to gain viewers, which has resulted to lucrative advertising deals being put on hold, the Journal reports.&#160;</p><p>Last month, <a href="https://www.wsj.com/articles/quibi-explores-strategic-options-including-a-possible-sale-11600707806">the Journal reported</a> that Quibi was exploring multiple options, including a possible sale. Katzenberg had recently told people he may have to shut down the company, <a href="https://www.theinformation.com/articles/katzenberg-tried-to-sell-quibi-content-raising-questions-about-services-future">The Information reported</a> on Tuesday.</p><p>A spokesperson for Quibi did not immediately respond to Business Insider's request for comment.&#160;</p>

<p>Despite the big-name investors and powerful names attached to the service &#8212; including former Hewlett Packard Enterprises CEO Meg Whitman at the helm &#8212; Quibi has struggled to gain a foothold in the streaming industry since its launch.</p><p>The service was intended to be used on-the-go, but the timing of its launch <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/05/11/business/media/jeffrey-katzenberg-quibi-coronavirus.html">coincided with the coronavirus outbreak</a>, which meant fewer people commuting and an economic downtown that may have made customers hesitant to pay for a subscription. And despite a roster of impressive Hollywood talent &#8212; including Liam Hemsworth and Anna Kendrick &#8212; <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/quibi-insiders-share-experiences-working-on-shows-movies-of-app-2020-6?r=ah-sub">none of Quibi's show's became major hits</a>.&#160;</p><p>Plus, Quibi is the subject of <a href="https://variety.com/2020/digital/news/quibi-patent-suit-moves-forward-1234718852/">a patent lawsuit</a> over the technology it uses to rotate the videos based on the orientation of the user's phone.&#160;</p><p>According to the Journal, Quibi will hold an investor call on Wednesday to provide an update on the company's status.</p>
