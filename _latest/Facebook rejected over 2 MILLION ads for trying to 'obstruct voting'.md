<p class="mol-para-with-font">Not everyone appreciates the effort.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Last week, President Trump criticized Facebook for limiting the reach of a New York Post article alleging Hunter Biden arranged a meeting between Ukrainian energy officials and his father, Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">A Facebook spokesman said it was 'reducing' the story's distribution on the platform while a third-party fact-checker verified its allegations.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Progressives have criticized the site for censoring news stories, too.&#160;&#160;</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Mark Zuckerberg gave the okay to alter Facebook's news feed algorithm to reduce the presence of stories by left-leaning sites like Mother Jones, according to <a class="class" rel="nofollow noreferrer noopener" target="_blank" href="https://www.wsj.com/articles/how-mark-zuckerberg-learned-politics-11602853200">the Wall Street Journal</a>.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">In addition to trying to stymie efforts at misinformation and intimidation, Facebook has been proactive in voter registration and education.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">The company announced it September it had already registered more than 2.5 million Americans to vote.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">It's pinned voting information at the top of Facebook, Instagram and WhatsApp, and launched poll-worker recruitment drives to address shortages because of the pandemic .</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">New political ads will be banned between October 27 and November 3, though existing ones can stay be reused and re-targeted .</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">And anyone claiming victory before a race been called by Reuters will have their post flagged with a link to the official results.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">'This election is not going to be business as usual,' Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg has said.&#160;</p><p
