Shares ofclimbed once again in after-hours trading on the earnings report.</p><a></a><a></a><p>There had been doubts that Tesla could achieve its <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2020/04/02/tech/tesla-sales-production/index.html" target="_blank">goal of delivering 500,000 cars</a> to customers this year, given the <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2020/03/19/cars/tesla-suspends-production/index.html" target="_blank">shutdown of its plant in Fremont California</a> , earlier due to the Covid-19 pandemic ordered by local health authorities. It has delivered 319,000 cars in the first three quarters of this year.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">Tesla reported net income of $874 million excluding special items, up 156% compare to what it made on that basis a year ago and nearly double its second quarter profit. Analysts had been forecasting earnings of $593 million. </p><p id="ad_rect_atf_02" class="ad-ad_rect_atf_02 ad-refresh-default"></p><p></p><p></p><ul class="cn cn-list-hierarchical-xs cn--idx-5 cn-zoneAdContainer"></ul><p class="zn-body__paragraph">Including special items, net income came in at a more modest $331 million. That was more than triple its second quarter earnings, which were released as it was coming off of the temporary lockdown of its plant. </p><p class="read-more-gradient"></p><p class="read-more-link" id="js-body-read-more">Read More</p><a></a><p>Revenue hit $8.8 billion, up 39% from a year ago. And cash flow more than tripled to $1.4 billion. Between the cash generated by operations and the money raised in recent stock sales, the <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2020/01/31/investing/tesla-cash-crunch/index.html" target="_blank">once cash-starved</a> company ended the quarter with $14.5 billion in cash on hand, up 69% in just three months.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">"We should have sufficient liquidity to fund our product roadmap, long-term capacity expansion plans and other expenses," the company said in its statement.</p><p class="zn-body__paragraph">Tesla said it recently upgraded its Fremont assembly line where it builds the Model Y, its lower priced SUV.
