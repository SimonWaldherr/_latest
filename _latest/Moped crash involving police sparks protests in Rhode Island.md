<div><p>Moped crash involving police sparks protests in Rhode Island Authorities are investigating whether a police vehicle struck the motorist.</p><p>A Rhode Island man was in a coma Wednesday after his moped crashed during a police chase earlier this week, sparking protests.</p><p>Authorities are investigating whether Jhamal Gonsalves' moped was struck by a Providence police vehicle just before crashing on Sunday.</p><p class="Ad incontentTeads incontentTeads--story ad-slot"></p><p>Video before the crash occurred showed police trying to get a group of people riding dirt bikes off the street. Gonsalves could be seen in the video turning right with a police cruiser pursuing him closely just before the crash occurs outside of the frame.</p><p>The incident sparked major protests Tuesday night as hundreds gathered in support of Gonsalves. At least 19 people have been arrested.</p><p class="ScrollSpy_container"><p></p></p><p class="RatioFrame aspect-ratio--auto"></p><img class="" alt="PHOTO: People rally in Providence, R.I., to support a man who was injured in an officer-involved moped crash, Oct. 20, 2020." src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7"><p class="Caption__Wrapper"></p><p>Representatives of Black Lives Matter Rhode Island and other activist groups spoke alongside family members of Gonsalves&#8217; family.</p><p>Gonsalves&#8217; father, Mark Gonsalves, urged protestors to remain nonviolent and told <a href="https://www.abc6.com/hundreds-rally-in-providence-to-support-man-injured-in-officer-involved-moped-crash-2/" target="_blank">ABC affiliate WLNE</a> that he didn&#8217;t want his son&#8217;s name associated with violence.</p><p>&#8220;I want a protest to be scheduled during the daytime, at the attorney general&#8217;s office, so the officer can be arrested,&#8221; Mark Gonsalves said. &#8220;So an indictment can be made and a warrant can be issued and the officer can be arrested.&#8221;</p></div>
