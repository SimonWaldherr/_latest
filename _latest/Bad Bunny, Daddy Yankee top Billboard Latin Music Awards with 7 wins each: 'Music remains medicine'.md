He did not attend the ceremony.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Daddy Yankee won six of his awards for his hit &#8220;Con Calma,&#8221; which featured the 1990s artist Snow. His honors included the hot Latin song aware, and song of the year honors for streaming and airplay and digital platforms.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The reggaeton star dedicated the first of his awards to those who had lost loved ones due to COVID-19, the disease caused by the coronavirus. &#8220;Music remains medicine,&#8221; he said.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Actress Gaby Espino hosted the ceremony, which aired live on Telemundo from the BB&amp;T Center in Sunrise, Florida. Bad Bunny and Daddy Yankee triumphed at the Billboard Latin Music Awards Wednesday, Oct. 21, 2020, claiming seven trophies apiece at the pandemic-delayed show."><p class="gnt_em_img_ccw gnt_em_img_ccw__cap gnt_em_img_ccw__crd"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Mexican romance singer-songwriter Armando Manzanero received a Lifetime Achievement Award during the ceremony. Enrique Iglesias was honored as Billboard's Top Latin Artist of All Time.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Luis Fonsi and Daddy Yankee were also recognized for &#8220;Despacito,&#8221; which was named song of the decade.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The ceremony, rescheduled from April&#160;to October, took&#160;place in-person at&#160;the&#160;BB&amp;T Center in Sunrise, Florida. It featured a limited audience, but had most of the trappings of a pre-pandemic awards show including a red carpet and live performances.&#160;</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-entertainment-music-2" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Billboard and NBCUniversal, Telemundo's parent company, said&#160;they put<a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/entertainment/music/2020/10/20/billboard-latin-music-awards-go-live-eschew-taped-performances/5990185002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a"> a number of safety measures in place to mitigate risk</a> of performers and crew members contracting COVID-19.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Five stages were constructed: a central stage for Espino and four outlying stages for performers, which allowed&#160;sets to be sanitized&#160;between acts and helped&#160;facilitate social distancing.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"It's certainly unprecedented for us," Jeff Mayzurk, Telemundo's executive vice president of&#160;operations and technology, told USA TODAY.
