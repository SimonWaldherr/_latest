<div><p>Sirius XM is reportedly close to reaching a $120 million per year deal with host Howard Stern. (Jared Siskin/Patrick McMullan via Getty Images)</p><p>In what's proven to be an up-and-down week, stocks bounced back from Tuesday's losses as President Donald Trump seemed to reverse the hard stance against further stimulus he articulated just the day before. </p><p>Wall Street, ever welcoming of further fiscal stimulus, cheered the White House's apparent shift in philosophy, and all three major U.S. indices rose on Wednesday. </p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p>The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 530 points, or 1.9%, to finish at 28,303. </p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p><b>Gene-editing discovery earns a Nobel Prize. </b>Gene-editing stocks like Crispr Therapeutics (ticker: <a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/stocks/crsp-crispr-therapeutics-ltd">CRSP</a>), Editas Medicine (<a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/stocks/edit-editas-medicine-inc">EDIT</a>) and Intellia Therapeutics (<a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/stocks/ntla-intellia-therapeutics-inc">NTLA</a>) all rose on Wednesday after the two scientists, who discovered the Crispr/Cas9 gene-editing technique, were given the Nobel Prize in chemistry. </p><p>Emmanuelle Charpentier and Jennifer Doudna each co-founded companies to capitalize on their discovery, which is a promising new way to reprogram the DNA of cells that may be spreading diseases in the body. </p><p><b>Howard Stern: vital to Sirus stock.</b> Sirius XM (<a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/stocks/siri-sirius-xm-holdings-inc">SIRI</a>) shareholders likely know this already, but "shock jock" Howard Stern is a vital part of the company's success. That's why the subscription radio service is reportedly close to reaching a $120 million per year deal with Stern to extend his contract, as he's brought in millions of listeners and his show continues to be popular. </p><p>Stern also enjoys negotiating power as competitors in podcasting threaten to erode Sirius' business &#8211; making it all the more important to retain the exclusive talent already on their roster. </p></div>
