<div><p>To prepare for flu season and a potential second wave of the coronavirus, CVS is planning to hire 15,000 employees at its U.S. locations. (Getty Images)</p><p>Following weekend stimulus negotiations led by House Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin, Rep. Pelosi set a 48-hour deadline for the White House to approve a stimulus deal to run before the election.</p><p>Both sides have been peddling toward another relief package but testy negotiations have caused the <a href="https://money.usnews.com/money/blogs/the-smarter-mutual-fund-investor/articles/how-would-rising-interest-rates-impact-the-market">markets</a> to point lower on Monday. Pelosi and Mnuchin will revisit talks on Tuesday. </p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p id="ad" class="ad lazy "></p><p>All major indices finished down on Monday as markets face continued <a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/investing-101/articles/what-is-the-cboe-volatility-index-vix">uncertainty</a>. The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell 1.44% to 28,195 points, the S&amp;P 500 dropped 1.63% to 3,426 points and the Nasdaq saw a steady decline of 1.65% to 11,478 points. </p><p><b>All good for China.</b> On a macroeconomic front, China claims its GDP grew 4.9% in the third quarter compared with the same period from a year ago, helping outlook on markets. China started the first quarter of this year with a 6.8% economic contraction, followed by a second-quarter growth of 3.2%. </p><p>China&#8217;s third-quarter expansion was driven by industrial production, and a steady recovery of the service sector. The U.S. saw real GDP decline to 31.4% in the second quarter and is expected to see a rise in the third quarter, according to economic forecasts from Deloitte.</p><p><b>IBM Q3 earnings.</b> IBM (ticker: <a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/stocks/ibm-international-business-machines-corp">IBM</a>) reported their earnings for their quarter ending Sept. 30 and results were in line with expectations albeit underwhelming. Earnings per share came in at analysts' expectations at $2.58. The company&#8217;s third-quarter revenue was $17.6 billion, which is about a 2.5% decrease from the prior year. </p><p>IBM stock has underperformed in 2020, down 9% year to date, compared with the rise in market value of other tech companies. The cloud company narrowed its focus on building its cloud computing business with the growth of e-commerce and as working from home has become the norm. </p><p id="ad" class="ad"></p><p><b>All hands on deck.</b> CVS (<a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/stocks/cvs-cvs-health-corp">CVS</a>) is planning to hire 15,000 employees across its U.S. locations ahead of flu season and a potential second wave of the coronavirus in the fourth quarter. The pharmacy chain anticipates more hands are needed on deck to weather the high inflows of patients expected to come this winter.<br></p></div>
