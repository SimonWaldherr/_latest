<div><p>A man was arrested for allegedly making a threat against Wichita Mayor Brandon Whipple, police said on Friday. Whipple told <a href="https://www.kwch.com/2020/10/16/man-arrested-for-threat-against-wichita-mayor-brandon-whipple/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">CBS Wichita affiliate KWCH</a> that the suspect was upset over the Kansas city's mask mandate.</p>

    
<p id="mpu-plus-top-right-rail" class="ad-mpu-plus-top-right-rail ad-wrapper ">
    </p>
<p>"From my understanding, we have someone who's upset with me over what I think is policies related to stopping the spread of COVID, who reached out to someone else looking for my address with the intent to do harm," Whipple told KWCH.</p><p><a href="https://www.wichita.gov/WPD/NewsReleases/2020-10-16%20Criminal%20Threat%20Arrest.pdf" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Wichita police said in a news release</a> that Meredith Dowty, 59, of Wichita, was arrested on a charge of making a criminal threat.&#160;</p>

<p>Whipple told KWCH that from what he gathered in text messages read to him, the suspect was allegedly interested in trying to kidnap him and causing harm with a knife. &#160;</p><p>According to the <a href="https://www.kansas.com/news/politics-government/article246513655.html" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Wichita Eagle</a>, Dowty is a retired firefighter who was honored by the Wichita City Council in 2008 for saving the life of a police officer who was shot. He also performs locally as musician, according to the paper.</p><p>Whipple enacted the city's mask ordinance in July. Since then, he has been a target for local anti-mask activists, according to the Wichita Eagle. The paper reported that about 120 people spoke for about seven hours &#8212;   mainly in opposition to the ordinance &#8212;   at a city council meeting in September.</p></div>
