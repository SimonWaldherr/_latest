<div><p>President <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Donald Trump</a> is already attacking <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/nbc" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">NBC</a>&#8217;s upcoming debate moderator, <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/topic/kristen-welker" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Kristen Welker,</a> as &#8220;terrible and unfair&#8221; in what appears to be preemptive damage control.</p><p>It&#8217;s a change of tone from January, when Trump congratulated Welker after she was given a slot as a weekend anchor, saying NBC &#8220;<a href="https://twitter.com/yashar/status/1317605070695878658?s=20" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">made a very wise decision</a>.&#8221;</p><p>Trump senior campaign adviser Jason Miller said on <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/fox-news" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Fox News</a> just last week that Welker &#8212; whom he characterized as <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/media/trump-calls-nbcs-kristen-welker-terrible-and-unfair-before-she-moderates-next-debate" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">&#8220;very fair&#8221; &#8212; would do an &#8220;excellent job&#8221;</a> moderating the third debate, which is set for Thursday in Nashville, Tennessee. &#8220;I have a very high opinion of Kristen Welker,&#8221; he said.</p><p>Trump previouosly agreed to Welker as a moderator.</p><p>But on Saturday, Trump ripped her in a tweet. &#8220;She&#8217;s aways been terrible &amp; unfair, just like most of the Fake News,&#8221; he wrote, adding: &#8220;But I&#8217;ll still play the game.&#8221;</p><p>The presidents oldest son, Donald Trump Jr., piped up in a retweet: &#8220;Yikes! with Guthrie and got <a href="https://twitter.com/Acyn/status/1317617984353914880?s=20" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">&#8220;A+ marks&#8221;</a> for his performance &#8212; something that might surprise his critics.</p><p>Trump also falsely insinuated that Guthrie had dropped out of sight. &#8220;But you know what?&#8221; he added. &#8220;We&#8217;re president, and they&#8217;re not.&#8221;</p><p>Both journalists and politicians &#8212; as well as Trump&#8217;s campaign adviser &#8212; hold Welker in high regard as fair and professional.</p><p>The Washington Women in Journalism Awards last month honored Welker as <a href="https://www.washingtonian.com/2020/09/22/meet-the-winners-of-the-2020-washington-women-in-journalism-awards/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">outstanding broadcast journalist</a> of the year.</p><p>Welker hasn&#8217;t responded to Trump&#8217;s attacks.</p><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" align="center"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">&#8220;They made a very wise decision,&#8221;
