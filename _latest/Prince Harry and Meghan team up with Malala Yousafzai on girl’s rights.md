Prince Harry and Meghan Markle had a video chat with Malala Yousafzai, the youngest Nobel Laureate on girl’s education. Research by Yousafzai’s fund suggests twenty million secondary school aged girls may never return to the classroom after the rampant pandemic, reports Arab News Pakistan.

Prince Harry and Meghan Markle are joining Malala and discussed issues related to challenges faced by girls in accessing education amid the coronavirus. The video chat snippets will be released on Sunday. This will be an interesting collaboration pertaining to the fact the youngest Nobel Laureate’s own experience stems from girls being stopped from getting education. She has now graduated with a degree in philosophy, politics and economics from Oxford University in June this year.

Whereas, Meghan Markle has long been campaigning on education for girls. She has profusely spoken about gender equality at forums including the United Nations Women Conference in 2015. This team up will be released on October 11 which has been declared as the International Day of the Girl Child that promotes girls’ rights and addresses the challenges young girls face around the world.
