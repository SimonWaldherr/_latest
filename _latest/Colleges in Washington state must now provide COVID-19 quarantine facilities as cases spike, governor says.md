<div><p>Colleges in Washington state must now provide COVID-19 quarantine facilities as cases spike, governor says Hundreds of cases have been confirmed at just the University of Washington.</p><p>Washington Gov. Jay Inslee has implemented a number of new protocols for <a href="https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/colleges-forced-reckon-rising-covid-19-cases/story?id=73006761" target="_blank">universities</a> as multiple campuses have seen spikes in <a href="https://abcnews.go.com/health/coronavirus" target="_blank">COVID-19</a> cases.</p><p>The requirements include a limit of two people allowed in sleeping rooms, a limit of five visitors in one place -- all of whom must be masked and socially distanced -- as well as the provision of quarantine facilities at Greek houses, off-campus congregate houses and dorms.</p><p class="Ad incontentTeads incontentTeads--story ad-slot"></p><p>More cases have been confirmed on campuses including the University of Washington and Washington State University, ABC Seattle station <a href="https://komonews.com/news/coronavirus/inslee-announces-new-measures-at-college-residences-to-slow-covid-19-spread" target="_blank">KOMO</a> reported, including 601 cases at UW alone as of Monday.</p><p class="ScrollSpy_container"><p></p></p><p class="RatioFrame aspect-ratio--auto"></p><img class="" alt="PHOTO: Washington state governor Jay Inslee talks at a press conference about the coronavirus outbreak, March 16, 2020, in Seattle." src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7"><p class="Caption__Wrapper"></p><p>"We've just got to get these spikes under control," Inslee told reporters at a news conference Tuesday.</p><p>Washington state experienced the first wave of COVID-19 in the U.S. after a patient there tested positive in late January.</p><p>To date, there have been more than 99,000 confirmed cases in the state and 2,300 deaths.</p></div>
