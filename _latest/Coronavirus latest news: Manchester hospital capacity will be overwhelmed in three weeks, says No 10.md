<p>Greater Manchester's intensive care capacity could be overwhelmed with Covid-19 cases within three weeks, Downing Street has said.</p>
<p>It comes as the Government piles increased pressure on the region to accept a move to the most stringent Tier 3 coronavirus restrictions.</p>
<p>Covid patients could take up the entire ICU capacity by November 8 even according to &#8216;best case&#8217; projections, with patients potentially taking up hospitals&#8217; entire surge capacity by November 12.</p>
<p>All three intensive care capacity units in the region will be used up by October 28, with a peak in hospital admissions expected by November 2.</p>
<p>Data on Manchester provided by the Prime Minister's spokesperson also showed that cases have tripled among the over-60 age group in the last 15 days.</p>
<p>The number of Covid inpatients in hospital beds is currently 40 per cent of the number at the height of the first wave, but the Prime Minister's spokesperson said that this is expected to rise significantly.</p>
<p>It comes after Andy Burnham, the mayor of Greater Manchester, has said he won't "roll over" and place the region under Tier 3 curbs at the "sight of a cheque".</p>
