<div><p class="speakable">A majority of Americans oppose calls for Democrats to &#8220;pack&#8221; the Supreme Court with additional justices if Republican succeed in confirming <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/judiciary/confirmation-of-amy-coney-barrett" target="_blank">Amy Coney Barrett</a> and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/joe-biden" target="_blank">Joe Biden</a> wins the presidency, according to a recent poll of likely voters.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="speakable">&#8220;Court-packing&#8221; emerged as a key issue on the presidential campaign trail in recent days as President Trump and top Republicans pushed to confirm Barrett to the nation&#8217;s highest court ahead of Election Day. Biden has repeatedly declined to reveal whether he would support efforts to expand the court.</p><p>Among likely voters, 58% said Democrats should not look to increase the size of the Supreme Court, according to the results of a poll conducted from Oct.15-18 by the New York Times and Siena College. Just 31% said they were in favor of court-packing, while 11% of responders were undecided.</p><p><strong><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/the-senates-path-to-confirming-amy-coney-barrett" target="_blank">THE SENATE'S PATH TO CONFIRMING AMY CONEY BARRETT&#160;</a></strong></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>The responses skewed heavily based on political affiliation, with 57% of registered Democrats in favor of court-packing and just 28% opposed. Among registered Republicans, just 6%&#160;support an expansion of the Supreme Court, while 89% were against the concept.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>The joint New York Times-Siena College poll was conducted through telephone interviews with 987 likely voters. The poll has a margin of error of plus or minus 3.4 percentage points.</p><p>Biden and his running mate, Sen. Kamala Harris, D-Calif., have faced increased scrutiny on the issue in recent days as Barrett&#8217;s confirmation hearings moved forward in the Senate. President Trump has hammered Biden over his refusal to publicly comment on court-packing.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p><strong><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/apps-products" target="_blank">CLICK HERE TO GET THE FOX NEWS APP</a></strong></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Earlier this month, Biden said he would not reveal his position on court-packing until after the election. But when pressed at an ABC town hall event last week, he said voters &#8220;have a right to know where I stand&#8221; and suggested he may reveal his stance before Nov. 3.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Harris would not comment on her stance on court-packing when asked during her debate with Vice President Mike Pence on Oct. 7.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Republican lawmakers have pledged to confirm Barrett to the Supreme Court before the election. Majority Leader Mitch McConnell said the Senate would hold its first confirmation vote next&#160;Monday.</p></div>
