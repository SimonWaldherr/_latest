<div><p> Purdue Pharma, the company that makes OxyContin, the powerful prescription painkiller that experts say helped touch off an opioid epidemic, will plead guilty to three federal criminal charges as part of a settlement of more than $8bn, justice department officials told The Associated Press. </p>
<p>The company will plead guilty to a criminal information being filed Wednesday in federal court in New Jersey to three counts, including conspiracy to defraud the United States and violating federal anti-kickback laws, the officials said. </p>
<p>The deal does not release any of the company&#8217;s executives or owners &#8211; members of the wealthy Sackler family &#8211; from criminal liability. A criminal investigation is ongoing.</p>
