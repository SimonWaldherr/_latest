<p>Oracle has slugged it out for decades with longtime rivals in enterprise tech, led by SAP, Microsoft and IBM.</p><p>With the rapid rise of the cloud, the tech giant &#8212; and its founder and CTO, Larry Ellison &#8212; has zeroed in on a new rival: Amazon, the dominant player in a trend that enabled businesses to set up networks on web-based platforms.</p><p>Oracle has been looking to catch up to Amazon in the cloud by <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/zoom-deal-big-win-turning-point-for-oracle-cloud-analysts-2020-5">expanding its data center footprint </a>and competing aggressively for high-profile cloud customers, such as <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/oracle-zoom-cloud-partnership-capacity-2020-4">Zoom </a>and <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/tiktok-deal-oracle-profile-cloud-amazon-web-services-2020-9">TikTok</a>.</p><p>The tech giant has also been luring the seasoned executives who helped transform Amazon into an enterprise powerhouse.&#160;</p><p>"The team Larry has brought in is about Oracle's pivot to the cloud," IDC President Crawford Del Prete told Business Insider. "It's critical that Oracle succeed in this pivot, and therefore he needs top talent to make it happen."</p><p>In the cloud era, a lot of the top talent comes from Amazon, which controls nearly 40% of the infrastructure and platform cloud market, according to IDC research. Microsoft is in second place, with Google in a distant third &#8212; leaving players like Oracle widely considered as also-rans.&#160;</p><p>The talent being brought on by Oracle could give it an advantage, as the database giant invests in its so-called 2nd-generation cloud platform, intended to give it the technological edge necessary to advance in the standings.</p><p>"They basically are targeting the folks who built the first generation cloud to build the 2nd generation cloud, aggregating all the lessons learned to build a better cloud." analyst Ray Wang of Constellation Research told Business Insider.</p>
