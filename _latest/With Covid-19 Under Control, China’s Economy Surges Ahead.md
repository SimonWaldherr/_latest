<div><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">BEIJING &#8212; As most of the world <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/14/world/europe/europe-coronavirus.html" title="">still struggles with the coronavirus pandemic</a>, China is showing once again that a fast economic rebound is possible when the virus is brought firmly under control.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">The Chinese economy surged 4.9 percent in the July-to-September quarter compared with the same months last year, the country&#8217;s National Bureau of Statistics announced on Monday. The robust performance brings China almost back up to the <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/01/16/business/economy/china-economy-gdp.html" title="">roughly 6 percent pace of growth</a> that it was reporting before the pandemic.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">Many of the world&#8217;s major economies have climbed quickly out of the depths of a contraction last spring, when shutdowns caused output to fall steeply. But China is the first to report growth that significantly surpasses where it was at this time last year. The United States and other nations are expected to report a third-quarter surge too, but they are still behind or just catching up to pre-pandemic levels.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">China&#8217;s lead could widen further in the months to come. It has almost no local transmission of the virus now, while the United States and Europe face another accelerating wave of cases.</p></div>
