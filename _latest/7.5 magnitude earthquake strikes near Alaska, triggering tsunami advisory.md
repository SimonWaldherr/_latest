<div><p>A 7.5 magnitude earthquake struck off the Alaska Peninsula on Monday afternoon, triggering a <a href="https://twitter.com/NWS/status/1318314056198115330" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">tsunami warning</a> in the region that has since been downgraded to an advisory, according to <a href="https://tsunami.gov/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">officials</a>. Some schools were also evacuated. There were no immediate reports of damage.</p>

    
<p id="mpu-plus-top-right-rail" class="ad-mpu-plus-top-right-rail ad-wrapper ">
    </p>
<p>The U.S. Geological Survey said the quake <a href="https://earthquake.usgs.gov/earthquakes/eventpage/us6000c9hg/executive" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">occurred</a> at 1:54 p.m. PT some 56 miles southeast of Sand Hill and nearly 600 miles southwest of Anchorage. The earthquake had a depth of about 25 miles. Several larger aftershocks were also recorded at magnitudes 5.8, 5.7, 5.2 and 5.5.</p><p>Emergency sirens were activated in Alaska and the Kodiak Emergency Operations Center is monitoring, CBS affiliate KTVA-TV <a href="https://www.alaskasnewssource.com/2020/10/19/tsunami-warning-issued-after-magnitude-74-earthquake-strikes-near-sand-point/" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">reported</a>. Police in the city of Homer are&#160;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/homerakpolice/posts/826277828115120" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">urging</a> residents in low elevation areas to move to higher ground.</p>

<p>KTVA-TV said some schools outside Homer had been evacuated.</p><p>"Right now, with everybody evacuating, it's really important they wear masks and keep a little bit of distance because we've got high risk down in the southern Kenai Peninsula now too," a Kenai Peninsula Borough School District spokesman said of the precautions being taken amid the <a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/feature/coronavirus/" target="_blank">coronavirus pandemic</a>.</p><p>The National Weather Service in <a href="https://twitter.com/NWSLosAngeles/status/1318312931155562496" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">Los Angeles</a> and <a href="https://twitter.com/NWSSanDiego/status/1318323759585980416" target="_blank" rel="nofollow noopener">San Diego</a> said there was no tsunami threat to the West Coast.</p> 
<p>In July, a&#160;<a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/news/earthquake-alaska-tsuani-warning-evacuations-kodiak/" target="_blank">powerful 7.8-magnitude earthquake</a>&#160;struck near Alaska and prompted a tsunami warning, which was canceled several hours later.</p></div>
