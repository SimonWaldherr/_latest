<p>Spain and France each surpassed one million confirmed coronavirus cases this week, as Europe battles outbreaks and record numbers throughout the continent.</p>   <p>Spain surpassed one million confirmed cases on Wednesday and is reporting <a href="https://coronavirus.jhu.edu/region/spain">1,005,295</a> cases as of Thursday evening local time, according to data from Johns Hopkins University. </p>   <p>It became the sixth nation worldwide to report one million cases, following  the U.S., India, Brazil, Russia and Argentina. </p>   <p>Hours later, France exceeded one million confirmed cases. It is now reporting <a href="https://coronavirus.jhu.edu/region/france">1,000,369</a>. </p>   <p>Spain and France now have the sixth and seventh highest global counts, respectively. Both countries have each reported more than 34,000 coronavirus-related deaths.</p>   <p>Europe has been battling rising COVID-19 case counts. The leaders of many EU countries are imposing stricter COVID-19 restrictions, while also trying to avoid national lockdowns.</p>   <p>Last week, Europe reported more than 977,000 new cases &#8212; its highest single-week case count, <a href="https://covid19.who.int/?gclid=Cj0KCQjwoJX8BRCZARIsAEWBFMKBmgcZf2gfycLUe3jxlo14h5xYsZE7Yuuq6qf3o1UoZBoficH-yJYaArRqEALw_wcB">according to the World Health Organization.</a></p>   <p>France's case count exceeded the one million mark a week after <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/10/14/923727094/paris-under-curfew-europe-reacts-as-countries-see-highest-ever-coronavirus-numbe">President Emmanuel Macron put a month-long curfew on Paris and other cities</a>. Macron declared a <a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/us-health-coronavirus-france-emergency/france-declares-public-health-state-of-emergency-over-covid-19-idUSKBN26Z2PQ">national emergency</a>. </p>   
    

    <p id="ad-backstage-News_World_Europe" class="ad-backstage"></p>

   <p>The Spanish government imposed a 15-day state of emergency in the Madrid region earlier this month, <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/10/09/922285239/spanish-government-orders-state-of-emergency-in-madrid-region-as-covid-19-cases-">NPR's Marisa Pe&#241;aloza reported</a>.</p>   <p>The U.S. has the most confirmed cases in the world, at 8,354,300. It <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/04/28/846741935/u-s-surpasses-1-million-coronavirus-cases">surpassed one million cases in April.</a> </p>   <p>Ireland became the first European country to return to a national lockdown after its total number of cases rose by 75% since early September. The government <a href="https://www.npr.org/sections/coronavirus-live-updates/2020/10/20/925811935/ireland-to-impose-six-week-national-lockdown-estimates-150-000-job-losses">asked residents to stay within a three-mile radius of their homes and closed many businesses.</a> </p>   <p>"We can make sure the second wave is only a ripple," Irish Deputy Prime Minister Leo Varadkar said on Monday, "but that depends on all of us."</p>   <p>Reese Oxner is an intern on NPR's News Desk. </p>
