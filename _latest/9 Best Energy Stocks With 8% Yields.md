Unlike with dividend stocks, investors who buy MLPs become junior partners in the company and typically receive a large portion of the company's profits via distributions. Many MLPs operate in the midstream <a href="https://money.usnews.com/investing/stock-market-news/slideshows/energy-stocks-to-buy-as-crude-prices-recover">oil and gas</a> segment, which has taken a big hit in 2020. However, the industry has stabilized in the second half of 2020, and high-quality MLPs provide both investment upside and some of the highest dividend yields on Wall Street. Here are nine MLPs and midstream stocks that Bank of America recommends.</p></div>
