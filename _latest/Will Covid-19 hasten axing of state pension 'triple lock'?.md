Covid-19 and economic hardship.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">The triple lock means annual state pension rises are decided by whatever is the highest of price inflation, average earnings growth or 2.5 per cent.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Confirmation today that September's headline inflation rate was 0.5 per cent, while wages declined 1 per cent over the summer, means that third element will apply.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">The Government has so far stuck to its popular state pension pledge to older voters.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">However, as the working age population struggles with wage cuts and rising job losses it is likely to face mounting pressure to abandon the guarantee.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">From April, pensioners are set to get an rate.&#160;</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">That's around &#163;9,300 or &#163;7,200 a year respectively - find out why they are different <a class="class" href="/money/pensions/article-3513785/I-paid-NI-44-years-feel-hard-STEVE-WEBB-replies.html">here.</a></p><p class="mol-para-with-font">Financial experts have already warned a big correction in pay levels after the Covid-19 crisis <a class="class" href="/money/pensions/article-8666859/Triple-lock-guarantee-state-pension-increases-curbed.html">could mean a massive increase in the state pension in April 2022.</a></p><p class="mol-para-with-font">This could provoke even greater opposition to the triple lock if the nation hasn't yet bounced back from the pandemic. Meanwhile, the outcome of Brexit remains an unknown until we see the shape of future trade deals with the EU and the US.&#160;</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">The Institute for Fiscal Studies points out that the latest increase means the basic state pension has risen 41 per cent over the past 11 years, compared to 25 per cent if it had been linked to inflation or 22 per cent to earnings growth.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">It cites estimates putting the extra cost at &#163;5.6billion if calculated up to the current year, plus a further &#163;3.2billion if the policy remains unchanged by 2024&#8211;25.</p><h3 class="mol-factbox-title">Lifetime allowance set to rise&#160;</h3> <p class="mol-para-with-font">The total you can put in your pension and get tax relief currently stands at &#163;1,073,100.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">But this limit is set to increase in line with September's 0.5 per cent inflation rate to &#163;1,078,900 next April.</p> <p class="adHolder mpu" id="mpu_factbox_1">

<p class="mol-para-with-font">Defenders of the triple lock insist it is a vital safeguard against pensioner poverty and that the state pension isn't as generous as similar schemes in other countries.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">And any move to axe it would cause a backlash among elderly voters, who paid contributions for their state pension throughout their working lives.</p><h2 class="mol-para-with-font mol-style-subhead">Pandemic could bring forward date when triple lock is abandoned</h2><p class="mol-para-with-font">'The triple lock ensures that state pension payments do not fall in real terms during periods of economic turmoil,' says Carl Emmerson, deputy director of the Institute for Fiscal Studies.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">'There are good reasons to want to do this. But these pensions will subsequently also be increased more quickly in line with the bounce back in earnings.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">'Periods of turmoil such as this thus lead to a ratcheting up in the value of state pensions while living standards of the working age population suffer.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">'At some point this will prove unsustainable. The current pandemic has highlighted this and, I suspect, brought forward the date at which the triple lock is abandoned.'</p><h2 class="mol-para-with-font mol-style-subhead">Pledge could face the chop as Government seeks to curb spending</h2><p class="mol-para-with-font">'Retirees are set for an inflation busting state pension boost next year,' says Ian Browne, pensions expert at Quilter.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">'This is despite a challenging economic backdrop in which many workers are taking a cut to their pay packet, are working fewer hours than they would like, or have lost their jobs altogether.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">'But even more contentious is the fact that once the furlough scheme ends later this year and if wages recover, in its current form the triple lock will provide an artificially large boost to state pension income in 2022/23 when we could be in the clasp of a deep recession and when the government is struggling to control the deficit.</p><p class="mol-para-with-font">'Given the Treasury&#8217;s final tab for the Government&#8217;s Covid-19 response is likely to surpass &#163;300billion, there is still the potential the triple lock to face the chop next year as the Chancellor goes hell for leather in reducing the deficit.'</p><img id="i-a2fea9ded5960857"
