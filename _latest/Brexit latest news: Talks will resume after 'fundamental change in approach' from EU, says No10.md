<p>Michel Barnier will return to London to restart Brexit talks with the UK following a "fundamental change in approach," from the EU that paves the way for a trade deal, Downing Street has said.</p>
<p>The two sides reached a breakthrough following a meeting between Lord Frost, the UK's chief negotiator, and Mr Barnier this morning.&#160;</p>
<p>A No10 spokesman said the Government now believed the EU was "serious about talking intensively, on all issues, and bringing the negotiation to a conclusion".</p>
<p>Mr Barnier gave a statement to the European Parliament this morning that signalled a change in approach, the spokesman said.</p>
<p>He is expected to come to London this week to restart trade talks.</p>
<p>Boris Johnson last week told the UK to prepare for a no-deal, with Michael Gove this week claiming the EU had effectively ended talks during the European Council summit.</p>
<p>Both sides insist they want to reach a deal.&#160;</p>
