<div><p class="teal-video-wrap"></p><p class="gnt_em_vt"></p><p class="gnt_em_vb gnt_em_vb__te"></p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">The claim: The president signed 9 executive orders to combat child trafficking</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">President Donald Trump's anti-human trafficking stance has become a hallmark of&#160;his administration. Daughter and adviser&#160;Ivanka Trump&#160;has spurred the president's investment in anti-trafficking measures since the beginning of his term. She called trafficking an "ugly stain on civilization" at the&#160;unveiling of the Trafficking in Persons&#160;report at the State Department in 2017, <a href="https://www.cnn.com/2017/06/27/politics/ivanka-trump-anti-human-trafficking-state-department/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">according to CNN</a>.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Ivanka Trump's work to mitigate human&#160;trafficking globally is also well-documented, from a <a href="https://www.instagram.com/p/BUeXDqUF1d5/?utm_source=ig_embed" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">meeting&#160;with survivors</a> in Italy&#160;to her influence in the Oval Office.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">During her speech at the Republican National Convention in August, Trump mentioned nine pieces of anti-trafficking legislation passed during the Trump administration.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"To protect the most vulnerable among us, I&#8217;ve worked alongside the president as he signed into law nine pieces of legislation to combat the evil of human trafficking," Ivanka Trump said, according to a <a href="https://www.rev.com/blog/transcripts/ivanka-trump-2020-rnc-speech-transcript" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">transcript </a>on the transcription blog Rev.</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-news-2" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">A claim posted to Facebook days later appeared to reflect Trump's statement, but it referenced nine executive orders instead of laws.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"Our President has signed 9 Executive Orders to combat child trafficking!" user Doug Ellingson&#160;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/doug.ellingson.71/posts/10157839963912462" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">posted</a>&#160;on Aug. 29. "Ask yourself why you don't know this? Who is fighting to keep it from you? And why don't they want you to know?"</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-news-3" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The viral post has over 39,000 shares and over 1,900 reactions. Ellingson did not elaborate on the contents of the post in response&#160;to a request for comment from USA TODAY.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><strong class="gnt_ar_b_al">More:</strong><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/factcheck/2020/09/11/fact-check-child-traffickers-arent-tagging-cars-potential-targets/5701105002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Fact check: No evidence child traffickers are tagging cars as potential targets with codes</a></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><strong class="gnt_ar_b_al">More:</strong><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/factcheck/2020/09/14/fact-check-list-10-donald-trump-accomplishments-3-true/3438025001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Fact check: On a viral list of 10 Trump 'accomplishments,' 3 are true</a></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p"><strong class="gnt_ar_b_al">More:</strong><a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/factcheck/2020/08/24/fact-check-8-000-trafficking-arrests-have-not-included-us-lawmakers/5601032002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Fact check: Over 8,000 US trafficking arrests since 2017 have not included members of Congress</a></p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">Trump's executive orders</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Executive orders issued by the president&#160;are readily available to the public on The White House&#160;<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">website</a>.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Trump has issued two executive orders targeting human trafficking. The first,&#160;aimed at disbanding transnational criminal organizations that traffic&#160;humans,&#160;was <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/presidential-executive-order-enforcing-federal-law-respect-transnational-criminal-organizations-preventing-international-trafficking/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">signed </a>on Feb. 9, 2017.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The second order concerned online child trafficking and sexual exploitation in the United States. It was <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/executive-order-combating-human-trafficking-online-child-exploitation-united-states/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">signed </a>Jan. 31.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Trump also issued three proclamations observing human trafficking. National Slavery and Human Trafficking Prevention Month has been recognized <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/proclamation-national-slavery-human-trafficking-prevention-month-2020/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">each</a><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/presidential-proclamation-national-slavery-human-trafficking-prevention-month-2019/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">January </a>since&#160;<a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/president-donald-j-trump-proclaims-january-2018-national-slavery-human-trafficking-prevention-month/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">2018.</a></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The Facebook user who posted the claim may have confused "legislation" with "executive order" and combined human trafficking with child trafficking.</p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">Did Trump sign 'nine pieces of legislation' against human trafficking?</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Not quite. According to the <a href="https://www.congress.gov/search?searchResultViewType=expanded&amp;q=%7B%22congress%22:%5B%22116%22%5D,%22search%22:%22trafficking%22,%22source%22:%22legislation%22,%22bill-status%22:%22law%22%7D" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">website </a>for Congress, before the RNC in August,&#160;Trump had signed eight bills expressly targeting&#160;human trafficking into law:</p><p id="~ad-slot-7103-usatoday-native-article_link-news-4" class="gnt_x_sl gnt_x_al"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Budgets for anti-human trafficking programs are referenced in other pieces of legislation passed during Trump's term. And two bills signed into law&#160;earlier this month, the <a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/116th-congress/senate-bill/982?q=%7B%22search%22%3A%5B%22human+trafficking%22%5D%7D&amp;s=4&amp;r=1" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Not Invisible Act of 2019 </a>and <a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/116th-congress/senate-bill/227/text?q=%7B%22search%22%3A%5B%22human+trafficking%22%5D%7D&amp;r=3&amp;s=4" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Savanna's Act</a>, addressed human trafficking prevention efforts for missing and murdered American&#160;Indians.</p><h2 class="gnt_ar_b_h2">Our rating: Partly&#160;false</h2><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">We rate this claim PARTLY FALSE, based on our research. The claim mistakes&#160;nine pieces of anti-human trafficking legislation for&#160;executive orders signed by Trump to combat child trafficking. The president has only signed two executive orders,&#160;one concerning child trafficking and another to prevent international trafficking.
