<div><p>Click to email this to a friend (Opens in new window)</p><p>Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)</p><p>Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)</p><p>A California judge ruled Monday that a rape case against former &#8220;That 70s Show&#8221; actor <a href="https://pagesix.com/tag/danny-masterson/">Danny Masterson</a>&#160;can move forward, a report said.</p>
<p>Masterson, who played Hyde on the hit sitcom, is accused of raping three women at his Los Angeles home in the early 2000s, <a href="https://variety.com/2020/tv/news/danny-masterson-rape-case-arraignment-1234774822/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Variety reported</a>.</p>
<p>He&#8217;s scheduled to be arraigned Nov. 2, the judge ruled Monday, rejecting his lawyer&#8217;s argument that the allegations were too old for him to faces charges.</p>
<p>Masterson, 44, could face life in prison if he&#8217;s convicted. He&#8217;ll remain free on $3.3 million bail pending his trial, according to the report.</p>
<p>Prosecutors charged Masterson under the state&#8217;s &#8220;one-strike&#8221; sexual offense law, which they argued blocks the charges from being subject to the statute of limitations, Variety reported.</p>
<p>In September, Masterson&#8217;s attorney, Tom Mersereau, filed a motion for the charges against him to be dropped.</p>
<p>The actor is also <a href="https://pagesix.com/2020/09/18/danny-masterson-makes-first-court-appearance-since-june-in-la/?_ga=2.188409645.46293044.1603107847-1892007394.1557935156">accused in a civil suit</a> of launching a harassment campaign against his accusers to &#8220;force them into &#173;silence.&#8221;</p>
