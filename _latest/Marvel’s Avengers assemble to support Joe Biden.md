<div><p>Our favorite superheroes want to make it clear as who they are supporting in the bid to the U.S. presidency. The cast of &#8216;Avengers&#8217; will assemble to pledge their support for Joe Biden by hosting a virtual fundraiser.</p>

<p>This event, which is set to take place tomorrow, will bring together Don Cheadle, Chris Evans, Scarlett Johansson, Paul Rudd, Mark Ruffalo and Zoe Saldana, who will be joined by the vice presidential candidate Sen. Kamala Harris. In addition, The Russo Brothers are also going to make an appearance for a Q&amp;A and trivia round.</p>

<p>As with many other celebrities doing their part and encouraging Americans to cast their vote, Mark Ruffalo has been using his social media account on Instagram to positively influence and inform people about the importance of voting. The actor has been extremely vocal in supporting Joe Biden as U.S.A&#8217;s next president, saying, &#8220;We must elect champions who will fight for the Green New Deal and push Congress to pass climate action that matches the scale of the problem with the urgency our country&#8217;s future demands.&#8221;</p>

<p>Joe Biden was also, for the first time in history, able to <a href="/entertainment/11819/biden-ad-enlists-the-beastie-boys-to-highlight-unsung-victims-of-the-pandemic">secure the rights of a song by the popular band Beastie boys</a> to be used in one of the presidential ads. Other big names that have expressed their support for Biden include the cast of the Broadway musical, <i>Hamilton, </i>Mindy Kaling, Kumail Nanjiani and Lilly Singh among others.</p>
