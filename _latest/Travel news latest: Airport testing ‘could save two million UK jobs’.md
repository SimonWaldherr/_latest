<p>A comprehensive airport testing scheme could save two million UK jobs, according to a senior travel industry expert.</p>
<p>Gloria Guevara, president and CEO of the World Travel and Tourism Council (WTTC), said: &#8220;Latest WTTC research shows the positive effect airport testing will have to revive international air travel, bring back jobs and resuscitate the global economy.&#160;</p>
<p>&#8220;Almost 20 million jobs across Europe could be saved, including three million in Germany, 1.93 million in the UK, 1.91 million in Italy and more than 1.5 million in France.&#8221;</p>
<p>Guevara praised the introduction of airport testing schemes at Charles de Gaulle and Orly airports in Paris, Heathrow in London and the recent trial which took place between Rome&#8217;s Fiumicino Airport and Milan Linate. But Guevara said to save international travel, we need a standardised testing protocol across the world.</p>
<p>Earlier this week the&#160;Transport Secretary Grant Shapps said&#160;that<a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/advice/quarantine-one-week-december-testing/"> he is "hopeful" that testing for international travellers</a> could be implemented by December 1, cutting quarantine from 14 days to approximately one week.</p>
