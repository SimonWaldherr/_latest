<div><p>An elderly Manhattan woman <a href="https://nypost.com/2020/09/29/elderly-woman-struck-by-revel-scooter-near-columbus-circle/">who was struck</a> by a Revel moped rider near Columbus Circle last month&#160;died from her injuries later that day, cops revealed Wednesday.</p>
<p>Helga Shnitker, 82, of the Upper West Side, was crossing West 60th Street on Broadway in the crosswalk just after 7 a.m. when an unidentified man on a moped ran into her, police said.</p>
<p>Shnitker suffered severe head trauma and was rushed to Mount Sinai Hospital, where she succumbed to her injuries, cops said.</p>
<p>The 23-year-old male Revel rider has not been charged. Police sources at the time of the collision said the victim was crossing against the light.</p>
<p>Shnitzker was the first pedestrian killed in a collision with a Revel rider since the scooter-rental company launched in New York more than two years ago.</p>
<p>Her death came just two months after <a href="https://nypost.com/2020/07/28/revel-shuts-down-service-in-nyc-following-series-of-crashes/">Revel shut down</a> its New York City operation after three fatal Revel collisions in the span of 10 days.</p>
<p>The company started up again in August with some <a href="https://nypost.com/2020/08/27/dot-chief-defends-allowing-revel-scooters-back-on-city-streets/">new safety protocols</a>.</p>
