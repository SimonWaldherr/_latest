<p>Social Capital's Chamath Palihapitiya is looking to <a href="https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/stocks/social-capital-spac-ipoc-clover-health-deal-chamath-palihapitiya-merger-2020-10-1029652323#">acquire the health insurance company Clover Health</a> via a SPAC, in a $3.7 billion transaction that's slated to close in 2021.</p><p>Clover offers private health-insurance plans for seniors, a product called <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/category/medicare-advantage">Medicare Advantage</a>. Competition is fierce for the <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/medicare-advantage-enrollment-oscar-bright-devoted-clover-alignment-2020-1">more than 24 million Americans</a> enrolled in Medicare Advantage plans, and for the thousands signing up daily as they turn 65. Startups like Clover are facing off against industry giants like UnitedHealthcare, Humana, and Aetna.</p><p>Founded in 2013, Clover Health&#160;got its start selling Medicare Advantage plans in New Jersey. In 2021, it plans to operate in Pennsylvania, Texas, Georgia, South Carolina, Arizona, Tennessee, and Mississippi.</p><p>The company in 2019 also laid off 140 employees, and the startup has <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2018/01/03/clover-health-insurance-start-up-angered-customers-missed-financials.html">struggled with billing and customer issues that influenced the plan's standing with Medicare</a>. Going into 2021, the plan has an overall star rating of <a href="https://cdn.cloverhealth.com/filer_public/3f/a2/3fa2a3d9-7edd-4e50-885f-39a7e13f5479/h5141_20ex016l_stars_eng_v3.pdf">three out of five</a>.&#160;</p><p><strong>Read more:</strong> <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/clover-health-growing-pains-as-medicare-advantage-plan-2019-6">Clover Health set out to upend healthcare for aging Americans. 5 years and $925 million in funding later, it's proving more challenging than expected.</a></p><p>Since 2015, Clover has steadily grown its membership base, predominantly in its New Jersey market.</p><p class="insider-raw-embed"></p><p>Over that time, Clover has consistently posted annual net losses. Through the first half of 2020, the company has posted a net gain of $34.7 million.</p><p class="insider-raw-embed"></p><p>Clover wasn't alone in posting big gains through the first six months of 2020.</p>

<p>Through the first half of this year, the big insurers posted positive earnings, with <a href="https://www.healthcaredive.com/news/unitedhealth-doubles-profit-in-q2-as-mlr-plummets-to-702-amid-pandemic/581647/">United Health Group roughly doubling its profits over the same time period the previous year</a>, and&#160;<a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2020/08/05/cvs-health-cvs-earnings-q2-2020.html">CVS Health beating expectations.</a></p><p>The period was marked by the coronavirus pandemic hitting the US,&#160;<a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/us-recession-economy-february-coronavirus-nber-official-data-2020-6">pushing the country into a recession</a>, and leading many Americans to postpone routine health appointments and procedures.</p><h2>Clover is promising big expansion plans, hinging on an untested new Medicare program&#160;</h2><p>In a <a href="https://twitter.com/chamath/status/1313461164890677249">tweet</a>, Palihapitiya laid out his expectations of growth for Clover. By 2021, Clover's membership is projected to be 273,000 people, roughly five times what it was at the middle of this year. Clover. That in turn would lead to revenue of $880 million, roughly double the company's 2019 figures.</p><p>The rapid expansion contrasts with the slower growth Clover has seen over the past few years.</p><p>Clover enrolled 39,400 members in 2019, 10,000 more than the year before. It signed on 54,400 people this year, <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/medicare-advantage-enrollment-oscar-bright-devoted-clover-alignment-2020-1">according to Business Insider's reporting.</a> As of the end of June, Clover's Medicare Advantage plans had 56,816 members.</p><p>A large part of that projected growth will come from Clover's plan to participate in <a href="https://innovation.cms.gov/innovation-models/direct-contracting-model-options">Medicare's direct contracting program.</a> The new program is a test to find ways to lower costs for the federal Medicare program, drawing in part from lessons learned through Medicare Advantage, in which insurers apply to participate.</p><p>In an <a href="https://cdn.cloverhealth.com/filer_public/53/d0/53d0dd4e-9a8f-4ca0-8ed2-e7c1b9a614fc/clover_health_deck.pdf">investor presentation</a>, Clover said that it has already agreed to care for 200,000 seniors through the direct contracting program next year, and it expects that will grow to&#160;450,000 by 2023. By contrast, it estimates it will cover 139,000 Medicare Advantage members that year.</p><h2><strong>Clover isn't alone in going after the red-hot Medicare Advantage market</strong></h2><p>Over the past five years, a crop of venture-backed health insurance companies have been growing their war chests, raising more than $4 billion. Some, including Clover, have their eyes set next on the public market.</p>
