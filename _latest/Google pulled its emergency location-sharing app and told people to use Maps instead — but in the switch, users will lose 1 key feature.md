<p>Google is removing its emergency location-sharing app Trusted Contacts in December, and is directing users to Google Maps instead.</p><p>Trusted Contacts, which Google launched in 2016, shares users' location with select family and friends.</p><p>Location sharing has since been built directly into Google Maps, the company said <a href="https://www.theverge.com/2020/10/18/21520182/google-discontinues-trusted-contacts-maps-emergency-location-sharing">in an email to users on Sunday</a>.</p><p>However, as&#160;<a href="https://www.theverge.com/2020/10/18/21520182/google-discontinues-trusted-contacts-maps-emergency-location-sharing">The Verge</a> points out, Google Maps requires you to actively broadcast your location, whereas Trusted Contacts does not. In an emergency, contacts can request a status update via the Trusted Contacts app, and if you don't respond, the app automatically shares your last known location.</p>
