<p>Fort Bragg, home of the US Army's XVIII Airborne Corps and Special Operations Command, claims its official Twitter account was hacked after explicit tweets were sent from the account.</p><p>"As many of you may know, there were a string of explicit Tweets from our account this afternoon," a representative from the installation said in a statement on Twitter. "This was not the work of our admins. Our account was hacked. We apologize to our followers."</p><p>"We have secured our account and looking into the matter," it added.</p><p>The official account began replying to an adult-content creator from the platform OnlyFans on Wednesday afternoon.</p>

<p id="gpt-post-defense-desktop_in_post_ad-fluid-2" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-desktop"></p>
<p>The base's account initially responded to a tweet from the creator about a subscriber unhappy with their content, saying, "He's lost and doesn't know a good thing when it's staring him in the eyes, or tickling his nose in this case."</p><p>In response to another suggestive tweet from the creator, the base's account said it wanted to give them "a deep long kiss" &#8212; in addition to other sexual acts.</p><p>The creator did not respond to Insider's requests for comment but did sarcastically reply to Ft. Bragg's tweets.</p><p>"Someone come get their fort," they tweeted, adding in another tweet, "Normalize horny tweeting from US Army forts."</p>
