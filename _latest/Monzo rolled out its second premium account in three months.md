</p><p>It has become imperative for Monzo to diversify its revenue streams and reduce its losses, which more than <a href="https://e.businessinsider.com/click/21846921.4/aHR0cHM6Ly9pbnRlbGxpZ2VuY2UuYnVzaW5lc3NpbnNpZGVyLmNvbS9wb3N0L3Zhcm8tYXR0YWlucy11cy1uYXRpb25hbC1iYW5rLWNoYXJ0ZXItbW9uem8tYW5udWFsLWxvc3Nlcy1tb3JlLXRoYW4tZG91YmxlLWJvZmFzLWRlZmVycmFscy1zaHJpbmstMjAyMC03P2NpZD1zZWFyY2g/5d233c18f730436f2414784fB66393499" target="_blank" rel="noopener">doubled</a>&#160;in its most recent fiscal year (ended February 29, 2020).&#160;In July, the neobank said that the pandemic has led it to call into&#160;<a href="https://e.businessinsider.com/click/21846921.4/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuY25iYy5jb20vMjAyMC8wNy8zMS9tb256by1sb3NzZXMtZG91YmxlLWFzLXVrLWRpZ2l0YWwtYmFuay13YXJucy1vZi1wYW5kZW1pYy11bmNlcnRhaW50eS5odG1s/5d233c18f730436f2414784fB263c33c4" target="_blank" rel="noopener">question</a>&#160;its ability to continue operating.</p><p>Since then, Monzo has made incremental moves to up revenues: Beginning in November, it will&#160;<a href="https://e.businessinsider.com/click/21846921.4/aHR0cHM6Ly9pbnRlbGxpZ2VuY2UuYnVzaW5lc3NpbnNpZGVyLmNvbS9wb3N0L2JvZmFzLW1vYmlsZS1yZWZyZXNoLWFkdmFuY2VzLWZpbmFuY2lhbC1lY29zeXN0ZW0tMjAyMC05P2NpZD1zZWFyY2gjSVRFTSUyMDI/5d233c18f730436f2414784fBb8ba9d82" target="_blank" rel="noopener">impose</a> a 3% ATM fee for withdrawals over &#163;250 ($319) per month for some users who use Monzo as a spending account but don't engage with it for <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/banking">banking</a> activities, like direct deposit or student loans. Chief product officer Mike Hudack underscored Monzo's laser focus on building sustainability, telling the Guardian that "everything we do needs to make money and be sustainable as a product."</p><p>Apart from adding fees, Monzo has been trying to monetize its customer base by striking a balance between products that are attractive, but also profitable.&#160;The retooled version of Monzo Plus, for example, deemphasized travel benefits that consumers would be unlikely to utilize amid the pandemic. That appears to be paying off, with the neobank reporting&#160;<a href="https://e.businessinsider.com/click/21846921.4/aHR0cHM6Ly9pbnRlbGxpZ2VuY2UuYnVzaW5lc3NpbnNpZGVyLmNvbS9wb3N0L2Zpcy1sYXVuY2hlcy1hLXN1YnNjcmlwdGlvbi1iYXNlZC1jb3JlLWJhbmtpbmctc29sdXRpb24tbW9uem8tcGx1cy1hbWFzc2VzLTUway11c2Vycy1pbi1maXJzdC1tb250aC1uYXR3ZXN0LWJvb3N0cy1pbi1icmFuY2gtYWNjZXNzaWJpbGl0eS0yMDIwLTg_Y2lkPXNlYXJjaCNJVEVNJTIwMg/5d233c18f730436f2414784fB88e59f53" target="_blank" rel="noopener">50,000</a>&#160;new Monzo Plus active users in the first month of relaunching, but it will need to sustain that momentum in order to significantly impact its bottom line, as a &#163;5 ($6.38) monthly fee at 50,000 users barely dents its latest annual loss of &#163;113.8 million ($145.2 million).</p><p>Priced three times higher than Plus, Monzo Premium could make a bigger impact&#8212;though that hinges on customers' willingness to shell out the money. Hudack remains confident despite the current economic downturn that "if you build something that's really powerful that people love and they want to keep, you make money from that."
