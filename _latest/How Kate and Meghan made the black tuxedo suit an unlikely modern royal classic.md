<div><p>Until recently, the most famous fashion moment involving a woman in a black tuxedo came in 1966 when the socialite Nan Kempner was turned away from&#160; La C&#244;te Basque restaurant in New York because she was wearing one of the 'Le Smoking' trouser suits which Yves Saint Laurent had recently&#160;introduced. In defiance, Kempner whipped off her trousers and took her table wearing only the jacket as a mini blazer dress.&#160;</p>
<p>Daring and laced with a sense of scandal, it&#8217;s a look which is about as far as you could get from the usual demure aesthetic of the Duchess of Cambridge. And yet,<a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/fashion/royals/duchess-cambridges-black-mcqueen-suit-ideal-choice-night-museum/"> Kate added a sleek black suit</a> to her wardrobe last week to present the Wildlife Photographer of the Year award in a virtual ceremony broadcast from the Natural History Museum.&#160;</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the Duchess of Sussex appeared in a new portrait for Time magazine this weekend, also wearing a black suit. Pared-back glamour was the vibe as she and Harry posed for photographer Matt Sayles to promote their roles as hosts of a virtual conversation titled &#8216;Engineering a Better World&#8217;.</p>
