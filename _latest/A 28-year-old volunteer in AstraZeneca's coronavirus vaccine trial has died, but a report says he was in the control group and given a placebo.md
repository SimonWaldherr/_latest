<p>A participant in AstraZeneca's coronavirus vaccine trial in Brazil has died, marking the world's first death from a COVID-19 vaccine trial.</p><p>The deceased was a 28-year-old man from Rio de Janeiro who died of complications from COVID-19, <a href="https://g1.globo.com/jornal-nacional/noticia/2020/10/21/voluntario-brasileiro-que-participava-de-testes-de-vacina-morre-por-complicacoes-da-covid.ghtml">Brazil's O Globo newspaper</a> and <a href="https://www.cnnbrasil.com.br/saude/2020/10/21/anvisa-e-notificada-de-morte-de-voluntario-em-testes-de-vacina-de-oxford">CNN Brasil</a> reported Wednesday.</p><p>Both outlets reported that he had volunteered to take part in AstraZeneca's clinical trials for its vaccine, which is being developed with the Oxford Vaccine Group.</p><p>According to O Globo, the man had been in the trial's control group and was given a placebo instead of the trial vaccine. Anvisa, Brazil's health body, said it was told of the death on Monday, CNN Brasil reported. The agency has declined to reveal the participant's identity.</p>

<p>The Oxford Vaccine Group has told Business Insider that the trial will continue.</p><p>"Following careful assessment of this case in Brazil, there have been no concerns about safety of the clinical trial and the independent review in addition to the Brazilian regulator have recommended that the trial should continue," said Joanna Bagniewska, a spokeswoman for the group.</p><p>
