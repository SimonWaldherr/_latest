<p>Andy Burnham, the mayor of Greater Manchester, has said he won't "roll over" and place the region under Tier 3 restrictions at the "sight of a cheque".</p>
<p>Last week, Mr Burnham called for more financial support for local businesses if the region was to go into tighter restrictions.&#160;</p>
<p>Prime Minister Boris Johnson is expected to offer Manchester's leaders up to &#163;100 million to accept the measures or risk having them imposed against their will.</p>
<p>Speaking on Sky News Mr Burnham said: "Tier 3 is a serious thing, it's a serious development if you have to ask people to go into just before winter, just before Christmas...</p>
<p>"People can't just be pressurised into it and I'm not going to be pressurised into it.</p>
<p>"And I'm not just going to roll over at the sight of a cheque, this is about properly supporting those people whose lives will be damaged by Tier 3."</p>
<p>It follows a warning by regional BMA committee chair, Dr Indy Kapila, that Greater Manchester could soon run out of intensive care beds as admissions continue to rise.</p>
