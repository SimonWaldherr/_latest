 <p>     </p> <p>The North West saw the highest rise in hospital fatalities of all regions with 57 deaths, followed by 34 in the North East and Yorkshire.</p> <p>Today's figure is a sharp increase from last Tuesday, when the Department of Health announced 50 deaths in all settings. A fortnight ago, on October 6, that number was 19.</p> <p>Today the Office for National Statistics (ONS) revealed that 40% of all the <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/coronavirus-1">coronavirus</a> deaths in England and Wales are occurring in the north west. </p> <p>A total of 438 deaths in England and Wales registered in the week ending October 9 mentioned Covid-19 on the death certificate, up from 321 the previous week.</p> <p>Of these 153 occurred in the North West.</p> <p>It is the highest number of registered deaths involving Covid-19 since the week ending July 3.</p> <p>The grim latest figures come as tense discussions between leaders in Manchester and the central government ended without agreement this afternoon.</p> <p><a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/boris-johnson">Boris Johnson</a> spoke to Greater Manchester mayor Andy Burnham as the deadline for agreeing a deal on tougher coronavirus controls passed without an official announcement. </p>

 <p>The Prime Minister is expected to set out the Government's next steps at a Downing Street press conference later on Tuesday after talks continued beyond midday.</p> <p>It's expected Tier 3 will be imposed on the Greater Manchester region.</p> <p>Last night, Health Secretary Matt Hancock named Nottinghamshire, South and West Yorkshire, the North East and Teesside as other areas potentially at risk of Tier 3 rules.</p> <p>Registered deaths involving Covid-19 increased week-on-week in all but two regions of England in the week to October 9.</p> <p>The exceptions were Eastern England and the South East.</p>

 <p>In Wales the weekly total increased from 25 to 37.</p> <p>The ONS figures show that, since the week ending September 4, registered coronavirus deaths have been roughly doubling every fortnight.</p> <p>The ONS has also put statistics out that show just over 59,000 deaths involving Covid-19 have now been registered in the UK since the pandemic first hit.</p> <p>ONS figures show that 53,863 deaths involving Covid-19 had occurred in England and Wales up to October 9, and had been registered by October 17.</p> <p class="loading-circle primary-theme-spinner"></p>
<p class="my-logo primary-loader-icon"></p>
<p>Figures published last week by the National Records for Scotland showed that 4,301 deaths involving Covid-19 had been registered in Scotland up to October 11, while 915 deaths had occurred in Northern Ireland up to October 9 (and had been registered up to October 14), according to the Northern Ireland Statistics and Research Agency.</p> <p>Together, these figures mean that so far 59,079 deaths have been registered in the UK where Covid-19 was mentioned on the death certificate, including suspected cases.</p></div>
