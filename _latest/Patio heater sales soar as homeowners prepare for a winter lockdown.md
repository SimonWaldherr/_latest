<div><p>Homeowners are getting ready for a winter lockdown sending sales of patio heaters and pizza ovens through the roof.</p>
<p>Under the new tier rules, those in tier two can only <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/family/life/can-meet-mix-household-stay-overnight-travel-friends-family-covid-tiers/">mix with other households</a> outdoors, leading many to take steps to get their garden ready for Covid-compliant socialising.</p>
<p>Home improvements were a common theme of the first lockdown, but the tropical temperatures meant that the garden was more likely to be ignored in favour of home offices and kitchen goods.</p>
<p>However, with the chillier weather being matched with further coronavirus restrictions and a possible circuit-breaker lockdown on the cards in the winter months, home owners are keen to make sure their houses are ready.</p>
<p>Sales of outdoor heating products at John Lewis were up by a staggering 1,625 per cent last week compared to the same week in 2019. The retailer said this was a record for the time of year.</p>
<p>Similarly, sales of pizza ovens, a popular garden accessory for the well off, were also up by 348 per cent.</p>
