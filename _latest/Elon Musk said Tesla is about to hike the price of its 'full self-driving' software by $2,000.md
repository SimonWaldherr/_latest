<p>Tesla is about to raise the price of its "full self-driving" software by about $2,000, CEO Elon Musk announced on Thursday.</p><p>The price of the assisted driving add-on will jump on Monday, October 26, Musk said on Twitter. The price hike will apply to both new vehicles and upgrades to cars customers have already bought.</p><p>After the price hike, the tech will cost $10,000.&#160;</p><p>It follows the <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/tesla-full-self-driving-software-release-beta-questions-2020-10?r=US&amp;IR=T">limited beta release</a> of the software on Tuesday.</p>

<p><a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/us-tesla-autopilot/u-s-senator-slams-teslas-misleading-name-for-autopilot-driver-assistance-system-idUSKBN1ZN20N">Despite the name of the feature</a>, full self-driving isn't fully autonomous. It is an assisted driving add-on to most Tesla vehicles, and can navigate highways, change lanes, and park your car. Tesla has warned drivers to only use it if they pay attention to the road and keep their hands on the wheel.</p><p>In a <a href="https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1319172039450775554">follow-up tweet</a>, Musk appeared to indicate the price would only rise in the US.</p><p>The rollout of the feature will be "extremely slow &amp; cautious," Musk tweeted Tuesday, and had been delayed because the electric vehicle company "had to rewrite everything."</p><p><strong>Read more:</strong> <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/the-coronavirus-pandemic-is-the-best-thing-for-teslas-business-2020-10?r=gd-sub">The coronavirus pandemic is the best thing that has ever happened to Tesla's business</a></p>

<p>This is just the latest in a series of price increases for the tech. Tesla raised the price by $1,000 <a href="https://twitter.com/elonmusk/status/1189350135614099457?s=20">in November 2019</a>, and then again <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/elon-musk-tesla-full-self-driving-price-increase-july-1-2020-5?r=US&amp;IR=T">in July</a>.</p><p>Musk has warned that the price will continue to rise as the software gets closer to full self-driving capability.
