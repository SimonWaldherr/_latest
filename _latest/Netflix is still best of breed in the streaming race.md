<div><p>But in-keeping with the fickle standards of Silicon Valley, where the next Facebook can quickly become another MySpace or Friends Reunited, Netflix is already in danger of being&#160;written off as another &#8220;has-been&#8221; in some&#160;quarters.</p>
<p>Some 2.2m new subscribers were notched up between July to the end of September, hardly a disaster on the face of it, but almost tiny compared to 16m and 10m in the first and second quarters respectively, and way below the 3.4m analysts had predicted.</p>
<p>When it comes to Netflix, Wall Street is fixated on this as a measure of success, hence the 6pc dive in its share price after the numbers were published. A warning that subscriber additions were &#8220;likely to be down&#8221; in the first half of next year, compared with 2020, won&#8217;t have helped investors&#8217; nerves either.</p>
<p>Yet, the previous two quarters were always going to be an aberration as <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/technology/2020/07/17/have-reached-peak-netflix/">people rushed to sign up during lockdown</a>.</p>
<p>Netflix has said repeatedly it was likely to be a one-off event. &#8220;Quarter-to-quarter fluctuations&#8221; are &#8220;not that meaningful in the context of the long run adoption of internet entertainment&#8221;, it said.</p>
