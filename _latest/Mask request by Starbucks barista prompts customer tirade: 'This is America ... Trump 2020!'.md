<div><p class="endmarkEnabled">A San Diego-area barista is speaking out after she was seen in a viral video calmly serving a customer screaming about coronavirus mask rules and Black Lives Matter.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The video of the incident Saturday at a Starbucks in Santee, about 18 miles northeast of San Diego, shows a customer at the counter with a mask pulled down below her chin.</p><p></p><p id="taboolaReadMoreBelow"></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">When the video starts, the woman appears to be in the middle of a sentence and is speaking calmly to barista Alex Beckom, who is Black. "When you talk to me like that, I know it's because you're discriminating against me because I'm a Trump supporter," the customer says. "F--- Black Lives Matter. I need a straw."</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Beckom, 19, gives her a straw: "Here you are."</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"Thank you," the customer says. "And I need sugar, too, please, the raw sugar, like, two packets, please."</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">As Beckom fetches packets of sugar for the customer, she says: "The next time you come in, I need you to keep a mask on."</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"Its not a law," the customer says, her voice growing louder.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"It's a hoax, I don't have to wear a mask, I'm not going to wear a mask," she shouts. "This is America. I don't have to do what you say! Trump 2020!"</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"You need to leave, thank you" Beckom says.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The woman starts to walk out of the store, but spins around and shouts back through the slightly ajar door: "F--- Black Lives Matter!"</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">NBC News does not know what occurred before the events shown in the video.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled"><a href="https://www.nbcsandiego.com/news/local/santee-barista-brushes-off-racist-anti-mask-tirade-from-customer-who-to-cover-up/2427377/" target="_blank">NBC San Diego</a> reported that the customer reportedly had pulled down her mask to speak to another barista.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Beckom told the station that when she asked the woman to keep the mask on in the store, she complied at first, but then pulled it down once more.</p><p class="lazyload-placeholder"></p><p class="caption__container">Alex Beckom, a Starbucks barista in Santee, Calif., was scolded by a customer who refused to keep her face mask on.</p><p class="caption__source">NBC San Diego</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">&#8220;I&#8217;m Black in America, so I&#8217;ve dealt with these kinds of situations before, so I am able to stay calm and collected in these types of situations because I don't want to risk my job," she said.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"Even the president got [Covid-19], so I don't understand how now it's something that's turned into such a them or or me situation when it should be everyone coming together to make sure we all don't get sick or get hurt," Beckom said.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">In a statement, a Starbucks spokesperson said, "This behavior is not welcome in our stores.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"Our focus right now is providing support to our partner who demonstrated tremendous composure during a very difficult interaction."</p></div>
