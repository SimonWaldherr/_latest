<div><p class="endmarkEnabled"><a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/coronavirus" target="_blank">Covid-19</a> cases across the globe hit 40 million Monday with the United States leading the world with the highest numbers of infections and deaths.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Nearly 10 months after President Donald Trump first downplayed the danger of Covid-19 and tried to reassure the American people that <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/white-house/mismanagement-missed-opportunities-how-white-house-bungled-coronavirus-response-n1158746" target="_blank">&#8220;hopefully, everything&#8217;s going to be great,&#8221; </a>nearly 8.2 million Covid-19 infections had been reported in the U.S., along with more than 220,000 deaths, according to <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/health/health-news/coronavirus-deaths-u-s-map-shows-number-fatalities-compared-confirmed-n1166966" target="_blank">the latest NBC News figures.</a></p><p></p><p id="taboolaReadMoreBelow"></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">And with the presidential election now just 15 days away and the Trump administration under fire for its handling of the pandemic, the number of new Covid-19 cases was climbing again toward what epidemiologists were calling a &#8220;third peak&#8221; of infections <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/covid-cases-continue-climb-almost-every-state-u-s-braces-n1243848" target="_blank">in just about every state.</a></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Many of the new infections have been in <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/covid-catching-kansas-numbers-are-getting-worse-n1243548" target="_blank">Midwestern and Mountain</a> states that weren&#8217;t hit as badly as the Northeast and the West Coast in the early days of the Covid-19 crisis, the latest figures show.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">"We are really struggling," Dr. Todd Vento, director of the Infectious Diseases TeleHealth Program of the Utah-based Intermountain Healthcare, said on NBC's "TODAY" show. "People are doing heroic work, but they are really getting to the point where it's going to be literally unsustainable."</p><a href="/news/us-news/world-surpasses-40-million-covid-19-cases-u-s-leads-n1243889#anchor-Inothercoronavirusnews" id="anchor-Inothercoronavirusnews" class="scrollLink"><h4 class="">In other coronavirus news:</h4></a><p class="endmarkEnabled">Worldwide, there were 40.1 million confirmed Covid-19 cases and 1.1 million coronavirus deaths as of Monday afternoon, according to the <a href="https://coronavirus.jhu.edu/map.html" target="_blank">Johns Hopkins University Covid-19 dashboard.</a></p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">India (7.6 million cases), Brazil (5.2 million cases) and Russia (1.4 million cases) had the next highest total number of coronavirus infections after the U.S.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The virus itself is believed to have originated in December in Wuhan, China, which has reported 90,989 infections, according to the dashboard.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">China also appears to be the first major economy to be recovering from the devastation wrought by the pandemic, reporting that its economy grew by <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2020/10/19/china-economy-q3-gdp-2020.html" target="_blank">4.9 percent </a>between July and September compared to the same quarter last year.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">By contrast, the U.S. economy under Trump has been mired in the doldrums as it struggles to recover from the worst downturn since the Great Depression.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">White House trade adviser Peter Navarro, appearing on Fox News, said Monday he doesn&#8217;t &#8220;believe the Chinese numbers,&#8221; and accused China of using the virus to &#8220;expand their economy, extend their geopolitical reach.&#8221;</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The most recent surge in new Covid-19 cases worldwide was being driven by Europe, which was reporting new cases of the coronavirus at a rate of 187 new cases per million people, the <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/2020/10/16/coronavirus-updates-latest-news-on-the-covid-19-pandemic.html" target="_blank">latest CNBC analysis</a> showed.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The rate in the U.S. was 167 new cases per million residents, the analysis also showed.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">As Europe was being battered by the dreaded second wave of the pandemic, governments there were facing sometimes violent pushback from residents as they tried to re-impose restrictions aimed at slowing the spread.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">In normally peaceful Prague, the capital of the Czech Republic, police used water cannons and pepper spray to <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/video/anti-coronavirus-lockdown-protest-descends-into-violence-in-prague-94103621819" target="_blank">clear demonstrators protesting re-imposed lockdown measures</a> from the city&#8217;s historic Old Town Square.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">In neighboring Slovakia, some 500 people <a href="https://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-54585828" target="_blank">described by the BBC</a> as &#8220;neo-Nazis and hardcore football fans&#8221; pelted the main government building in Bratislava with bottles and stones.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The bustling streets of Paris and eight other French cities were deserted Sunday night as a new overnight curfew went into effect.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Switzerland made mask wearing in public places mandatory and the Dutch royal family was shamed into cutting short a vacation in Greece, which they embarked on after the Netherlands imposed a partial lockdown and urged residents not to travel.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">&#8220;We see people&#8217;s reaction to media reports,&#8221; King Willem-Alexander and Queen Maxima said in a statement. &#8220;They are intense, and they affect us. We do not want to leave any doubt about it: to get the Covid-19 virus under control, it is necessary to follow the guidelines. The discussion of our holiday does not contribute to that."</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">In Poland, conservative <a href="https://uk.reuters.com/article/health-coronavirus-poland-kaczynski-idUKL8N2HA3QO" target="_blank">powerbroker Jaroslaw Kaczynski </a>became the latest big name European politician to announce he was <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/live/2020/10/19/world/covid-coronavirus?action=click&amp;pgtype=Article&amp;state=default&amp;module=styln-coronavirus&amp;region=MAIN_CONTENT_1&amp;context=storyline_latest_updates_recirc#polands-de-facto-leader-goes-into-quarantine-as-the-country-fights-a-virus-resurgence" target="_blank">going into quarantine</a> after he reported coming into contact with a coronavirus-infected person.</p></div>
