<div><p>President <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/donald-trump" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Donald Trump</a> sought to insult former Vice President&#160;<a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/joe-biden" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Joe Biden</a> on Sunday by saying that his Democratic opponent would listen to scientists if elected president.</p><p>Amid a global pandemic that&#8217;s claimed more than 219,000 lives in the U.S., Trump mocked Biden during a campaign rally in Carson City, Nevada, which around 5,000 mostly unmasked supporters attended.</p><p>&#8220;If you vote for Biden, he will surrender your jobs to China. He will surrender your future to the virus. He&#8217;s going to lock down, he&#8217;s going to want us to lock down,&#8221; Trump said.</p><p>&#8220;He&#8217;ll listen to the scientists,&#8221; Trump continued.&#160;&#8220;If I listened totally to the scientists, we would right now have a country that would be in a massive depression, instead of, we&#8217;re like a rocket ship. Take a look at the numbers.&#8221;&#160;</p><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" align="center"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">Trump mocks Biden for pledging to listen to scientists. <p>Throughout the COVID-19 pandemic, Trump has dismissed and disputed science, sent mixed messaging to the public, ignored his own government&#8217;s guidance, feuded with public health experts and mocked others for following the rules &#8213; even after he and dozens of people in his circle tested positive.</p><p>To date, the U.S. has the highest COVID-19 case count and death toll of any country in the world, and the rates of new infections and deaths continue to climb.</p><p>Biden campaign spokesperson Andrew Bates responded to Trump&#8217;s comments in a statement <a href="https://www.axios.com/trump-warns-lockdowns-if-biden-elected-scientists-c1263b65-15c9-47a1-9d6b-63161f6c5170.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">to Axios.</a></p><p>&#8220;Donald Trump tanked the strong economy he inherited from the Obama-Biden administration by continually discounting and attacking warnings from the scientific and medical experts working around the clock to save lives,&#8221; Bates said. &#8220;Now new coronavirus cases are surging and layoffs are rising.&#8221;</p><p>While the <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/business/2020/oct/02/us-unemployment-numbers-elections-economy-jobs#:~:text=The%20US%20unemployment%20rate%20fell,pandemic%20shut%20down%20the%20US." target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">unemployment rate rebounded</a> over the summer as local economies reopened, economists said the pace of recovery has slowed. As the number of coronavirus cases began to climb in the fall, major companies <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/01/business/economy/layoffs-unemployment-claims.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">announced</a> tens of thousands of layoffs. The unemployment rate is currently higher than it was when Trump took office.</p><p>&#160;</p><p>&#160;</p><p class="content-list-component embed-asset yr-content-list-embed"></p>
