<div><p>NYPD detectives are on the hunt for the vandal who endangered cops and the public by cutting the brake line on a police radio car that was later involved in an accident.</p>
<p>The Transit District 20 vehicle was involved in a fender-bender on Oct. 7, when it rear-ended a car at the corner of Queens Boulevard and Union Turnpike, causing minor damage and resulting in no injuries, sources told The Post Tuesday.</p>
<p>The car was taken out of service after the wreck, as is standard practice for official police vehicles damaged in the line of duty.</p>
<p>But when mechanics examined the car at a Queens garage on Tuesday, they determined that the brake line had been intentionally cut, the sources said.</p>
<p>Cops are now trying to find the fiend who pulled off the dangerous stunt.</p>
