<div><p>Behind the scenes, experts are increasingly concerned that their views and recommendations are being ignored as <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2020/09/25/uk-cases-covid-19-deaths-coronavirus-numbers-today/">case numbers rise</a> and panic sets in.</p>
<p>Take&#160;<a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2020/09/25/give-pubs-restaurants-drinking-time-curfew-hospitality-board/">the 10pm curfew</a>. The Telegraph understands that Sage did not include such a cut-off in its list of recommendations put forward to bring down case numbers &#8211; but Downing Street pressed ahead with it anyway.</p>
<p>Government sources say the requirement was added based on "back of the fag packet" calculations, without any scientific research or modelling to back them up.</p>
<p>Scientists on Sage are said to be irritated that they are being blamed for restrictions that they did not recommend, and are calling for Number 10 to publish their original recommendations.&#160;</p>
<p>Asked by The Telegraph whether we could see the Sage advice that prompted the new restrictions (watch a pub boss reacting to the curfew in the video below), the Government responded: "The advice will be published online in due course as part of our regular publications.&#160;We continue to publish Sage minutes and available evidence when they are no longer under live consideration for policy decisions."</p>
