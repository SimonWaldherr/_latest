Democratic presidential candidate Joe Biden has a 9-point lead over President Donald Trump in the crucial swing state of Michigan less than three weeks before the election.

According to a new Hill-Harvard poll conducted by HarrisX, 54% of voters say they support the former vice president compared to 43% of voters who support the president.

Trump narrowly carried Michigan in the 2016 election against then-Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton, winning by less than a percentage point.

The Hill-Harvard poll found that Biden is also leading in Pennsylvania by 5 points, 51% to Trump's 46%. The two candidates are tied with 48% support in Florida. Trump also won both of those states in 2016.
