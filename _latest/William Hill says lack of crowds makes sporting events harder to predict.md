<div><p>Home advantage at sporting events has become the latest victim of the pandemic after William Hill said the lack of crowds at fixtures&#160;is making them harder to predict.</p>
<p>The FTSE 250&#160;gambling giant said&#160;results had been "more unpredictable than normal" due to events being played behind closed doors, and it expects volatile gross win margins to continue for some time.&#160;</p>
<p>It came as the bookmaker&#160;warned that further local lockdowns would hit core revenues, as it reported a decline in third-quarter sales.</p>
<p>The company, which is set to be acquired by Las Vegas&#160;casino titan Caesars Entertainment for &#163;2.9bn, estimated&#160;that shutting 100 betting shops for four weeks due to&#160;local lockdowns would reduce core earnings by around &#163;2m.&#160;</p>
<p>On Wednesday, around 10pc of its UK retail estate was located in regions under Tier 3 restrictions where betting shops have been forced to close, it said.&#160;</p>
<p>William Hill posted a 9pc slump in net revenue for the three months to the end of September &#8211; an improvement on the 32pc fall it reported at the end of the first half.</p>
