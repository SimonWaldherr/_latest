 <p>     </p> <p>Businessman Jones then used a forklift to move Mr O'Leary into his Audi A6 before staging his death as a suicide and burning his body.</p> <p>Mr O'Leary's wife Sian admitted there were "issues" in their marriage - but despite his affair she still loved him.</p> <p>Judge Mrs Justice Nerys Jefford said: "Michael O'Leary did something wrong. He had an affair with a wife of a man who had been his friend for many years but he did not deserve to pay for that with his life."</p> <p>In an emotional address to the court, Mr O'Leary's son Wayne called Jones a "coward" and a "monster".</p>

He hasn't shown any remorse - if he had he would have left us with more than a few pieces of bone.</p> <p>"You sent us on a wild goose chase believing dad had taken his own life all the while thinking you had done a good job.</p>

 <p>Swansea Crown Court heard gun enthusiast Jones had discovered "intimate" sexual messages between Mr O'Leary and his wife Rhianon.</p> <p>Prosecutor William Hughes QC said: "Mike O'Leary was lured to this location expecting a private meeting with Rhianon Jones. </p> <p>"Instead he was met by this defendant who was armed with a powerful rifle which he used to deliberately shoot him dead.</p> <p>"Steps were subsequently taken to hide and cover up his crimes - not in panic but a clear and calculated approach.</p> <p>"This was a carefully planned and well thought-out murder far from an accident."</p>

<p>Building and farming boss Jones claimed Mr O'Leary's death was an accident and he "panicked" when the gun went off during a struggle before burning his body in a series of fires.</p> <p>Mr O'Leary was shot dead at the farm near Nantgaredig, Carmarthenshire before Jones dumped Mr O'Leary's car by a river to stage his disappearance as suicide.</p> <p>He put on Mr O'Leary's shoes and walked down to river to make it look like he had jumped in.</p> <p>Jones also used Mr O'Leary's phone to send text messages to his wife Sian and three sons saying "I'm so sorry."</p> <p>Jones, of Bronwydd, Carmarthen, must serve a minimum of 30 years before he can be released.</p></div>
