<div><h2 class="css-1xhqoos e4t6qsa0" id="link-NaN"></h2><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">It&#8217;s not just the public polls. Recent private polls conducted by political campaigns are filled with bad news for President Trump. He is doing eight to 10 percentage points worse in many congressional districts than he did in 2016, <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://politicalwire.com/2020/10/21/house-districts-full-of-danger-signs-for-trump/" title="" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Dave Wasserman of The Cook Political Report writes</a>.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">His struggles have jeopardized the Republicans&#8217; Senate majority and will probably lead to further Democratic gains in the House. &#8220;It would be a pleasant surprise if we only lost 10 House seats,&#8221; one Republican member of Congress told The Cook Political Report.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">But there is one exception, and it will be <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/09/18/briefing/joe-biden-cdc-amy-dorris-your-friday-briefing.html" title="">familiar</a> to regular readers of this newsletter: Trump and other Republicans don&#8217;t seem to be doing worse among Latino voters than in 2016. Nationwide, Republicans are still winning about one-third of the Latino vote, polls show.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">As a result, Trump still has a good chance to win both Florida and Texas. Similarly, Senator John Cornyn of Texas continues to lead narrowly in <a class="css-1g7m0tk" href="https://www.texastribune.org/2020/10/15/mj-hegar-senate-majority-pac/" title="" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">his own re-election race</a>, and House Republicans could hold onto districts in California, Florida and Texas.</p></div>
