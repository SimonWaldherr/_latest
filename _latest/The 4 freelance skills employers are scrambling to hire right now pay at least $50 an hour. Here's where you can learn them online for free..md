<p>According to data from the freelancing platform <a href="https://www.upwork.com">Upwork</a>, demand for <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/the-new-rules-of-freelancing-how-to-pitch-work-writing-pandemic">freelance workers</a> has risen dramatically in the last several months, especially in industries whose emphasis is on facilitating the shift to digital, remote work.</p><p>Upwork, which specializes in placing remote freelancers, <a href="https://www.upwork.com/press/2020/06/11/fourth-annual-future-workforce-report/">conducted a survey in June</a> of more than 1,500 US hiring managers. Nearly half said they're more likely to hire freelancers, and 72% are continuing or increasing their use of independent professionals, <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/upwork-ceo-shares-how-you-can-become-a-successful-freelancer-2020-9">according to prior reporting from Business Insider</a>.</p><p>Hayden Brown, the president and CEO of Upwork, told Business Insider that one of the best ways to find success as a freelancer is to discover what skill sets employers are looking for and then become proficient in those areas.&#160;</p><p>In recent months, <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/research-suggests-remote-work-hitting-managers-the-hardest-how-to-manage-teams">employer interest in several fields</a> has risen, including network security, marketing strategy, ecommerce, and email &amp; marketing automation, said Brown.</p><p>"Companies are adopting dynamic ways of flexing with their teams, figuring out how to bring full-time employees and skilled, independent workers together for projects," <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/upwork-ceo-shares-how-you-can-become-a-successful-freelancer-2020-9">Brown told Business Insider</a>. "People are starting to break down those old barriers of how they use to hire, or who they use to hire."</p><p>Below is a list of the most-popular, high-earning industries on Upwork, as well as where prospective freelancers can go to learn the skills they need to break into those lines of work.</p><p>
