<div><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Mike "Doc" Emrick, a Hall of Fame hockey announcer, is retiring as a broadcaster Monday.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">The 74-year-old Emrick has called more than 3,750 games over a 47-year career.&#160;He's been the main voice for NHL games on NBC since moving to the network in a full-time capacity in 2011. He was the New Jersey Devils play-by-play announcer for 21 seasons prior to that.</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"I hope I can handle retirement OK, especially since I've never done it before," <a href="https://nypost.com/2020/10/19/mike-doc-emrick-retiring-from-legendary-nhl-broadcasting-career/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">Emrick told the New York Post</a>. "But I've just been extremely lucky for 50 years. And NBC has been so good to me, especially since the pandemic, when I was allowed to work from home in a studio NBC created.</p><img class="gnt_em_img_i" alt='Mike "Doc" Emrick during the 2019 Stanley Cup Final.'><p class="gnt_em_img_ccw gnt_em_img_ccw__cap gnt_em_img_ccw__crd"></p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">"Now, into my golden years, this just seemed to be the time that was right."</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">In Emrick's hockey calling career he covered 22 Stanley Cups and <a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/nhl/2019/06/12/doc-emrick-game-7-call-bruins-blues-stanley-cup-final/1429291001/" target="_blank" rel="noopener" class="gnt_ar_b_a">45 Game 7s</a>. He previously spent time with ESPN, ABC, Fox and CBS for six Olympic Winter Games.&#160;</p><p class="gnt_ar_b_p">Emrick was awarded the Hockey Hall of Fame's Foster Hewitt Memorial Award for outstanding contributions to hockey broadcasting in 2008.</p><a class="gnt_em gnt_em__lp gnt_em_gl gnt_em__el" href="/picture-gallery/sports/nhl/2020/08/11/nhl-playoffs-best-photos-first-round/3343953001/"><img class="gnt_em_gl_i" alt="" width="660" height="372"><p class="gnt_em_gl_i_nt"></p><p class="gnt_em_gl_vg"></p><p class="gnt_em_t gnt_em_t__hb"></p></a></div>
