<div><p>The nation has recovered from the <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/the-report/articles/2017-07-25/how-long-will-the-economic-recovery-last">worst recession</a> &#8211; a steep downturn spanning 2007 to 2009 &#8211; since the Great Depression of the 1930s. Unemployment surpassed 10 percent nationwide at the peak, with joblessness running higher in some states. Since then, unemployment has fallen below 5 percent nationally, though the <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/economy/articles/2017-07-13/study-3-in-10-americans-havent-recovered-from-great-recession">recovery hasn&#8217;t been consistent</a> nationwide. The growth of the nation's <a href="http://www.tradingeconomics.com/united-states/gdp-growth" target="_blank">gross domestic product</a>, 3.2 percent on an annualized basis in the first quarter of 2019, was above that of the previous period.  </p><p>Colorado is the top state for economy. It&#8217;s followed by Utah, and West Coast states Washington, California and Oregon to round out the top five.
