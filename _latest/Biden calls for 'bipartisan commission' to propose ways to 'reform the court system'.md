<div><p class="endmarkEnabled">WASHINGTON &#8212; Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden <a href="https://www.cbsnews.com/news/trump-60-minutes-biden-supreme-court/" target="_blank">told &#8220;60 Minutes&#8221;</a> he would set up a "bipartisan commission of scholars" from across the ideological spectrum to explore ways to change the Supreme Court.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">&#8220;And I will &#8212; ask them to over 180 days come back to me with recommendations as to how to reform the court system because it&#8217;s getting out of whack,&#8221; Biden said, according to excerpts released Thursday by CBS of an interview to air Sunday.</p><p></p><p id="taboolaReadMoreBelow"></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">&#8220;And it&#8217;s not about court packing,&#8221; Biden said. &#8220;There&#8217;s a number of other things that our constitutional scholars have debated and I&#8217;ve looked to see what recommendations that commission might make.&#8221;</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Biden's 180-day timeline for a commission indicates that restructuring the courts wouldn&#8217;t be a priority in the first six months of his presidency, should he win the election.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The answer comes as Biden faces pressure to take a position on the push to <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2020-election/i-m-not-fan-court-packing-biden-responds-after-trump-n1243077" target="_blank">add seats to the Supreme Court</a> in response to what Democrats describe as a theft by Republicans for enabling President Donald Trump to fill a vacancy in an election year after preventing President Barack Obama from doing so in 2016.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The clip was released on the same day that Biden faces Trump in their <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2020-election/final-showdown-5-things-watch-last-trump-biden-debate-n1244074" target="_blank">final debate</a> before Election Day.</p><p></p><p class="endmarkEnabled">It also comes on the same day that a Republican-led committee <a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/congress/senate-judiciary-committee-vote-amy-coney-barrett-supreme-court-nomination-n1244253" target="_blank">voted to advance</a> the nomination of Amy Coney Barrett, a conservative and originalist, to the full Senate to replace the late liberal icon Ruth Bader Ginsburg. Democrats vociferously objected, citing the election in less than two weeks.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">Biden has been caught between a push by progressives to expand the court to rebalance it ideologically, and his pitch to work across the aisle, restore institutions and return to normalcy.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">The Democrat has previously expressed skepticism for &#8220;court packing&#8221; and said he&#8217;s &#8220;not a fan&#8221; of that idea. He has also expressed openness to other ideas to restructure the court, such as altering lifetime tenure of justices.</p><p class="endmarkEnabled">&#8220;There&#8217;s a number of alternatives that are &#8212; go well beyond packing,&#8221; he told &#8220;60 Minutes.&#8221; &#8220;The last thing we need to do is turn the Supreme Court into just a political football, whoever has the most votes gets whatever they want. Presidents come and go. Supreme Court justices stay for generations.&#8221;</p></div>
