<div><p class="speakable">Democratic nominee <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/joe-biden" target="_blank">Joe Biden</a>&#160;said Friday he will &#8220;make clear&#8221; his position on adding justices to the Supreme Court when the Senate votes on the nomination of <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/politics/judiciary/confirmation-of-amy-coney-barrett" target="_blank">Amy Coney Barrett</a>.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="speakable">&#8220;I'm going to make clear my position in the next several days when they vote on this nominee, but I&#8217;ve got to keep the focus on that,&#8221; he said in an interview with <a href="https://www.fox2detroit.com/news/exclusive-biden-says-hell-make-stance-on-packing-supreme-court-clear-when-senate-votes-on-amy-coney-barrett" target="_blank">Fox 2 Detroit</a>.</p><p><strong><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-biden-dueling-town-halls" target="_blank">BIDEN MOVES ON COURT PACKING STANCE, WHILE TRUMP SPARS WITH NBC IN DUELING TOWN HALLS</a></strong></p><p>He later said that "when the [Senate Judiciary] Committee makes the decision, and the vote begins to occur, that's when I'll let you know my position.&#8221;&#160;</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Biden suggested Republicans were&#160;&#8220;packing the court&#8221; by filling the vacancy left by the death of Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg with Barrett -- who faced Senate Judiciary Committee hearings this week. Democrats have said Republicans are rushing through the pick too close to Election Day, with millions of Americans already voting.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>"Court packing&#8221; is not normally used to refer to filling vacancies, although a number of Democrats have attempted to use that term to describe the process in recent weeks. Instead, it has traditionally referred to potential politically motiviated plans to expand&#160;the number of justices on the court.</p><p>It was attempted unsuccessfully by President Franklin Delano Roosevelt in 1937 to force through parts of his New Deal that were ruled unconstitutional by the high court, and has been a fringe idea for years. It has been condemned in the past by both Biden and Ginsburg.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p><strong><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/biden-court-packing-stance-1983-bonehead-idea" target="_blank">BIDEN IS SILENT NOW ON COURT-PACKING, BUT IN 1983 HE CALLED IT A 'BONEHEAD' IDEA</a></strong></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>The notion, however, has gained traction&#160;on the left since Republicans refused to hold hearings for Barack Obama nominee Merrick Garland in 2016. A number of Democrats in the presidential primary backed court packing, although Biden rejected the idea at the time.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>In recent days, Biden has been evasive on the subject. Last week, he said &#8220;no, they don&#8217;t&#8221; <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/biden-voters-deserve-packing-supreme-court" target="_blank">when asked</a> if voters deserve to know his stance. This week, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/trump-biden-dueling-town-halls" target="_blank">he changed</a> his tone and said voters &#8220;do have a right to know where I stand.&#8221;</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p><strong><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/apps-products?pid=AppArticleLink">CLICK HERE TO GET THE FOX NEWS APP</a></strong></p><p>But in the interview with <a href="https://www.fox2detroit.com/news/exclusive-biden-says-hell-make-stance-on-packing-supreme-court-clear-when-senate-votes-on-amy-coney-barrett" target="_blank">Fox 2 Detroit</a>, he did not give a clear answer when asked when court packing might be&#160;appropriate.</p><p>&#8220;As you know, I've not been a fan of packing the court but what might happen is that we have to take a look at how this all works out and determine whether there's other means by which we should take a look at how to make sure that this kind of thing doesn&#8217;t happen,&#8221; he said.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p></div>
