<div><p>Prime Minister Boris Johnson will offer Manchester's leaders up to &#163;100 million on Monday to accept <a href="https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/0/manchester-latest-local-lockdown-rules-currently-tier-2/">Tier-3&#160;coronavirus&#160;restrictions</a> or risk having them imposed against their will.</p>
<p>Andy Burnham, the Mayor of Greater Manchester, described talks with Downing Street on Sunday as &#8220;constructive&#8221;, increasing speculation&#160;he might be ready to do a deal.</p>
<p>The Prime Minister wants to avoid imposing Tier-3&#160;status on Manchester without local consent, and Government sources said talks with its leaders could take &#8220;days&#8221;.</p>
<p>Ministers believe&#160; if the situation in Manchester continues to worsen, Mr Burnham will come under increasing pressure locally to accept tighter measures for the sake of public health.</p>
<p>Mr Johnson has said he will intervene to impose Tier-3&#160;status if there is no agreement.</p>
