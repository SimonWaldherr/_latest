<div><p class="sc-pHJJA iocNwz above-article-prompt-donations"></p><p>A nine-year-old climate change activist in <a href="/topic/india">India</a> was detained by <a href="/topic/police">police</a> in <a href="/topic/new-delhi">New Delhi</a>, according to reports.</p><p>Licypriya Kangujam, and fellow activist Aarav Seth, aged 12, were picked up by officers at the weekend after protesting about <a href="/topic/air-pollution">air pollution</a> outside the Parliament House in the capital. New Delhi has among the worst air quality in the world and the city is often blanketed in thick smog.</p><p></p><p>Licypriya shared the news that she had been &#8220;briefly" held by police on her <a href="/topic/twitter">Twitter</a> account.&#160;</p><p></p><p id="article-im-prompt"></p><p>According to a series of posts, she and Aarav were put in the back of a police truck and taken to an &#8220;undisclosed location&#8221;. They were held for around 40 minutes, according to the protesters.&#160;</p><p></p><p>&#8220;Instead of stopping air pollution, they&#8217;re trying to stop me,&#8221; Ms Kangujam tweeted.</p><p>Licypriya has been a climate activist since she was seven years old and has received a World Children Peace Prize as well as an India Peace Prize.&#160;</p><p>Sharing pictures of the poor air quality in the city, she said: "This is not the time to stay silent. I will continue my protest in front of the Parliament House. 21 out of 30 most polluted cities is in India.&#160;</p><p></p><p>&#8220;No concrete action yet. Instead of blame game, we want urgent climate action now with permanent solution to fight the air pollution.&#8221;</p><p>She also offered to provide a session on air pollution and the climate crisis for New Delhi police officers.&#160;</p><p class="sc-oTaAA eBoKzo"></p><p>Indian news site News18 reported that the young activists were not arrested but let off with a warning, and told if they returned to protest outside Parliament House they would be detained.&#160;</p><p id="taboola-carousel-thumbnails"></p><p>The police arrested the children because they had been protesting at a &#8220;high-security zone&#8221;, according to the network. However Licypriya wrote on Twitter that she held a demonstration at the building each week.&#160;</p><p>Indian citizens, fellow activists and scientists shared their outrage at the detention of the young activists.&#160;</p><p>Jean-Pascal van Ypersele, a vice-chair with the UN&#8217;s Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change and a climate physicist, tweeted: &#8220;Licypriya, who just turned 9 year-old, was arrested on October 18 in India. Her crime? Asking for more climate action! Please support her, listen to her, and follow her! She also has a nice sense of humour... #FaceTheClimateEmergency.&#8221;</p><p>According to <a rel="nofollow" href="https://www.iqair.com/newsroom/air-pollution-far-deadlier-coronavirus-new-data-ranks-health-threat-cities-worst-best">IQAir</a>, a real-time air quality information platform, New Delhi has the worst air pollution of any capital city. Some 1.25 million people are killed by air pollution in India every year.</p><p class="sc-ptCms fzKffT"></p></div>
