<div><p>The <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries">2020 Best Countries rankings,</a> formed in partnership with BAV Group, a unit of global marketing communications company VMLY&amp;R, and the <a href="https://www.usnews.com/best-graduate-schools/top-business-schools/university-of-pennsylvania-01194">Wharton School</a> of the University of Pennsylvania, are based on a study that surveyed more than 20,000 global citizens from four regions to assess perceptions of 73 countries on 75 different metrics. The <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/quality-of-life-rankings">Quality of Life subranking</a> is based on an equally weighted average of scores from nine country attributes that relate to quality of life in a country: affordable, a good job market, economically stable, family friendly, income equality, politically stable, safe, well-developed public education system and <a href="/news/best-countries/slideshows/countries-with-the-most-well-developed-public-health-care-system/">well-developed public health system</a>. The Quality of Life subranking score had a 17% weight in the overall Best Countries ranking.</p><p>People consistently view a small group of nations as best providing for their citizens. For the fifth consecutive year, <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/canada">Canada</a> ranks No. 1 overall for providing a good quality of life. Survey respondents view the North American country as No. 1 for being politically stable, No. 4 for having a well-developed public education system, and No. 2 for having a good job market, a perception <a href="http://www.ncee.org/programs-affiliates/center-on-international-education-benchmarking/top-performing-countries/">supported by independent research.</a> The North American country is seen as possessing the most well-developed public health care system. In fact, Canada is rated in the top 10 in all but one of the nine attributes, affordability, where Asian countries dominate.</p><p>Seven European countries are ranked in the top 10: <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/denmark">Denmark</a>, <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/sweden">Sweden</a> and <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/norway">Norway</a> immediately follow Canada, with <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/australia">Australia, </a><a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/netherlands">the Netherlands, </a><a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/switzerland">Switzerland</a>, <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/new-zealand">New Zealand, </a><a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/finland">Finland</a> and <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/germany">Germany </a>also finishing in the top 10.</p><p>Countries perceived to provide a lower quality of life perform most poorly in areas concerning personal safety and economic opportunity., followed by <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/iran">Iran</a>, <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/lebanon">Lebanon</a>, followed by <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/jordan">Jordan</a>, <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/ukraine">Ukraine</a>, <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/serbia">Serbia</a> and <a href="//www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> are at the bottom of the quality of life ranking. Azerbaijan finishes last for its public education system, while Lebanon places last for safety. </p><p>The <a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/best-countries/united-states">United States</a> ranks No. 15, up two spots from last year, by survey respondents for providing a good quality of life. Even though it placed No. 1 for its job market, the country placed No. 56 for affordability.</p></div>
