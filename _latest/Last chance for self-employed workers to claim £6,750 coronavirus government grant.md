The second grant opened for applications on August 17, 2020 and closes today, October 19, 2020.</p> <h3>Who qualifies for the grant?</h3>

 <p>     </p> <p>To be eligible, you must have filed a tax return for 2018/19 to&#160;HMRC, meaning you must have been self-employed on April 6, 2019.</p> <p>The grants are calculated based on your average profits over three years (ie in 2016/17, 2017/18 and 2018/19).</p> <p>If you weren't in business in 2016/17, it will be based on your average profits in 2017/18 and 2018/19, or otherwise the last tax year.</p> <p>To qualify, you must also earn more than half of your total income from self-employment and your profits must not exceed &#163;50,000 a year.</p> <p>Self-employed workers who are company directors or run their businesses as limited companies&#160;are not eligible for the support.</p> <p><strong> Get the latest money advice, news and help straight to your inbox - sign up at <a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/email">mirror.co.uk/email</a> </strong></p> <h3>How can I apply for the help?</h3> <p> You can make your claim via the <a href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/claim-a-grant-through-the-self-employment-income-support-scheme" rel="nofollow">Gov.uk website</a>. </p> <p>Self-employed workers will need the following information to apply:</p> <ul> <li> Self-assessment unique taxpayer reference (UTR) &#8211; if you do not have this, you can <a href="https://www.gov.uk/find-lost-utr-number" rel="nofollow">find out how to get your lost UTR here</a> </li> <li> National insurance number &#8211; <a href="https://www.gov.uk/lost-national-insurance-number" rel="nofollow">find out how to locate a lost national insurance number</a> </li> <li>Government Gateway user ID and password &#8211; if you do not have a user ID, you can create one when you make your claim online</li> <li>UK bank details (only provide bank account details where a Bacs payment can be accepted).</li> </ul> <p>Applicants must make the claim themselves through HMRC and cannot use an agent or accountant to act on their behalf.</p> <p><strong></strong></p> <p class="loading-circle primary-theme-spinner"></p>

<h3>What happens next?</h3> <p><a href="https://www.mirror.co.uk/all-about/rishi-sunak">Rishi Sunak</a> announced a new self-employed grant&#160;on September 24 for those who are still financially vulnerable. </p> <p>However, the latest scheme will cover just 20% of average monthly profits, up to a total of &#163;1,875, spanning from November to January next year.</p> <p>An additional second grant will be available for self-employed workers from February 2021&#160;to April 30.</p> <p>The government is yet to confirm how much this second grant will cover.</p></div>
