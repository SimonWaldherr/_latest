<div><p class="sc-pHJJA iocNwz above-article-prompt-donations"></p><p><a href="/topic/iran">Iran</a> and <a href="/topic/russia">Russia</a> have used stolen voter registration data to send fake emails to incite unrest and damage the integrity of the 2020 election campaign, according to the US intelligence community.</p><p>In a late press conference, director of National Intelligence <a href="/topic/john-ratcliffe">John Ratcliffe</a> and <a href="/topic/fbi">FBI</a> director <a href="/topic/christopher-wray">Christopher Wray</a> said the two countries had taken specific actions to influence public opinion on the election.
</p><p></p><p>&#8220;They hope they will cause confusion, sow chaos, and undermine your confidence in American democracy,&#8221; Mr Ratcliffe said.
</p><p id="article-im-prompt"></p><p>He said the stolen data was used to send spook emails in an attempt to damage Donald Trump, and send videos that falsely implied individuals could fraudulently cast ballots from overseas.
</p><p></p><p></p><p>Shortly before the announcement, <a rel="nofollow" href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/technology/2020/10/20/proud-boys-emails-florida/">The Washington Post</a> reported that Iran was behind threatening emails sent to Democratic voters this week claiming to be from the &#8220;Proud Boys&#8221;, which said the group was in &#8220;possession of all your information&#8221;.</p><p>&#8220;You will vote for Trump on Election Day or we will come after you,&#8221; the <a rel="nofollow" href="https://twitter.com/glennkirschner2/status/1319065658538266631">emails said</a>. &#8220;Change your party affiliation to Republican to let us know you received our message and will comply. We will know which candidate you voted for. I would take this seriously if I were you.&#8221;</p><p id="taboola-carousel-thumbnails"></p><p>Mr Ratcliff said the actions were a &#8220;desperate attempts by desperate advisories,&#8221; while Mr Wray said it was part of a concerted effort to undermine confidence in the election and make Americans believe their votes don&#8217;t count.
