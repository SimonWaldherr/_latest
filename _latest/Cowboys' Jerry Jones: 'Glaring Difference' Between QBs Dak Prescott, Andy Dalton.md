(AP Photo/Brandon Wade)" src="https://static-assets.bleacherreport.net/img/atoms/lazyImage/logo.png"><p class="credit">Brandon Wade/Associated Press</p><p class="">The <a href="http://bleacherreport.com/dallas-cowboys">Dallas Cowboys</a> played their first full game without injured quarterback Dak Prescott since 2015 on Monday, and the results weren't pretty, with the team dropping a 38-10 home game to the <a href="http://bleacherreport.com/arizona-cardinals">Arizona Cardinals</a>.</p><p class="">On Tuesday, Cowboys owner Jerry Jones said on Dallas radio station 105.3 The Fan (h/t&#160;<a href="https://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/nfl/cowboys/2020/10/20/dallas-cowboys-jerry-jones-andy-dalton-dak-prescott-quarterback-difference/5990991002/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Jori Epstein</a>&#160;of&#160;USA Today)&#160;there was a "pretty glaring difference" between Prescott, who suffered a season-ending dislocation and compound fracture of his right ankle on Oct. 11 against the <a href="http://bleacherreport.com/new-york-giants">New York Giants</a>, and <a href="http://bleacherreport.com/andy-dalton">Andy Dalton</a>, a former backup now thrust into the starter spotlight.&#160;</p><p class="">"That was the pretty glaring difference out there last night," Jones said. "But it doesn't have to stay that way."</p><p id="br-ad-29245129" class="br-ad-lazy"></p><p class="">Dalton completed 34 of his 54 passes for just 266 yards (4.9 yards per pass attempt). He threw a garbage-time touchdown to wide receiver Amari Cooper with 2:48 remaining and also tossed a pair of second-half interceptions.</p><p class="">The week before, Dalton came on in relief of Prescott to complete 9-of-11 passes for 111 yards in a 37-34 win over the New York Giants.</p><p class="">Prescott has largely shined during his five-year career, completing 66.0 percent of his passes for 106 touchdowns and 40 interceptions. Dallas has gone 42-27 in his starts and won the NFC East twice, including his 2016 rookie season, when the team finished 13-3 and earned the conference's No. 1 seed.</p><a rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank" class="molecule thumbnail"><img alt="" src="https://static-assets.bleacherreport.net/img/atoms/lazyImage/logo.png">Video Play Button</a><p class="atom video-indicator"><p class="text">Videos you might like </p> </p><p class="">Per&#160;<a href="https://www.footballoutsiders.com/stats/nfl/qb/2019" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Football Outsiders</a>, Prescott finished first in effective yards,&#160;defense-adjusted yards above replacement (DYAR) and sixth in&#160;defense-adjusted value over average (DVOA) among signal-callers who threw at least 200 passes last year.</p><p class="">Any backup would face a tough task to come anywhere close to replacing Prescott's production, including Dalton, a three-time Pro Bowler who led the Cincinnati Bengals to the playoffs from 2011-2015.&#160;</p><p class="">Dalton has now played well in one game and struggled in another, but the Dallas defense continues to be the team's biggest problem regardless of who's under center.&#160;<a href="https://www.footballoutsiders.com/stats/nfl/team-defense/2020" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Football Outsiders</a>&#160;ranks the unit 28th out of 32 teams in DVOA. The Cowboys have also allowed&#160;<a href="https://www.pro-football-reference.com/years/2020/opp.htm" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">8.0 yards per play</a>, ranking 25th in the NFL.</p><p class="">Regardless of the team's struggles, Dallas is still first in the NFC East at 2-4. The Cowboys will look to get back in the win column when they visit the Washington Football Team on Sunday at 1 p.m. ET.</p></div>
