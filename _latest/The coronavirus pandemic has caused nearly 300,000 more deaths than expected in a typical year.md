<div><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">It is not clear whether that spike is caused by the well-recognized shift in covid-19 deaths toward younger people between May and August, or deaths from other causes, the CDC said.</p><p class="absolute z-0">AD</p><p class=""></p><p class="powa-wrapper"></p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">The report comes with just two weeks left in a presidential campaign whose central issue is President Trump&#8217;s handling of the pandemic. Trump has sought at every turn, <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/trump-fauci-campaign-biden/2020/10/19/30b2fe58-1226-11eb-82af-864652063d61_story.html?itid=lk_inline_manual_7">including in remarks Monday,</a> to minimize the virus&#8217;s impact, despite a covid-19 death toll that is likely to be the third-leading cause of mortality in the United States this year, behind heart disease and cancer. That stance has proven to be the president&#8217;s enduring weakness as the election looms Nov.&#8201;3.</p><p class="absolute z-0">AD</p><p class=""></p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">His Democratic opponent, Joe Biden, has made his plans to tackle the pandemic the major focus of his bid to capture the White House.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">&#8220;The number of people dying from this pandemic is higher than we think,&#8221; said Steven Woolf, director emeritus of the Center on Society and Health at Virginia Commonwealth University, who has conducted independent analyses of excess mortality. &#8220;This study shows it. Others have as well.&#8221;</p><p class="absolute z-0">AD</p><p class=""></p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">The data cover the period Feb.&#8201;1 to Sept.&#8201;16, meaning that excess deaths have almost certainly reached 300,000. Woolf said they are likely to hit 400,000 by the end of the year. The numbers were assembled by the National Center for Health Statistics, a unit of the CDC.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">Outside analyses, including some by <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2020/investigations/coronavirus-excess-deaths-may/?itid=ap_emmabrown&amp;itid=lk_inline_manual_13">The Washington Post and researchers at Yale University</a>, have found two main causes for excess deaths. Many probably were the result of covid-19, although they were not recorded that way on death certificates. Others are probably the result of deaths at home or in nursing homes from heart attacks, diabetes, strokes and Alzheimer&#8217;s disease, among people afraid to seek care in hospitals or unable to get it.</p><p class="absolute z-0">AD</p><p class=""></p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">Overall, the CDC found &#8220;that excess deaths have occurred every week since March, 2020,&#8221; with a peak during the week of April&#8201;11 and another during the week ending Aug.&#8201;8. Those dates roughly coincide with the virus&#8217;s surge into the New York metro area near the start of the outbreak and a second major rise across the Sun Belt when many states reopened too soon in an effort to revive flagging economies.</p><p class="absolute z-0">AD</p><p class=""></p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">The United States is in the midst of another sharp increase in coronavirus infections, this one centered in the upper Midwest and Plains states. The seven-day rolling average of cases, considered the most accurate barometer, is near 60,000 per day. At least 219,000 people have died of covid-19 so far, according to <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2020/national/coronavirus-us-cases-deaths/?itid=hp_pandemic-guide-box&amp;itid=lk_inline_manual_17">data kept by The Washington Post.</a></p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">All told, an estimated 285,404 more people died than would be expected in a typical year, the CDC reported.</p><p class="absolute z-0">AD</p><p class=""></p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">While the virus continues to prey mainly on older people and, disproportionately, African Americans and Latinos, the rate of excess mortality among 25- to 44-year-olds was less expected. Among 45- to 64-year-olds the increase was 14.6&#8201;percent, and among 65- to 74-year-olds it was 24.2&#8201;percent.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md ">The unexpected mortality rate of adults in the prime of their lives, from 25 to 65, has been a source of ongoing concern in recent years, especially since a spike in deaths from drugs, alcohol and suicide was recognized.</p><p class="font--body font-copy gray-darkest ma-0 pb-md bold">
