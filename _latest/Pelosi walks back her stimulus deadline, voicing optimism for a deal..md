<div><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">House Speaker Nancy Pelosi, Democrat of California, said on Tuesday that she was optimistic that a deal could be reached with the Trump administration to pass a new economic relief bill before Election Day, setting aside a deadline she had imposed for reaching a compromise by the end of the day.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">Ms. Pelosi and Steven Mnuchin, the Treasury secretary, were scheduled to discuss the matter at 3 p.m. Tuesday. &#8220;Hopefully, by the end of the day today, we&#8217;ll know where we all are,&#8221; the speaker said in an interview on Bloomberg TV.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">On Sunday, Ms. Pelosi said that to pass a bill before the election, a deal would have to be reached within 48 hours. But in the interview on Tuesday, she softened that time limit, saying instead that legislation would have to be finished by the end of next week for aid to begin flowing by Nov. 3.</p><p class="css-158dogj evys1bk0">&#8220;It isn&#8217;t that this day was the day that we have a deal,&#8221; Ms. Pelosi said. &#8220;It was a day that we would have our terms on the table to be able to go to the next step.&#8221;</p></div>
