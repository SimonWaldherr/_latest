<div><p class="speakable">Republican Sens. <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/ted-cruz" target="_blank">Ted Cruz</a>, <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/lindsey-graham" target="_blank">Lindsey Graham</a> and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/person/h/josh-hawley" target="_blank">Josh Hawley</a> on Thursday called on the heads of Twitter and Facebook to testify, and said a subpoena was in the works, as critics claimed the social media platforms have been censoring reporting critical of Democrats.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="speakable">&#8220;This is election interference and we're 19 days out from an election,&#8221; Cruz, R-Texas,&#160;said. &#8220;It has no precedent in the history of democracy. The Senate Judiciary Committee wants to know what the hell is going on.&#8221;</p><p>The Senate Judiciary Committee leaders announced they will vote on a subpoena Tuesday for Jack Dorsey, the CEO of Twitter, to testify before the committee on Friday, Oct. 23.&#160;Hawley said he hoped the committee would vote to subpoena Facebook CEO Mark&#160;Zuckerberg, too.&#160;</p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/twitter-trump-campaign-account-video-hunter-biden" target="_blank"><strong>TWITTER SAYS TRUMP CAMPAIGN 'VIOLATED' RULES, LOCKS ACCOUNT OVER TWEET REFERENCING HUNTER BIDEN REPORT</strong></a></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>A Facebook spokesperson did not immediately respond to a request for comment, while&#160;Twitter declined to comment on the GOP requests&#160;to appear on Capitol Hill.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>The action comes as both Twitter and Facebook took steps to stop the widespread dissemination of an explosive New York Post&#160;report this week that purports to show emails from Hunter Biden linking his father to his Ukraine business dealings.&#160;</p><img src="https://a57.foxnews.com/static.foxnews.com/foxnews.com/content/uploads/2020/10/640/320/4dorsey-newsletter-xx.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1" alt="Jack Dorsey, Twitter CEO"><p>Republicans accused&#160;the social media platforms of censorship and implied the tech giants were in cahoots with the Biden campaign to suppress a negative story.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>They also made a case for hypocrisy, saying Twitter did nothing to censor&#160;reporting on the Steele dossier that was damaging to President Trump or to flag reporting on Russia collusion allegations. The Republicans pointed to the power the tech giants wield and how their decisions on content can influence voters' understanding of the candidates.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>&#8220;We're going to finally have an accounting that's long overdue,&#8221; Graham, R-S.C., said.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>&#8220;The power behind these platforms have been taken to a level that truly is dangerous," the Judiciary Committee chairman added.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p><a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/republican-twitter-facebook-blackout-hunter-biden" target="_blank"><strong>TRUMP, REPUBLICANS SLAM TWITTER, FACEBOOK 'BLACKOUT' OF HUNTER BIDEN ARTICLE</strong></a></p><p>Twitter took major steps to stop the spread of the story, including&#160;locking&#160;the New York Post's Twitter account Wednesday and suspending the official&#160;account of the Trump campaign for tweeting out a video on Joe Biden that cites the story.</p><img src="https://a57.foxnews.com/static.foxnews.com/foxnews.com/content/uploads/2020/10/640/320/Hunter-Biden-Getty.jpg?ve=1&amp;tl=1" alt="MILWAUKEE, WI - AUGUST 20: In this screenshot from the DNCC&#8217;s livestream of the 2020 Democratic National Convention, Hunter Biden, son of Democratic presidential nominee Joe Biden, addresses the virtual convention on August 20, 2020. "Our policy only covers links to or images of hacked material themselves."</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Twitter's Dorsey also&#160;<a href="https://twitter.com/jack/status/1316528193621327876" target="_blank">shared&#160;</a>the update and said the company had not done a good enough job of communicating why it took the actions it did.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>The GOP lawmakers say while they don&#8217;t know if the New York Post reporting is true, they believe in a free press.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Hawley already invited Dorsey and Zuckerberg to testify before his subcommittee and said a subpoena for Dorsey to appear before the full committee next week is the right first step, while urging the committee to consider one for Zuckerberg, too.&#160;</p><p>"We believe in a free press in this country and we also believe in free elections," Hawley, R-Mo., said. "The attempt to rig&#160;an election, which is what we're seeing here by monopolies,&#160;is unprecedented in American history.
