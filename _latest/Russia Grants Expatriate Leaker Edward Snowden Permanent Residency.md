<div><p>Edward Snowden, the expatriate intelligence leaker whom President <a href="//www.usnews.com/elections/donald-trump">Donald Trump</a> considered granting a pardon earlier this summer, has received an unlimited residency permit in Russia, his lawyer told Russian state news on Thursday. </p><p id="ac-lre-player"></p><p>The residency permit for the former National Security Agency contractor was set to expire in April but was extended until June due to the spread of the <a href="//www.usnews.com/topics/subjects/coronavirus">coronavirus</a>. There are many people who think that he should be somehow treated differently and other people think he did very bad things." </p><p>The Republican and Democratic leaders of the House Armed Services Committee said at the time that pardoning Snowden would amount to "<a href="https://www.usnews.com/news/national-news/articles/2020-08-17/potential-trump-pardon-for-edward-snowden-a-serious-mistake-lawmakers-say">a serious mistake</a>" that "would completely undermine this administration's position and mock our national security workforce who take immense caution in their work to keep us safe." </p><p>Snowden exposed programs that have subsequently been reformed, come under new oversight or have been eliminated. He fled U.S. territory in 2013 for Hong Kong, where he first met with reporters to release the information he had taken. Days later he traveled to Russia, where he was eventually granted asylum and has remained since.</p><p id="ad" class="ad"></p><p>As Russian state news described Thursday, "U.S. administration officials have repeatedly stated that they consider Snowden to be a traitor and have no intention of forgiving him, as he seriously damaged national security interests," according to a translation. <br></p></div>
