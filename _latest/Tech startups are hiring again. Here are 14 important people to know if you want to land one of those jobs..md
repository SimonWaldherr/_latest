<p>Founders know that growing a team isn't about hiring lots of people so much as finding the right people.</p><p>For that reason, people who specialize in recruiting &#8212;&#160; known as the "talent partner" &#8212; have become an essential employee at most venture capital firms, where they're dedicated to finding and closing candidates for portfolio companies, as well as maintaining a Rolodex of experts who offer counsel as problems arise. It's just one of the ways that tech investors "add value" on top of providing capital.</p><p>Our first-ever list of the top people to know if you want to work at a startup highlights 14 talent partners at venture capital firms. We considered factors like experience, scope of work, and the caliber of companies in their network.</p><p>Their jobs have changed in a year rocked by the coronavirus pandemic, as many startups were forced, at first, to lay off staff or pause their hiring plans as a way to cut costs and avoid a sudden death in a recession. Talent partners say they stepped into more of a consulting role, advising founders on how to keep employees engaged in a remote-work environment.</p><p>And the good news is: Startups are hiring again, they say.</p><p>"Today, our talent team is just as busy as we were before the pandemic," says Sequoia Capital partner Jamie Bott.</p><p>Here are 14 talent partners to know right now, listed alphabetically by their firm's name.</p>
