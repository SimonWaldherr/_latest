<p>House Speaker Nancy Pelosi said Tuesday that she was "optimistic" of agreeing a stimulus package with the White House by the end of the week, after her and Treasury Secretary Steven Mnuchin established more "common ground" during a phone conversation.</p><p>Pelosi is pushing for a $2.2 trillion stimulus package &#8212; the White House is offering $1.88 trillion, but Senate Republicans warned Tuesday they would likely block any deal worth $1.8 trillion or higher.</p><p>After her latest call with Mnuchin on Tuesday afternoon, Pelosi told <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-10-20/pelosi-says-stimulus-bill-being-written-still-issues-unresolved?sref=lAVQtUwz">Bloomberg</a>: "I'm optimistic because I do think we have a shared value ... we want to crush the virus."</p><p>When asked about the chances of agreeing a compromise this week, Pelosi said: "That's the plan. That's what I would hope."</p>
<p id="gpt-post-politics-mobile_in_post_ad-fluid-2" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-mobile"></p>

<p id="gpt-post-politics-desktop_in_post_ad-fluid-2" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-desktop"></p>
<p>Pelosi had previously set a Tuesday deadline for an agreement, <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/nancy-pelosi-deadline-stimulus-white-house-change-deal-negotiations-mnuchin-2020-10">but walked that back</a>, and said that a deal agreed this week could pass into law before the November 3 election.</p><p>In a letter to House Democrats on Tuesday, Pelosi said her conversation with Mnuchin "provided more clarity and common ground as we move closer to an agreement."</p><p>The Tuesday deadline had "enabled us to see that decisions could be reached," she said, adding:&#160;"both sides are serious about finding a compromise."</p><p>White House chief of staff Mark Meadows told <a href="https://www.cnbc.com/video/2020/10/20/mark-meadows-on-stimulus-bill-theres-still-a-number-of-open-items-on-stimulus-talks-will-continue-tomorrow.html">CNBC</a> that he hoped to see "some kind of agreement before the weekend."</p>
<p id="gpt-post-politics-mobile_in_post_ad-fluid-3" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-mobile"></p>

<p id="gpt-post-politics-desktop_in_post_ad-fluid-3" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-desktop"></p>
<p>Pelosi and Mnuchin had made "good progress" during talks, Meadows said, but added there was still "a ways to go" before a deal was agreed. "We're not just down to a difference of language and a few dollars," he said.</p><p>Pelosi and Mnuchin will speak again on Wednesday.</p><p>Even if the two sides agree a deal, Republicans in the Senate could stop it becoming law.</p><p>At a closed-door lunch with Senate Republicans on Tuesday, Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell <a href="https://www.businessinsider.com/mcconnell-white-house-stimulus-deal-republicans-democrats-before-election-2020-10">warned the White House against striking an agreement</a> with Democrats before Election Day, the <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/us-policy/2020/10/20/trump-economic-stimulus-pelosi/">Washington Post</a> first reported.</p>
<p id="gpt-post-politics-mobile_in_post_ad-fluid-4" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-mobile"></p>

<p id="gpt-post-politics-desktop_in_post_ad-fluid-4" class="ad ad-wrapper fluid in-post only-desktop"></p>
<p>Later in a press conference Tuesday, McConnell said that if the House agreed to a comprehensive coronavirus stimulus package, "at some point we'll bring it to the floor," but he didn't specify how long the process would take or whether he would support such a deal.</p><p>Pelosi is pushing for a $2.2 trillion stimulus package. The Trump administration's offer has increased to $1.88 trillion, Meadows said, but President Donald Trump said Tuesday he was willing to go higher than even the Democrats were proposing.</p><p>Senator Mitt Romney told reporters Tuesday that a stimulus package costing $1.8 trillion or higher would most likely not make it through the Senate. "I don't support something of that level," he said, <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-10-20/pelosi-says-stimulus-bill-being-written-still-issues-unresolved?sref=lAVQtUwz">per Bloomberg.</a></p><p>Senator Richard Shelby also said Tuesday that the White House's proposal was too costly. "I'm not optimistic about us doing anything," he said.</p>
