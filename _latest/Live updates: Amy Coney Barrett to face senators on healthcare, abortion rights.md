Supreme Court nominee Amy Coney Barrett will face senators’ questions over her approach to healthcare and legal precedent and over her past public comments criticizing the landmark abortion ruling Roe vs. Wade during a second day of confirmation hearings today.

The mood is likely to shift to a more confrontational tone after the first day’s opening statements as Barrett is grilled in 30-minute segments by Democrats gravely opposed to President Trump’s nominee, yet virtually powerless to stop her rise.
