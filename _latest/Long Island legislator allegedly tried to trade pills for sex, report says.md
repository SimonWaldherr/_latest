<div><p>A Long Island pol, who is also a renowned physician, was arrested Tuesday in a sting operation after allegedly trying to swap pills for sexual favors, according to a report.</p>
<p>Dr. William Spencer, a legislator for Suffolk County&#8217;s 18th District, was nabbed in a parking lot behind a Goodwill store where he believed he was meeting a woman, <a href="https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/local/crime-and-courts/long-island-lawmaker-doctor-arrested-after-trying-to-trade-drugs-for-sexual-favors-sources/2679208/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">sources told NBC New York</a>.</p>
<p>But he was set up by a task force led by the Suffolk County district attorney&#8217;s office, along with Suffolk and Nassau County police, the sheriff&#8217;s office and agents from the Drug Enforcement Administration, the outlet reported.</p>
<p>In addition to <a href="https://nypost.com/2020/05/06/long-island-town-told-to-diet-to-combat-coronavirus/">being a legislator and a well-regarded doctor</a>, Spencer is also an ordained minister. He now faces drug-related charges, the report said.</p>
<p>Spencer couldn&#8217;t be reached for comment by The Post on Tuesday night. The DA&#8217;s office didn&#8217;t return calls or emails on Tuesday night.</p>
