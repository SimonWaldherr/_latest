<div><p>Prime Minister Imran Khan congratulated and distributed awards to Deputy Commissioners of Attock and Bannu as these cities become the Clean Green Campion cities in the first phase of Clean and Green Index.</p>

<p>There are 35 sub-indicators for ranking the cities are used against 5 core pillars access to water, sanitation, hygiene, solid waste management and plantation.</p>

<p>Under the first Clean Green Cities Index Awards of Cities and Champions, Attock grabbed the first position in Punjab for a lead in plantation, Bahawalpur stood second in solid waste, Lahore bagged the third position in water and hygiene, Gujrat came in fourth while Rawalpindi achieved the fifth position.</p>

<p>In Khyber-Pakhtunkhwa (KP), Bannu stood first (overall) while Kohat and Abbottabad stood second and third, respectively.</p>

<p>The innovation award for cities was also given to Peshawar for its performance in access to safe drinking water, Sahiwal for community participation and Gujranwala for beautification and parks.</p>

<p>The award of the best performing clean green champions went to Dr Akram Shahzad (Khanewal), Ashfaq Nazar (Sialkot), Bilquis Rehana (Faisalabad), Haseena Hussain, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (Peshawar) and Aqil Zaman (Swabi).</p>

<p>It is pertinent to note that the Prime Minister launched the Clean Green Pakistan Movement on November 25, 2019 with an aim to introduce cleanliness competition among cities.</p>

<p>As per Prime Minister&#8217;s Office, &#8220;The movement has been introduced to facilitate behavioral change, build positive attitudes and ensure institutional strengthening for better water, sanitation and hygiene services and facilities.&#8221;</p>
