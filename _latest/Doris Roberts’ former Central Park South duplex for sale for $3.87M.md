<div><p>This duplex on Central Park South comes with outdoor space and park views, as well as a celebrity past.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s the former home of Doris Roberts, who played Ray Romano&#8217;s mother Marie on &#8220;Everybody Loves Raymond.&#8221; (She died in 2016, at age 90.)</p>
<p>The co-op, at 200 Central Park South, <a href="https://streeteasy.com/building/200-central-park-south-new_york/1112i-12j" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">is asking $3.87 million</a> &#8212; down half a million dollars from its $4.34 million asking price earlier this month.</p>
<p>The spread consists of two separate units. One is a two-bedroom, two-bathroom on the 11th and 12th floors, where Roberts lived.</p>
<p>The second, around 1,000 square feet, is a disconnected unit on the 12th floor that could be combined to create around 2,800 square feet of living space.</p>
<p>The units can also be bought separately: Roberts&#8217; former duplex is now asking $2.75 million, down from $2.95 million.</p>
<p>If combined, the units can feature three bedrooms, 3&#189; bathrooms and views from the park to Carnegie Hall and Alwyn Court.</p>
<p>Built in 1963, the squat, 35-story corner building is near 220 Central Park South &#8212; otherwise known as the &#8220;billionaires&#8217; bunker&#8221; and Billionaires Row. Building amenities include valet and maid service, a concierge, a bike room and parking.</p>
<p>The listing brokers are Douglas Elliman&#8217;s Joe Wagner and Kyle Lee.</p>
