<div><p>Rep. <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/news/topic/alexandria-ocasio-cortez" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez</a> (D-N.Y.) was the talk of the Internet on Tuesday night as she live-streamed popular online game Among Us on Twitch, playing alongside Twitch and YouTube personalities like Pokimane, Jack Septic Eye and Hasanabi.</p><p>She even got fellow Democratic congresswoman and member of &#8220;<a href="https://www.huffpost.com/entry/the-squad-members-win-democratic-primaries-reelection_n_5f33771fc5b64cc99fdfca65" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">The Squad</a>&#8221; Ilhan Omar of Minnesota in on the fun.</p><p>Ocasio-Cortez, who took to Twitch as a way to encourage young Americans to vote in the upcoming election, had <a href="https://twitter.com/AOC/status/1318276809977434112" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">asked her Twitter followers</a> on Monday if anyone was keen to play Among Us with her &#8220;to get out the vote.&#8221;</p><p>She said she&#8217;d never played the <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/14/style/among-us.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">multiplayer game</a> before but that it looked like &#8220;a lot of fun.&#8221;</p><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" align="center"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">Anyone want to play Among Us with me on Twitch to get out the vote? (I&#8217;ve never played but it looks like a lot of fun)</p>&#8212; Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (@AOC) <a href="https://twitter.com/AOC/status/1318276809977434112?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">October 19, 2020</a></blockquote>
