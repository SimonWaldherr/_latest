<div><p class="css-38z03z">A court document containing potentially sensitive information about <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ghislaine-maxwell">Ghislaine Maxwell</a> and her relationship with the late sex offender <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/jeffrey-epstein">Jeffrey Epstein</a> was unsealed on Thursday morning in New York just moments before a court-imposed deadline.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Roughly a dozen Maxwell files have been unsealed, with the first one on the docket covering Epstein accuser Virginia Giuffre&#8217;s counsel alleging Maxwell dodged a question &#8220;about allegedly &#8216;adult&#8217; sexual activity related to Jeffrey Epstein&#8221;.</p><p id="sign-in-gate"></p><p class="css-38z03z">The unsealing of Maxwell&#8217;s deposition, which she had given during past civil litigation,<strong> </strong>came after an appeals panel ruled it could be released, and a lower court effectively<strong> </strong>decided that it should be released as soon as possible.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Maxwell&#8217;s lawyers had urged the US second circuit court of appeals panel of judges to overturn Manhattan federal court judge Loretta Preska&#8217;s July <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2020/jul/23/ghislaine-maxwell-case-extremely-personal-documents-to-be-unsealed">ruling</a> to release the 418 pages of sworn testimony.</p><p class="css-38z03z">But the appeals judges decided on Monday that Preska rightly determined<strong> </strong>that the public had a right to access documents from legal proceedings and that transcripts should be released because arguments by Maxwell&#8217;s attorneys lacked merit.</p><p class="css-38z03z">In the civil case where this deposition originated, Epstein accuser Virginia Giuffre maintained that Maxwell drew her into Epstein&#8217;s circle as a teen, under the false pretences of providing work as a masseuse. Giuffre alleged that Maxwell and Epstein of pressured her to engage in sex with rich and powerful men, such as Britain&#8217;s Prince Andrew.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Giuffre&#8217;s 2015 civil action said that Maxwell defamed her in claiming she was a liar for alleging the pair participated in sexual impropriety. Prince Andrew, the Duke of York, has adamantly denied Giuffre&#8217;s claims.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Maxwell, who was <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2020/jul/02/ghislaine-maxwell-arrest-jeffrey-epstein-charges-latest-fbi">arrested</a> in July for alleged sexual crimes, conspiracy and perjury involving Epstein, argued in court papers that unsealing the deposition from this old civil case &#8220;will lead to a violation of [her] due process right to a fair trial by an impartial jury&#8221; in her criminal proceedings.</p><p class="css-38z03z">Maxwell has <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2020/jul/13/ghislaine-maxwell-court-sex-trafficking-trial-epstein">pleaded</a> not guilty in her criminal case. The Manhattan US attorney&#8217;s office used her deposition &#8211; which Maxwell believed was confidential &#8211; in its perjury accusation against her in the criminal case, claiming she lied under oath.</p><p class="css-38z03z">More details soon &#8230;</p></div>
