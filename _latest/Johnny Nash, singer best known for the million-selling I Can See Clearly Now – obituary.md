Johnny Nash, who has died aged 80, was a musician best known for his 1972 hit I Can See Clearly Now, which he wrote, sang and produced and which went to No 1 in the US and No 5 in the UK. With its irresistibly sunny optimism it played a leading role in spreading the reggae gospel around the world – and Nash was also instrumental in making Bob Marley a global superstar.

John Lester Nash was born on August 19 1940 in Houston, Texas, and began singing in earnest in the choir of his Baptist church. By the time he was 13 he was appearing on a local television show, Matinee, singing R & B covers.
