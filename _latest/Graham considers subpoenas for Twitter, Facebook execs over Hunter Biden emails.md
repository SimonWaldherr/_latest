<div><p class="speakable">Senate Judiciary Committee Chairman <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/lindsey-graham" target="_blank">Lindsey Graham, R-S.C.,</a> is considering subpoenaing social media executives over their platforms' role in suppressing news&#160;about the&#160;alleged emails involving&#160;<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/joe-biden" target="_blank">former Vice President Joe Biden's</a> son Hunter.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="ad gam"></p><p class="speakable">The committee staff is currently working with Facebook and Twitter to have&#160;<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/mark-zuckerberg" target="_blank">Mark Zuckerberg</a> and <a href="https://www.foxnews.com/category/person/jack-dorsey" target="_blank">Jack Dorsey</a>, the companies'&#160;respective CEOs, appear before the committee on Oct. 22.</p><p>"If an agreement for voluntary testimony is not reached, the committee will vote on authorizing the subpoenas at a date to be determined," a committee press release reads.</p><p>The release included a draft motion for the Oct. 22 hearing. In addition to the CEOs testimonies, it would call for "any other content-moderation policies, practices, or actions that may interfere with or influence elections for federal office" as well as "any other recent determinations to temporarily reduce distribution of material pending fact-checker review and/or block and mark material as potentially unsafe."</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>A spokesperson for Sen. Ted Cruz, R-Texas, who also sits on the committee, told Fox News the senator expected both companies to testify. &#8220;Sen. Cruz has been working with Chairman Graham and expects both Twitter and Facebook to testify before the Judiciary Committee before Election Day," the spokesperson said.</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>Graham's latest move comes amid widespread accusations that social media platforms favor Democrats in the way they regulate&#160;the distribution of certain content. Twitter argued&#160;the article violated Twitter rules, but&#160;has since backed off and&#160;<a href="https://www.foxnews.com/politics/twitter-announces-new-policies-after-hunter-biden-report-imbroglio" target="_blank">announced new policies</a>.&#160;</p><p class="ad gam"></p><p>"Straight blocking of URLs was wrong, and we updated our policy and enforcement to fix," Dorsey tweeted Friday.
