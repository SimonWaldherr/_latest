The Best Countries rankings , formed in partnership with BAV Group, a unit of global marketing communications company VMLY&R, and the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, are based on a survey that asked more than 20,000 people from four regions to associate 73 countries with specific characteristics. The Movers subranking is based on an equally weighted average of scores from four country attributes: different, distinctive, dynamic and unique. The Movers subranking score had a 14% weight in the overall Best Countries ranking .

The United Arab Emirates , named the most competitive economy in the Arab world by the World Economic Forum, places first in this subranking. One of the most liberal of the Gulf nations, survey participants consider the UAE the most distinctive country.

Manufacturing giants India and China claim the second and third spots, respectively, while Egypt and Japan rounded out the top five countries. Of the remaining BRICS countries, Brazil finished at No. 9, Russia ranked No. 12 and South Africa dropped from No. 10 to No. 17.

Economic potential is perceived to be heavily concentrated in Asia , with countries from the continent claiming nearly half of the top 20 spots. Singapore , ranked seventh this year, has a solid infrastructure that helps support sustained growth. No. 6 Thailand boasts a healthy, efficient economy with low unemployment and poverty rates.

Perceptions of European nations are similarly divided. Despite continued economic struggles since the debt crisis, Greece and Italy rank among the highest of European countries. Only No. 19 Switzerland ranks higher. A majority of Central and Eastern European nations, including Estonia, Slovenia, Lithuania, Poland and Latvia, the Czech Republic , Slovakia and Romania , round out the bottom of the list.
